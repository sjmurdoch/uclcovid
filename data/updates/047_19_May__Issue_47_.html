1. Funding call: UCL Covid-19 Rapid Response

Applications are now invited for projects focused on rapid responses and novel solutions to understand and tackle the challenges faced during and after the coronavirus pandemic. This call is open to all disciplines across UCL.


The aim is to mobilise the expertise and infrastructure of UCL and partner hospitals to mitigate the impacts of the pandemic on health, the economy, environment, society and culture. The call is intended to seed novel research projects, bridge funding gaps, pump-prime further external research grant applications or boost ongoing strategic projects and initiatives where value can be added.


This scheme will be supported by the UCL Coronavirus Response Fund, with co-funding from the UCL Wellcome Institutional Strategic Support Fund along with UCLH and Moorfields NIHR Biomedical Research Centres (with the possibility of additional funding from other sources).


Projects may be 6–12 months in duration and are expected to cost between £20k and £50k. This is a rolling call with the first deadline at 12noon on 2 June 2020. Find out more, including how to apply, on the SLMS website.

                

2. Mental Health Awareness Week: UCL's new Mental Health Strategy

Mental health is one of the biggest challenges of the 21st century, with 615 million people worldwide suffering from depression and anxiety. Although there has been progress in recent years to break the taboos surrounding mental health, there is still so much more to learn about the human brain and how we can better understand and support people’s wellbeing. The additional challenges we are facing due to the coronavirus pandemic mean this research is now more urgent than ever.


UCL’s Mental Health Strategy outlines our vision for interdisciplinary mental health research, supported by the university’s flagship Institute of Mental Health. Part of the Faculty of Brain Sciences, the Institute was launched in 2019 to centralise research and expertise in mental health from across UCL’s different faculties. It aims to address the full spectrum of lifelong mental health issues from developmental disorders in childhood, to dementia in the elderly.


The Mental Health Strategy is available to download now from the Institute of Mental Health website. You can also find out more about how UCL is transforming mental health and social wellbeing for all during the pandemic in this article by Professor Alan Thompson, Dean of the Faculty of Brain Sciences and Pro Vice Provost (London).

4. Teams Tips Tuesday

Today is the first of our Teams Tips Tuesdays, with hints and ideas brought to you from ISD. This week, we’re looking at spring cleaning your Teams.


There are many ways of keeping Teams tidy and not letting the information and activity bar get out of control.


Top Tip: If you have a team which you wish to remain a member of but are currently not actively using, you can hide it by clicking on the ellipsis (…) menu item on the right of the team title and selecting ‘hide’. You will still receive notifications from that team and can access it at any time by clicking on the ‘Hidden teams’ section on your team list.


New this month: One of the development releases delivered this month increases the number of attendees viewable on screen from four to nine, so you can see more of your colleagues (and their inspired background choices!) at the same time.


If you have any tips for using Teams which have really helped you make the most of the product, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #teamstipstuesday. For further information about using Teams effectively, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site.

6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
