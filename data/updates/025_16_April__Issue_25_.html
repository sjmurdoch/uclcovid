1. How UCL staff and students are tackling coronavirus

UCL researchers are using the world’s most powerful supercomputers to study coronavirus and speed up the development of treatments and vaccines.


Professor Peter Coveney, who leads the EU H2020 Computational Biomedicine Centre of Excellence, and his colleagues at the UCL Centre for Computational Science, are part of an international consortium of researchers using supercomputers to scan libraries of drugs that have been approved to treat existing diseases, and are therefore already considered safe for humans. They are also able to refine the list of drugs available down to those that are most likely to bind to the coronavirus and prevent it from infecting human cells.


This is a much quicker way of finding suitable new treatments for the virus. It can take pharmaceutical companies many years and billions of pounds to take just one drug from discovery to market, but these researchers are hoping to do the same in a fraction of that time and cost.


More information about this research is available on the UCL News website.

                                               

2. Remote, not Distant at UCL: Managing stress

A UCL study of over 70,000 people has found that the current highest stress factor appears to be worries about either catching coronavirus or becoming seriously ill from it. This is highest amongst adults aged 30-59.


If you’re feeling anxious about coronavirus and the impact of the pandemic on your life and work, we hope you’ll take a look at Remote, not Distant at UCL. There you can find support and resources for taking care of your mental and emotional health, including:


  • 10 Minute Mind, an online meditation programme
  • Confidential counselling services from Care First
  • SilverCloud, an online Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) programme


All of these resources are free for UCL staff.


The UCL Covid-19 Social Study is led by Dr Daisy Fancourt (Associate Professor of Epidemiology) and Professor Andrew Steptoe (Professor of Psychology and Epidemiology) from the UCL Institute of Epidemiology and Health Care. If you would like to take part, please visit the study website.

                                               

3. Digital research and learning resources

While our libraries are physically closed, colleagues in UCL Library Services are working hard to make more digital content available for research and learning. To date, 104 additional collections and publishers have been added to the Library Services website and the list continues to grow.


Please use UCL Explore to access electronic books, articles and databases, as some electronic resources are only available through this platform. If you need to login with your UCL details, you will be able to do this via UCL Explore.


For more information about using digital resources, please visit the E-resources help page.


4. Copyright restrictions relaxed

Some copyright restrictions have been temporarily relaxed to allow more educational material to be digitised and made available online, to support taught courses during the pandemic.


If you are currently preparing reading list materials and extended reading lists for students, please contact the Teaching and Learning Support Team in the Library for more information at readinglists@ucl.ac.uk.

                                        

5. Online advice and engagement

  • Answers to frequently asked questions about international travel have been updated on the new coronavirus information hub.
  • Yesterday there were 1,371 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 689 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 308 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or visit our online support hub. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
