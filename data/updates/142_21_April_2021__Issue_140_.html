1. Returning to work on campus

In line with the Government’s roadmap to ease lockdown restrictions, some staff and sub-contractors who had been working remotely came back to work on campus last week. We are now developing further plans for a cautious return to onsite activity, according to the dates set out by the Government:


  • No earlier than Monday 17 May: In line with step 3 of the roadmap, students can return to campus for in-person teaching;
  • No earlier than Monday 21 June: The final step of the Government roadmap, when we hope all staff will be able to start returning to work on campus. We will share more information and guidance about this step in due course.


For the latest information, please visit UCL’s working on campus webpage.


Managers of teams whose services or buildings are reopening have been asked to consider whether their staff are required on site for some or all of the time and if so, to plan for their return carefully. This includes reviewing and updating local risk assessments and inviting staff to team or individual inductions. Resources and guidance for managers can be found on the Resources for Managers page.


2. Term 3 guidance for students

Last week, the Government announced that all remaining higher education students can return to in-person teaching no earlier than 17 May, in line with step 3 of the national roadmap to ease lockdown restrictions.


Students should remain where they are to continue with their studies and not return to term-time accommodation before their face-to-face teaching resumes, unless they have mental health or wellbeing challenges impacting on their ability to study successfully. Individual departments will let students know when in-person teaching restarts for their programme.


We will be providing more updated guidance for students and returning to campus in due course. For the latest information, please visit our Term 3 Guidance webpage.


3. Surge testing

Surge testing is the term used to describe an increase in symptom-free testing and contact tracing activity, as a result of the identification of a COVID-19 variant or mutation of concern. It does not necessarily mean a surge in overall COVID-19 cases.


The implementation of surge testing is being led at the Local Authority level and is linked to the post code where you are currently living. It is therefore important to follow your Local Authority guidelines about if, when and how to get tested. If you’ve recently spent time within one of the areas targeted for surge testing but do not live there, you should continue to follow the national restrictions and check with your local authority whether you should get a test. You can view the latest Government information on areas that are undergoing surge testing here.


4. Annual leave

We are now just over halfway through the academic year and the annual leave year. We want all staff to achieve a good work-life balance and take your annual leave and time away from work, for your mental and physical health.


Staff will be able to carry forward 5 days of annual leave into the next annual leave year and this also applies to those on furlough leave.

Please complete Week 41 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


In March, April and May, there will be three prize draws with a prize of £50 in John Lewis vouchers, with winners picked from staff who have completed the Your Wellbeing survey every week – find out more about the prize draw on the UCL HR website.


6. Coronavirus: The Whole Story – What does the future of work look like?

Coming out of lockdown doesn't mean returning to life as we knew it, especially in the world of work. This week, we’re talking to two guests whose research focuses on work and wellbeing in the digital age: neuroscientist Professor Anna Cox (UCL Interaction Centre), who uses social science methods to study work-based technology use and reduce work-based stress, and PhD candidate Dave Cook (UCL Anthropology), whose research explores the lives of digital nomads working in Southeast Asia. They discuss the future of work, and how COVID-19 has the potential to change things for the better. Listen now on UCL Minds.
