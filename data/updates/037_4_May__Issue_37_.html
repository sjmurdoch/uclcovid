3. How UCL staff and students are tackling the pandemic

New research from the Bartlett School of Architecture’s Urban Dynamics Lab has revealed that nearly two thirds of London’s pavements are not wide enough for people to observe the Government’s social distancing guidelines. Only 36 per cent of streets in Greater London have pavements at least three metres wide – judged to be the minimum required for people to maintain their distance.


The research raises questions about how easy to use and inclusive London’s streets are. It also highlights a need for investment and reallocation, possibly taking inspiration from other cities around the world where authorities have adapted to lower traffic levels by creating more spaces for walking and cycling, closing streets to cars and creating more cycle lanes.


You can find out more and see information for your London area on the UCL News website.


4. UCL's Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – new episode available now

Coronavirus: The Whole Story is UCL’s weekly podcast, exploring the pandemic from the perspective of researchers across UCL.


Presented by writer, broadcaster and UCL graduate Vivienne Parry, each episode looks at the coronavirus outbreak through a different lens, covering history, psychology, social sciences, arts, engineering, economics, law and politics.


Episode 3 is out now. This week we are discussing how, and when, the lockdown in the UK might end. Our special guests are Professor Paul Ormerod, Visiting Professor at the UCL Centre for Decision Making and Professor David Alexander, Professor of Risk and Disaster Reduction at the UCL Institute for Risk and Disaster Reduction.


You can listen on the UCL Minds website or wherever you find your podcasts. If you’d like to be part of a future episode – or if you've got a question you'd like a UCL researcher to answer – we’d love to hear from you. Please get in touch by emailing minds@ucl.ac.uk.


5. UCL insurance and working from home

Many of you have asked whether UCL’s insurance policy covers employees who are working from home. Our policy does indeed cover remote workers, and includes personal accident cover (providing the injury happened when carrying out occupational duties, including research activity) and professional indemnity cover for staff providing online teaching and advice. Personal accident cover is currently provided for UCL members of staff only, and does not extend to include their household or family members.


Working from home is not something that home insurers would usually ask you to declare and UCL has cover on its policy for items temporarily moved or taken off-site up to the value of £100,000. However, if you are in any doubt or you have any questions, you should discuss them with your home insurance policy provider.


If work equipment is lost or damaged whilst working from home and the item is valued above the level of the excess on the UCL insurance policy (£2,000), please contact Serena Ezra or June Campbell in the insurance team. Any claims submitted for damaged, lost or stolen equipment valued at less than this amount may need to be covered internally by your own department.


For more information please contact the insurance team.

7. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
