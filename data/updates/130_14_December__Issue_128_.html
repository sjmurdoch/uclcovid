1. Message from Prof. David Price, Prof. Anthony Smith and Fiona Ryland


We would like to take a moment to thank each of you for your contribution in 2020, a truly exceptional year.

    

In recognition of your extraordinary work this year, there will be three additional closure days over the Christmas break, so UCL will be closed for two weeks from 5:30pm on Friday 18 December 2020 until 9am on Monday 4 January 2021. We will be using this time to get some rest and we hope you will feel able to do the same. You deserve it for yourself and for those close to you to switch off completely after the year you have had and all the hard work you have put in.

    

Not everyone will be able to take this time off, and those that can’t will be able to carry these days over into the New Year instead. If you are working over the closure period, we would like to say a huge thank you for supporting our students and staff who need to remain on campus.

    

Our current ways of working will remain in place when we return in January. If you have been working remotely, please continue to do so wherever possible, in line with Government guidance. If and when you do need to come to UCL, bookable work spaces will be available at 1 St Martin’s Le Grand.

    

We will continue to use several of our office spaces, including Bidborough House, Torrington Place and 1 St Martin’s Le Grand, for additional student study space and the Government’s national testing programme for universities. These study spaces are currently operating at above 80% capacity, and the asymptomatic testing site is playing an important role in supporting our students to travel to and from London more safely. Thank you to everyone who has given up their usual office space, which has allowed UCL to use these spaces to support our students and continue to provide them with best possible experience, despite the challenges of the pandemic.

    

This approach will remain in place until the end of Term Two, when we will review the situation again and confirm our plans for the future.

    

With best wishes,


Professor David Price, Vice-Provost (Research)

Professor Anthony Smith, Vice-Provost (Education & Student Affairs)

Fiona Ryland, Chief Operating Officer

                                                                                                           

2. Testing and phased return to face-to-face teaching and in Term Two

      

Departments are writing to students today (Monday 14 December) with information about arrangements for the start of Term Two, in response to recent Government guidance about the phased return of students to universities across the country.


When planning the phased return of students, departments have considered practical / laboratory / studio subject requirements, early assessments, and other subject-related factors. Students will continue to have the option of studying wherever they are based, either remotely or on campus, according to travel restrictions and their personal circumstances.


We are also encouraging students planning to travel to London in January to get tested for coronavirus at UCL’s asymptomatic testing centre at Bidborough House, as part of the Government’s national testing programme for universities.


Please read the summary of advice for students about Term Two on UCL Student News.

4. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast - Is the vaccine the beginning of the end?


Last week, the first handful of people in the world received a COVID-19 vaccine outside of a trial. In this week's episode, we discuss the extraordinary achievement that this represents - highlighting the value and importance of collaboration across borders and across disciplines. We've talked about vaccines before on Episode 16 but now we're returning to the topic with Professor Martina Micheletti, Professor of Bioprocess Fluid Dynamics in the UCL Department of Biochemical Engineering, and co-director of the future vaccine manufacturing hub Vax Hub, which works to secure the supply of essential vaccines.
