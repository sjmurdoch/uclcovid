1. UCL's life-saving breathing aid

The UCL-Ventura breathing aid, a low-flow Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP) device, is now being deployed to treat coronavirus patients in 46 NHS hospitals across the UK, including London, Belfast, Glasgow, Hull, Newcastle, Liverpool, Manchester and Norwich.


All the details required to make the device are available for manufacturers and humanitarian agencies to download at no cost from covid19research.uclb.com/product/ucl-cpap, a research licensing website developed by UCL Business (UCLB) to disseminate technologies designed to help in the fight against the pandemic. These designs have been downloaded by more than 1,800 teams from 105 countries around the world and 20 teams have manufactured prototypes for testing in Brazil, Bulgaria, Canada, Colombia, Germany, India, Iran, Mexico, Russia, South Africa and the USA.


If you’re a UCL researcher with a ready-to-release technology that could be useful in the fight against coronavirus, and you’d like to make it rapidly available to others at no cost, we encourage you to use UCLB’s research licensing platform – find out more on the UCLB website.

3. How is the lockdown affecting our mental health?

How are the coronavirus restrictions affecting our mental health and what can we do to look after ourselves? Watch this explanation from Dr Daisy Fancourt, Associate Professor of Psychobiology and Epidemiology in the UCL Institute of Epidemiology & Health, in the latest in our coronavirus video series.


Dr Fancourt leads on the UCL Covid-19 Social Study, the UK’s largest survey of adults’ wellbeing and mental health during the pandemic. If you’d like to take part in the Covid-19 Social Study you can still sign up on the study’s website.


Making sure that we look after our mental health and wellbeing is more important than ever, so please do make sure that you’re aware of the support that UCL can offer you, and have a look at the self-care and wellbeing resources listed on the Remote, not Distant at UCL website.


4. Recovery from Covid-19: An update from UCL Grand Challenges

Last month, UCL Grand Challenges launched a special initiative calling for expressions of interest in research projects focused on societal recovery from the Covid-19 pandemic.


In just a few weeks, we received over 250 Expressions of Interest (EOIs), involving more than 300 researchers from every faculty across UCL. They covered a vast range of topics: from youth attitudes towards the virus and its impact on urban slums, to lessons from religious communities and astronauts. Many proposals are focused on creating an equitable economic recovery from the pandemic, with a significant number on the topic of inequalities more broadly.


The Grand Challenges team are now working through the EOIs. We’d like to take this opportunity to thank all of those who submitted proposals – we will be in touch soon. You can read the full update on the Grand Challenges website.

6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
