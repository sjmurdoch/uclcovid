1. Life-saving breathing aids developed at UCL

UCL engineers have worked with Mercedes Formula One and clinicians at UCLH to produce a breathing aid that can help coronavirus patients stay out of intensive care. The device was developed and approved for use in the NHS in just ten days – a process that normally takes months or even years.

          

The breathing aid, known as Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP), has been used extensively in hospitals in Italy and China to help coronavirus patients with serious lung infections to breathe more easily, when oxygen alone is insufficient.

          

One hundred devices are due to be delivered to UCLH for clinical trials, to be followed by a rapid roll-out to hospitals around the country. The news today that the CPAP has received regulatory approval has been covered worldwide. There is more information on the UCL website or you can listen to the headline story on BBC Radio 4’s Today Programme (from 1 minute 20 seconds).

3. Email security while working remotely

With everyone now living their lives remotely and via the internet, sadly there is evidence that fraudsters are trying to hack into our systems. The increased use of email to send documents and sensitive information means it’s more important than ever that we think carefully about what we’re sharing.


Please follow these tips from UCL’s Information Security Group to make sure you’re communicating safely and securely:

          

  • Where possible don’t use email. If you need to send a quick message, can you use Teams instead? If you regularly share files with someone, can you create a Teams group and use that for file sharing?
  • If you have to use email, avoid including anything sensitive in the message itself. Save it in a file and then place that file into a 7Zip folder and set a password.
  • Don’t send passwords via email. If you’re contacting someone outside UCL, please send the password as a text or call them. Within UCL, you can also use LastPass – email isg@ucl.ac.uk if you don’t have an account yet.
  • If you receive something that looks odd, think before you click on a link or open an attachment. You might want to check-in with the supposed sender – but remember not to use email to contact them, in case their account has been compromised.
  • If you think you might have been phished, change your UCL password immediately and email isg@ucl.ac.uk or phone 020 7679 7338 (internal 37338) for help.


For more advice and tools to help you online, please visit ISD’s Stay Secure webpages.

          

4. Student assessments

On Friday 27 March, we contacted all undergraduate and postgraduate taught students to update them on their planned assessments in summer 2020.


First year undergraduates will be required to undertake a capstone assessment: a single short piece of work to reflect on and demonstrate their learning across their programme.


For the rest of our undergraduates and postgraduate taught students, colleagues are working to create alternative assessments that will still test their learning and understanding, but administered in a different format, so they can be completed remotely. We have told students that they will receive more information about their assessments by 6 April 2020.


We have also reassured them that no assessments will be earlier than previously advertised, and they will receive more specific advice from their departments as soon as the details are ready – while acknowledging that this is a major task and will take some time. We will be able to add more information about summer assessments to the Current Students website shortly.

          

5. Graduation ceremonies

All graduation ceremonies this year have been cancelled. This covers ceremonies in June, July and September 2020.


We are contacting all affected students directly today and our Events Team is working hard to arrange an alternative celebration, which is likely to take place next year. More information is available on the Graduation Ceremonies website.


6. Accessing electronic books, journals and databases

UCL Library Services is working hard to give staff and students remote access to even more resources during the coronavirus outbreak.


In addition to the 85,000+ electronic journals, over 700,000 e-books and more than 200 online databases that are usually available, 30 new collections and databases have now been added. These include all current Cambridge University Press textbooks and the National Emergency Library, which provides access to 1.5 million books (UCL’s total collection is around 2 million).


The team is continuing to work with Jisc Collections and directly with publishers to arrange temporary access to more material during the outbreak. New resources will be added to the Library Services website as they become available and we will keep you updated there.


7. Coronavirus expert database

The UK Parliament’s Knowledge Exchange Unit (KEU) is creating a coronavirus expert database, to speed up the exchange of information and research. Signing up does not commit you to contributing in any way; it simply means Parliament has your details to hand and can contact you quickly.


If you have expertise relating to the coronavirus outbreak or its impacts, please get in touch with Audrey Tan, UCL Public Policy Engagement Facilitator, SLMS, who is drawing together a list of contacts from across the university.
