1. UCL Health of the Public: Virtual launch

Join us on Tuesday 8 September to celebrate the launch of UCL Health of the Public, a major new initiative supported by the Provost’s Strategic Development Fund.


We want to bring the UCL community together to tackle public health challenges from every angle, harnessing our wealth of expertise across disciplines from social sciences, humanities and law to engineering and physical sciences and the built environment, as well as biomedical sciences. By working together, we will encourage innovative perspectives in our research and education to ensure a healthy future for all.


Join the event online at 2pm to hear from colleagues from across UCL and find out how you can get involved. We will announce a number of exciting opportunities for collaboration, including the launch of our academic leaders’ network.


You can download the full programme here and find out more and sign up via Eventbrite. Registration is essential in order to receive the joining instructions for the event.


2. Remote, not Distant at UCL: What’s new

We’re encouraging colleagues to consider cycling or walking as an option for all or some of your commute, if you’re returning to work on campus.


You can find plenty of advice and resources for walking and cycling on the Sustainable UCL webpages, including average times from London’s main central stations, tips on choosing and maintaining a bike and cycling with confidence, and ideas for making the walking portion of your journey more interesting by going off the beaten track.


UCL is installing 600 new cycle racks on campus, and the UCL Cycle Scheme allows you to buy a bike tax-free. Our new ‘buddy’ scheme can also pair you up with an experienced cyclist if you’re new to cycling – find out more about the scheme, plus some top tips, from our first Bike Buddy.


You can also change the time that you travel, to avoid peak times on public transport – please discuss this with your line manager to find an option that works for you. However you choose to travel to work, we’d love it if you could share pictures of your journey on the Remote, not Distant at UCL virtual wall.


3. Further advice and support

Daily coronavirus updates

These emails are sent every weekday to all UCL staff and postgraduate research students. They contain information about the pandemic and UCL’s response, including guidance about working remotely, policies and procedures, news about teaching and management arrangements and updates about UCL’s COVID-19 research.


If you would like to submit an item about the pandemic or UCL’s response to be included in the daily coronavirus update, please email internalcommunications@ucl.ac.uk.

                                                                                           

Your Wellbeing weekly survey

The UCL Your Wellbeing check-in survey is a short, weekly survey for staff and postgraduate research students. The data will be used to find out how our community is coping with the huge changes caused by the coronavirus crisis and inform future improvements. Complete week 6 of the survey now.


Coronavirus information hub and research webpages

The information hub at ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus is updated regularly with information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions about coronavirus as well as links to advice from other parts of UCL.


The COVID-19 research webpages showcase the work that UCL is carrying out on coronavirus, and contain information and resources for researchers.


Working remotely

The Remote, not Distant at UCL pages contain advice and resources to help staff adapt to remote working, including self-care and wellbeing, digital communication resources and guidance for managers.     


Keeping safe on campus

The Keeping Safe on Campus website provides live updates about our returning to campus pilots, as well as safety and social distancing guidelines for those on site.                                                                                                                                     

Health and Safety advice

The Working Safely During a Pandemic website from Safety Services provides guidance on how to manage the health and safety of staff at UCL during the pandemic. Topics include using display screen equipment, working with COVID-19 samples and risk assessments for returning to campus.


Your health and wellbeing

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

                                      

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.


Face coverings

You can find information on face coverings, including how to make your own, on the UCL Institute of Making website and the Public Health England website.   

                                                                                            

Questions?

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager in the first instance.


If you have a more general question, please check that it has not already been answered on the websites given above. If it hasn’t, members of UCL staff can submit a question about keeping safe on campus via our online form. Alternatively you can email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699. Lines are open 9am–5pm, Monday–Friday.
