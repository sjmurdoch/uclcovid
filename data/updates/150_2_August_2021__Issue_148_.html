1. COVID-19 testing and reporting: Updated guidance

If you don’t have coronavirus symptoms, you should get tested for COVID-19 twice a week. Anyone in England can order rapid lateral flow tests (LFTs) to be delivered to your home address or local pharmacy, and you’ll get your results in 30 minutes. Tests can also be collected from UCL Workplace Health at 123 Grays Inn Road. The Lateral Flow Testing centre at Bidborough House has closed but a smaller-scale service is now operating at the Student Centre, for symptom-free staff and students who prefer to test on-site or who require assistance.


If you do have symptoms, you and everyone you live with must self-isolate immediately and order a polymerase chain reaction (PCR) test. These tests are checked by a lab and results take 1-3 days.


Every time you take a rapid lateral flow test or a PCR test, you should report your results to the NHS and UCL Connect to Protect, to help UCL monitor and respond to potential cases. Reporting your results (both positive and negative) helps to reduce the spread of the virus and protect people at higher risk of infection.


You should also use Connect to Protect if you have symptoms of coronavirus or if you need to self-isolate.


If a student or member of staff tells you they have symptoms, have tested positive or need to self-isolate, please remind them to follow NHS guidance and report their status to UCL Connect to Protect. The Connect to Protect team will get in touch with the individual and their line manager or supervisor to make sure they have access to any support they need.


The Connect to Protect team treats all reports confidentially. If someone has tested positive or been asked to self-isolate, the team will only inform their line manager, Director and / or Head of Department, and any close contacts who are at risk. If you are told that someone has tested positive for COVID-19 or has been asked to self-isolate, this is private medical information and you must not share it with anyone else unless authorised to do so by the individual or UCL Connect to Protect.


UCL supports the roll-out of the COVID-19 vaccination and we encourage staff to get vaccinated as soon as they are eligible. Find out more about the vaccine and how to book yours on the NHS website.


2. Transport for London: Planned industrial action on 3–6 August and 24–27 August

TfL have advised that planned industrial action will take place on 3, 4, 5, 6 August and 24, 25, 26, 27 August. Staff are advised to keep up to date on the disruptions on the TfL website.  

    

Although we recognise that many staff will be working from home on those dates, those who are unable to do so are advised to visit the Transport for London website for information about alternative routes and other general travel advice in order to plan your journey in advance. Where staff cannot work from home and will be unable to get to work, annual leave or unpaid leave should be booked in line with our Special Leave Policy.


3. The Your Wellbeing survey is changing: One year review

The Your Wellbeing survey has been running for just over a year and has been vital to help the Provost and leadership team understand how people have been coping during the pandemic.


From Monday 2 August, the Your Wellbeing survey will be phased into the weekly People Pulse survey, to help us understand how our staff are coping as we transition into hybrid working. You can find out more about the changes on the HR Website and complete this week's survey here. A one-year review of the Your Wellbeing survey is also available, showing the results of the survey in full.


4. “Please give me space” lanyards available for staff

As coronavirus restrictions are relaxed, many colleagues, including those who have been shielding or are anxious about social distancing requirements being relaxed, may choose to wear a Please give me space lanyard or badge.


Lanyards for staff are now available to collect from Security in the Andrew Huxley building. If colleagues would like a lanyard but are unable to visit campus, we ask that departments support them by arranging collection and posting on their behalf.


If you are intending to collect your lanyard in person, please note that the first Monday of each month is generally a very busy day as new staff are onboarded and so is best avoided if you wish to socially distance from others more easily.

                     

5. Remote working pilot policy and guidance and hybrid working FAQs

A new pilot Remote Working Policy and set of hybrid working FAQs have been produced to support existing guidance and policies on working from home and on campus, during the pandemic, including key dates and the expectations of our staff.


Both documents will be reviewed on an ongoing basis alongside our existing Interim People Management guidance.
