1. UCL's life-saving breathing aid

Last week we reported that the UK Government has ordered up to 10,000 UCL-Ventura (CPAP) breathing aids, developed by engineers from UCL and Mercedes Formula with clinicians at UCLH.


All the details required to make the UCL-Ventura device have now been made available online and at no cost so it can be produced by manufacturers around the world. The device has been designed so it can be made quickly by the thousands, and it is hoped that making this information freely available will help healthcare systems provide respiratory support for coronavirus patients across the globe.


More information about the UCL-Ventura and how to download the design is available on the UCL News website.

     

2. Ensuring no academic disadvantage for students

The coronavirus outbreak will have affected students’ preparation for, and performance in, assessments and so we are taking steps to ensure that as many students as possible progress to the next stage of their studies or graduate at the end of this academic year. Today we have announced that exceptional amendments have been made to Academic Regulations to support student progression and maintain academic standards.


We have written to all students to let them know that UCL has adopted a “no detriment” principle, which recognises students’ academic performance so far and ensures that academic outcomes won’t be negatively impacted by the alternative assessments due to take place this summer.


Undergraduate finalists or postgraduate taught students will be awarded degree classifications that are either in line with the classification they have achieved in their work to date, or an improved classification if they perform better in their final assessments. A similar approach has been adopted for second or third year students progressing to the next year of study in September, while first year assessments have been replaced by a “capstone assessment” where students reflect on their learning across the year.


These changes are among a broad range of measures that UCL has taken to ensure students are treated fairly. More information is available on the UCL website.

     

3. Research IT Services (RITS)

Research IT Services (RITS) continue to support UCL researchers while our buildings are closed, including storing and managing research data and assisting with programming. You can see a full list of what they offer on the RITS website.


The team is also offering researchers one-to-one advice with virtual sessions over Microsoft Teams. Please email rits@ucl.ac.uk to arrange a time to speak to someone.


You may find that working remotely provides you with time to develop your IT skills, such as programming, data science, AI or machine learning, or to explore the digital resources that can support your research. Please visit the RITS Training webpages to find out more about what’s available.


If you would like to be kept up-to-date about Research IT Services at UCL sign-up to the monthly newsletter. A special edition about the response to the coronavirus outbreak and remote research will be published tomorrow, Wednesday 8 April.


4. Student accommodation

Students, particularly our postgraduate taught students, have been worried about being charged for accommodation in the period after the end of Term 3, the so-called Term 4. We will not be charging accommodation fees for students for this Term 4 period who will no longer be staying with us, as we have done for the Term 3 period.


We are contacting all our students who will be affected directly. If you are approached by students who are worried about their living situation, please advise them to contact askUCL or their Hall Warden.

6. Further support available for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.


If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or visit our online support hub. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
