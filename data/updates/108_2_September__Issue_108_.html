1. Campus buildings now open

At the beginning of July, we shared a roadmap for reopening campus including over 100 buildings that needed both safety and statutory checks. With thanks to the Estates Operations team and colleagues across UCL, all buildings on campus are now open, on schedule and in time for the start of the new term. This marks a significant point in our return to onsite activity and will ensure that we are best placed to welcome back our staff and students for the 2020/21 academic year.


We have added two short videos for staff to the UCL website: one describing the measures UCL is putting in place to reduce the risk of infection on campus, and another outlining what you can do to keep everyone safe.


For more information about what UCL is doing to welcome staff and students back onsite, please visit the Keeping Safe on Campus webpages.

                               

2. Zoom support for staff and students

ISD support is now available for staff and students using Zoom. Please visit our UCL Zoom Support site to get started and for any technical help you might need. You can also find information about using Zoom for teaching and answers to frequently asked questions.


3. Information for students about pre-enrolment

We have updated our answers to frequently asked questions about pre-enrolment, including instructions for pre-enrolling online and information about visa applications.

4. Today's Teams Tip

Two of the key differences you will notice during calls and meetings are that meeting or call screen now pops out as a separate window, and control icons appear at the top right of the window. The usual features are still there – raise hand, show participants etc. – but there are also a few new surprises.


Top Tip: in large meetings with over 10 attendees you can change your view to see more people. To do this, click on the ellipsis (…) and select ‘Large gallery’ mode to see a bigger grid of up to 49 video feeds. Alternatively, try ‘Together’ mode to bring everyone into the same shared space and fill seats in a virtual room. You can learn more about these features on the Teams Sharepoint site.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #TeamsTips. For more information, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, contact the IT Services Desk.


5. Remote, not Distant at UCL: Wellbeing and mindfulness

Whether you're working on campus or continuing to work remotely for the time being, it's important to look after your mental and emotional wellbeing.


Please take a look at UCL's self-care resources or take a free online mindfulness course to help reduce and manage your stress levels.
