1. An end of term message from the Provost

Michael Spence has recorded an end of term message for UCL staff ahead of the annual Easter closure – you can watch the message on Staff News.


2. Return to UCL roadshows and Future of Work survey

We are holding a series of Return to Campus Roadshows following the Government’s announcement of the phased easing of lockdown and what this means for more activity on campus – find out more and register here. You can also view the recording of the BEAMS roadshow that took place on 25 March (UCL login required).


If you haven’t already, please complete the Future of Work pulse checker to let us know what has and hasn't worked about working from home, and what you you like to see in the future. This short, one-off survey contains seven questions – over 3,500 of you have already had your say, and you have until 18 April 2021 to give us your views.


3. Symptom-free COVID-19 testing over the Easter closure

UCL’s COVID-19 testing centre at Bidborough House will operate limited opening times over the Easter closure, opening on Tuesday 6 and Friday 9 April so that staff and students can continue to test twice a week. Opening dates/times are as follows:


  • Friday 2 April (Good Friday) – CLOSED
  • Monday 5 April (Easter Monday) – CLOSED
  • Tuesday 6 April – Open 8.30am–3pm
  • Wednesday 7 April – CLOSED
  • Thursday 8 April – CLOSED
  • Friday 9 April – Open 8.30am–3pm
  • Monday 12 April – Opening resumes as normal.


If you cannot reach Bidborough House on these days, community testing is available to all and you can find your nearest facility here.


Anyone that is part of a household or bubble of a school pupil or staff member are also eligible for home-test kits, which can be collected from your nearest participating test-site, or ordered to your home address. Find out more about booking tests on UCL’s Coronavirus web pages.


The last date for dropping off symptom-free PCR samples (at the Royal Free and for out-of-hours staff) is Tuesday 30 March, to allow time for processing prior to closure. Collections will resume the following week.


4. Statement: Uncertainty about and cuts to research funding

Professor David Price, UCL’s Vice-Provost (Research), has given a statement on the impact on the UK's position as a scientific superpower posed by the current uncertainty around Horizon Europe and Official Development Assistance (ODA) research funding.


Read the full statement on UCL News.


5. UCL Education Conference 2021 programme released – sign up to attend on April 14

UCL President and Provost Dr Michael Spence and academic and psychologist Dame Shirley Pearce will lead this year's conference focused on 'Bringing us all together: Connected Learning, connected learners, and learning through collaboration'. The Education Conference offers an opportunity for UCL's teaching and learning community to reflect on how COVID has changed education in the short and long-term, and to share and celebrate examples of innovative practice amidst the pandemic. The Education Awards and Student Choice Awards shortlists, recognising colleagues who've made outstanding contributions to the learning experience and success of our students, will also be announced on the day.


Abstracts are now live alongside the draft conference programme. Don't forget to sign up.


6. Online workstation assessments: A reminder

To ensure you’re comfortable while you’re working – whether that is on campus or from home – UCL requires that all staff and postgraduate researchers complete a DSE assessment to reduce the risks of ill-health associated with using display screen equipment.


To find out more and complete your assessment, please visit the Safety Services website.


7. Your Wellbeing: Complete this week's survey

Please complete Week 39 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


In March, April and May, there will be three prize draws with a prize of £50 in John Lewis vouchers, with winners picked from staff who have completed the Your Wellbeing survey every week – find out more about the prize draw on the UCL HR website.


8. Coronavirus: The Whole Story – How do we build pandemic resilience?

This week, host Vivienne Parry OBE speaks to Professor Sir Richard Blundell, Professor of Political Economy and Director of the ESRC Centre for the Microeconomic Analysis of Public Policy, and Julia Kreienkamp, a researcher at the UCL Global Governance Institute. What is pandemic resilience, and how are going to get it? What we can do to help mitigate the long-term harms of coronavirus and future pandemics? Listen now on UCL Minds.
