Daily coronavirus (Covid-19) update


Dear colleagues,


We are writing to you with todayâ€™s update about coronavirus, with new guidance for staff and students about international travel and advice about working from home.


As of today, staff and students should not travel internationally on UCL business until 1 May 2020. Delegations and visits organised by central UCL teams have been cancelled and SMT are not travelling on UCL business. The Global Engagement Office will be in touch directly with members of UCL delegations who were due to travel to specific locations shortly (e.g. to India and the west coast of the USA). This position recognises that we should do all we can to prevent the spread of the disease and to protect the more vulnerable members of our communities.


Please therefore postpone or cancel all incoming external international visitors for seminars and conferences with immediate effect. We also strongly recommend cancelling or postponing work-related international travel. Please consider whether planned international collaborative activity could instead be conducted virtually (e.g. via Microsoft Teams).


The advice on personal international travel currently remains as in yesterdayâ€™s daily update: in short, follow the advice issued by the Foreign & Commonwealth Office (FCO), which advises against all but essential travel to certain countries and regions. Please be aware that advice may change while you are travelling, which could affect your ability to return home or require you to self-isolate.


We recognise that we cannot stop staff travelling where they wish to do so. If you do choose to travel against FCO advice at the start of your trip and you are not able to return to work when you return (either because you are unwell or you are required to self-isolate) you may not be eligible for special leave.


The university remains open as usual on the advice of Public Health England. However, we are conscious that more staff than usual may need to work from home. This may be because they have an underlying health condition, additional coronavirus-related caring responsibilities, or a requirement to self-isolate. These or other personal reasons should be discussed and agreed with your line manager. More generally, it is possible that the university may be required to close at some point in the future.


Please make sure that, wherever possible, you and your teams are able to work remotely if needed, by taking the following precautionary measures now:

      

  • Make sure your line manager has your telephone number and that you have the telephone number of your direct reports.
  • Please register your mobile number now via MyHR so we can contact you via SMS in case of an emergency. On the menu, go to Employee Self Service then select Personal Information and Personal Information again. Students should register their mobile numbers via Portico.
  • If you have a work laptop and charger, take them home with you each night. You may also choose to use your own personal computer at home if you prefer.
  • Make yourself familiar with Microsoft Teams and ensure you know how to use Desktop@UCL Anywhere, the service that allows remote access to the S: drive and other platforms such as MyHR.
  • If you are involved in teaching and assessing students, please review the resources and information available to support you if on-campus teaching is suspended.

    

For more information about working remotely and the online tools available, please visit the IT Services website. You can also access technical support and help guides via the IT Services help desk.


We understand that not everyone will be able to work remotely. If this applies to you, you will be granted special leave. Please speak to your line manager in the first instance to arrange this.


Of course, we hope that none of this is necessary, but it is important that we are prepared in case circumstances change.

Support available for staff and students

If you have any concerns about your health you should contact the free NHS 111 telephone helpline (or find specific advice on the NHS website if you are unable to get through).


If you are worried about the coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager in the first instance. You can also access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
