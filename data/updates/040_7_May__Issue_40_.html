Thursday 7 May 2020

Daily coronavirus (Covid-19)
update: issue 40


Dear colleagues,


We’re now into a seventh week of lockdown and I know that many of you are facing some real workload challenges and many competing priorities. Staff in student-facing roles will be particularly aware of the pace of change, as we get to grips with completely reviewed and adapted examinations processes and work through the marking that follows whilst simultaneously planning ahead for new ways of delivering programmes for the next academic term.


The scale of these changes is considerable – and I know will be requiring a huge amount of mental and physical effort from you all, at a time when there are big calls on your reserves in your personal lives as well. I am enormously grateful for everything you are doing, as I know our students will be: both now through examinations and summer project work, and from term 1 as you support them through the transition out of lockdown and back to campus in some form. It’s clear that significant social distancing will be in place for at least part of the next academic year, and we’ll need to do many things differently.


I can’t overstate how important the work on our teaching offer for term 1 is. Our students are absolutely fundamental to our identity as a research-based university, and making sure we can offer them the best of UCL, even in constrained times, has to be our top priority. We need to be focused on our community like never before, creating and maintaining a sense of connection while we’re working remotely and as we transition back to campus, so that new and returning students continue to feel part of UCL and are supported to learn effectively.


The pandemic is also exposing one of the difficult truths of the economics of higher education: that research is not funded at a sufficient level to cover its full costs, and that we are reliant on student fee income, particularly overseas fee income, to fund our full range of activities. If we are not able to recruit sufficient students because we can’t demonstrate to them that we are more than prepared to offer our usual high quality education then we are facing major challenges without easy solutions.


Recognising this, we want to do everything we can to support you:


  • Training and advice: the Arena Centre and Digital Education are working closely with the Office of the Vice-Provost (Education & Student Affairs) and with staff from all the faculties to provide advice, templates, online training and other resources. Through your faculty delivery teams, they can co-ordinate support and advice as you think through what is possible and what you may have to prioritise. There is expert help and guidance available and I would encourage you to draw on it wherever you can.
  • Review and prioritisation: I also want to ask you to think creatively and tactically about what you can realistically offer for next year. An excellent teaching programme for 2020-21 will necessarily be different from what we might have planned in more conventional times – and will require decisions about what not to run. Faculty Delivery Teams are working with you to identify which modules are best suited for online delivery so that we can put focused effort where it will make the most difference to students, rather than trying to get as much as possible online.


Throughout my time as Provost, I’ve championed education and pushed the university always to put education on an equal footing with research. For this crisis period, I find myself needing to go further to ask you to prioritise education for the next few months, wherever and however you can. For those members of academic staff who feel they are being pulled in multiple directions, I want to reassure you that the prioritisation of education delivery now will be recognised in appraisals and promotions cases by your heads of department and Deans. The promotions framework is more than sufficiently flexible to recognise the impact of leadership you show on education through this pandemic, and to reward those who take on significant co-ordination and other roles to steer us through the crisis.


UCL thrives on opening up opportunities, and we have never been afraid to rethink the norms of education and research. For as long as our ways of working are disrupted, this rethinking is absolutely vital. Full focus on education now is needed to secure the longer-term viability of our research, and so that we can continue to offer the high-quality environment for research and research-based learning that is our hallmark. I know that what I am asking will require you to dig deep, but I also know that, with everyone playing their part, we will continue to educate graduates who are proud to carry UCL’s name.


Best wishes,


Professor Michael Arthur
UCL President & Provost

There will be no updates on the Bank Holiday or over the weekend unless we need to contact you urgently. We hope you enjoy the long weekend and have a chance to switch off and spend time with your loved ones.


1. Preparing for the Government announcement on the easing of lockdown restrictions

As you may have heard, it’s widely expected that the Prime Minister will make an announcement over the next few days regarding plans to ease the lockdown restrictions. At present, the extent of the Government’s plans is not known.


This is a really important moment for many of us who want to get back into our offices, labs, studios and libraries but please remember that ‘unlocking’ UCL from its present state is a really big job and will take some time.


UCL’s Gold team has developed plans for post-lockdown operations and an action plan has been considered over the last couple of weeks, which will be updated as soon as there is any Government announcement. Once we know what a return to campus might look like and when it might happen we will let you know.  And of course we will get the campus safely open as quickly as we can. In the meantime, please continue to work as you have been doing – and thank you for your continued patience and understanding.


2. Equality, Diversity and Inclusion check-in survey: Tell us how you're feeling

The pandemic is continuing to have a huge impact on everyone and our day-to-day lives have changed drastically. It’s important that we learn from this so we can plan how to support you both in the short term and in the longer term for example through reflecting the impact of this coronavirus crisis in our promotion policies in the years ahead. Please give us 10 minutes of your time to respond to our questionnaire, which is completely anonymous, to take stock and tell us how you’re feeling about your situation and about what’s happening at the university level.


The survey is being run collaboratively by the central Equality, Diversity and Inclusion (EDI) Team and the faculty Vice Deans of EDI. You can fill in the survey online and it will be open for responses until 5pm on 15 May.


3. How UCL staff and students are tackling the pandemic

The UCL Covid-19 Social Study is the UK’s largest survey of adults’ wellbeing and mental health during the coronavirus epidemic, looking at the psychological and social effects of coronavirus in the UK.


This week, the survey has revealed that worries about falling ill with the coronavirus have increased over the last week amid growing speculation that the UK may ease lockdown restrictions. With an announcement expected from the Prime Minister this weekend about an ease to the restrictions, the survey’s findings can help to inform the Government’s response so that it considers and mitigates these risks.


Making sure that we look after our mental health and wellbeing is more important than ever, so please do make sure that you’re aware of the support that UCL can offer you, and check out free resources such as the NHS’s Every Mind Matters website and Action for Happiness.


If you’d like to take part in the Covid-19 Social Study you can still sign up on the study’s website. The website also has some great resources for “Creative Isolation” during lockdown.


4. Student Psychological and Counselling Services – support for students

UCL’s counselling service, Student Psychological and Counselling Services (SPCS) is continuing to offer support to students throughout the current situation. Please continue to remind your students that SPCS services are still available. Students can register as usual for ongoing counselling and psychiatric assessment delivered through Microsoft Teams.


You can find out more on the Staff News website.


5. Online advice and engagement


  • Yesterday there were 4,285 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 1,646 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 1,721 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
