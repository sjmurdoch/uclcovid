1. International travel guidance

The Foreign & Commonwealth Office (FCO) is now advising against all but essential international travel indefinitely. This position recognises that we should do all we can to prevent the spread of coronavirus and protect the more vulnerable members of our communities.

In light of this advice, UCL is extending its travel restrictions as of today. Staff and students should not travel internationally on UCL business until 1 August 2020.

Please postpone or cancel any planned trips and any incoming external international visitors for seminars and conferences from today up to 1 August. Please also consider whether planned international collaborative activity could instead be conducted virtually (e.g. via Microsoft Teams).

With many countries having put or putting travel restrictions in place at short notice, we advise that you do not make any non-essential bookings at this time. If you are required to travel for essential reasons please use Key Travel to make the booking. Using Key Travel allows UCL to contact you quickly and bring you back to the UK safely in case of emergency.  As a consequence, UCL will no longer accept expense claims for any international travel bookings made from today. Please submit any outstanding historical international travel-related expenses claims by the end of April.

Neither you nor UCL should be out-of-pocket for travel cancelled or curtailed due to coronavirus.  Please cancel your bookings as this will help both Key Travel and colleagues in Procurement who are working through staff bookings and expense claims to notify staff of bookings that need to be cancelled.

If you need help with a booking or would like further information on the cancellation and claims process, you can find out more on UCLâ€™s Travel Guidance webpages.
