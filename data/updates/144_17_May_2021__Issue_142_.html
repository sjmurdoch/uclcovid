Dear colleagues,


Today marks step 3 of the Government’s roadmap to ease lockdown restrictions, with more staff and students returning to campus. 1. Before you return to campus

Please follow the most up to date COVID-19 safety measures, which currently include washing your hands frequently, covering your face and maintaining 2m social distancing.


Visit our Keeping Safe on Campus webpages for the latest guidance and make sure you follow these steps before you return:


Risk assessments

Please could managers review and update local risk assessments in accordance with UCL’s revised generic risk assessment and we have simplified this process to make this easier. Find out more on the UCL Safety Services webpages.


Induction training

You must complete the Return to UCL Induction, which has been updated to reflect the Government roadmap. If you have previously completed the course, you can also take this updated course as a refresher to ensure that you’re up to date.


Workstation assessment

All staff should complete their Display Screen Equipment (DSE) assessment to ensure your onsite workstation is suitable for you.


Symptom-free testing

The Government is encouraging everyone without symptoms to get tested twice a week. You can get tested locally or order a test to be delivered to your home, and UCL staff and students can also get tested at our Bidborough House testing centre. Find out more on the UCL coronavirus webpages.


For students intending to return to campus, the Government’s advice is to get tested before travelling, and then twice a week when they return. We have published updated advice for students with details about how and where they can take COVID-19 tests, and a video showing a tour of UCL’s Bidborough House COVID testing facility by Carol Paige, Student Union UCL’s Democracy, Operations and Community Officer.


2. Travel guidance from 17 May

UCL has reviewed its position in line with the latest Government advice on travel. Staff and students may now travel on UCL business to countries that are on the Green or Amber lists for essential reasons. This is to reflect the ongoing uncertainty around travel and our need to prioritise safety. Please consider alternatives to travel such as using virtual platforms like Microsoft Teams prior to booking.


Where travel is necessary staff will need to:


  • Discuss travel plans with their supervisor, Department Manager or Head of Department;
  • Ensure they can continue to work if required to quarantine upon return. Where this is not possible staff may be asked to take annual or unpaid leave. Please see HR's policies and guidance about leave and absence related to coronavirus.   
  • Follow all Government requirements for travelling and returning safely, including COVID-19 tests and quarantine. If travel is deemed essential for UCL business staff will not be expected to personally meet the costs of these additional requirements; where travel is being paid for via a grant, the grant should also cover these costs (please speak to the grant provider in advance).  
  • Use Key Travel, and book flexible tickets or refundable travel options, and ensure they have a valid risk assessment and insurance.


For further information and guidance visit UCL’s Travel Pages.


3. ComFluCOV COVID-19 vaccine trial

The NIHR UCLH Clinical Research Facility is recruiting volunteers for a new vaccine trial. The study will look at the side effects and immune response given when people receive their COVID-19 booster and flu vaccine at the same appointment. If you are in London, under 65 and have received your first Pfizer vaccine dose in the last 4-12 weeks, you may be eligible to take part. Find out more on the ComFluCOV trial website.
