2. Security information for staff on campus

Sadly, there has been an increase in opportunists trying to access UCLâ€™s buildings recently. If you are working on campus, please be extra vigilant and ensure all doors are secure, particularly those with ID card access, and be careful of tailgaters. Contact security if you see a stranger in a building with no ID.


Be careful if you are visiting campus to pick up equipment and do not leave it unattended at any time. If you cycle to campus we recommend using UCL bike racks, which are CCTV monitored, and two locks (a D-lock and a chain).


UCL Security are working 24 hours a day, seven days a week to keep you safe. We can all help to keep our community safe by staying alert and being active bystanders. To speak to the Security team in an emergency, please call 020 7679 2222 or 2222 from an internal phone. In a non-emergency situation you can call 020 7679 2108. Find out more on the UCL Security website.

4. UK Government support package for research

At the end of last month, the UK Government announced a support package for research, made up of two elements. The first is the COVID-19 Grant Extension Allocation, which is to be used to sustain awards funded through UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) and the National Academies. UCL will be given an allocation by UKRI for use until September next year to ensure that grant outcomes continue to be met. The expectation is that projects ending between 1 April 2020 and 31 March 2021 should be prioritised.


We have yet to receive what UCLâ€™s confirmed allocation will be or the exact terms and conditions but once we do, we shall be able to determine how best to use it in line with UKRIâ€™s expectations. More information will be shared as soon as it becomes available.


The second element is the Research Stabilisation Package: a mixture of low-interest loans with long pay-back periods (around 10 years), supplemented by a small number of Government grants. The Government is still to develop the details, including metrics and conditions, and is due to consult with the sector soon. We will respond to that consultation and assess how and whether we should utilise it.
