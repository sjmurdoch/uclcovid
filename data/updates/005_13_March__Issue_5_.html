Friday 13 March 2020

Daily coronavirus (Covid-19)
update: issue 5


Dear colleagues,


I know many of our staff and students have been worried about the possible impact of coronavirus including what it means for teaching and assessments.


The coronavirus contingency planning group and Education Committee have been working on plans for some time to ensure the safety of our students and staff, and to uphold our academic standards. This afternoon Academic Committee confirmed that:


  • Face-to-face teaching will cease with effect from today, 13 March, with as much teaching as possible moving online from Monday 16 March until the end of term 2.
  • All teaching in term 3 will be delivered online via Moodle as far as possible. This will predominantly affect revision classes for undergraduates and teaching for postgraduate taught students.
  • We will seek to significantly reduce the amount of further formal assessment for first-year undergraduates this year. This is to reduce the burden on staff.
  • Alternative forms of assessment will be put in place for all other taught students, such as open book assessments and further coursework, to enable students to complete assessments away from the university.


We are updating our students about these changes today through the MyUCL e-newsletter and other student channels. We are working with staff across the university to provide the support you need to deliver alternative teaching and assessments, and we will be able to share more details over the coming days about how this will be implemented.


These are extraordinary days and I am sharing with you further reflections in my Provost’s View this week. I am proud of the way that the UCL community is pulling together.  


With thanks for your hard work,

Professor Michael Arthur

UCL President & Provost

1. Confirmed cases of coronavirus at UCL

I wrote to you yesterday to let you know that one of our students has tested positive for coronavirus (Covid-19). 

         

Since then, we have been notified today of a further case – a member of staff who works for one of our contractors. The member of staff has not been on the UCL estate since last Friday and we have not been advised to take any further action by Public Health England in regards to this case.

         

Our immediate priority is to support any students and staff who have been confirmed with the coronavirus. Both our Student Support & Wellbeing and Occupational Health teams are in close communication with individuals affected. We ask that their privacy is respected.

         

I know that this news will concern many of you, who are already feeling anxious about the impact of the virus. If you are worried, I encourage you to speak to your family and friends, or use the specialist counselling services provided by Care First.

         

It is inevitable that there will be further cases in the coming days and weeks as cases increase in the UK.

         

I want to reassure you that we are working closely with Public Health England and the NHS to ensure that UCL remains a safe place for our students, staff and visitors. We will continue to follow their advice and will take further action as required in regard to further cases confirmed at UCL.


2. Working remotely

If you have a pre-existing health condition, or you are close to someone who has, we strongly recommend that you start working from home if possible. Please speak to your line manager when you can if this applies to you. If you are not able to work from home, you will be granted special leave. You can also contact NHS 111 or your GP for medical advice.  

        

If this doesn’t apply to you but you are worried about coming into work, please speak to your line manager about whether you can work from home. This will be agreed on a case-by-case basis.  

        

Please be aware that the increase in staff working remotely, and today’s decision to introduce online teaching for students, will put unprecedented demands on our IT systems. Please be patient. If you can’t connect immediately, try again later.  

        

I would like to reassure you that we are currently expanding capacity on key systems, including Moodle, Desktop@UCL Anywhere and our VPN (for access to services only available on the UCL network).

        

However, to make sure we are as prepared as possible, please take the following steps:

  

  • Make sure you have the necessary software installed on your UCL laptop or home computer.  
  • Use the VPN only when needed, as capacity is limited. You don’t need to use it for Office365, Microsoft Teams or other productivity tools. To save using the VPN, please copy your working files from the N: and S: drives to MS OneDrive (which is cloud accessible).
  • For teaching staff, if you are preparing online lectures, please speak to your department if you think you need a headset. You will also need to install the Lecturecast Personal Capture application on your computer.
  • Make use of Microsoft Teams, especially for collaborating (e.g. for daily “stand-up” calls with your team).  
  • Please keep an eye on the ISD website for latest advice. We will be regularly updating the remote working page with further guidance and support materials.


The Teaching Continuity webpages provide key guidance on how to teach and assess online. They are being updated daily and link to the most up-to-date information including how-to videos.


3. Precautionary measures

Please remember that washing your hands is one of the easiest ways to protect yourself and others from illnesses including coronavirus, and could help mitigate the impact of the infection.

       

The advice from the NHS is that washing hands well with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, whenever you get home or into work, is the most effective preventative measure to take. Hand sanitiser can be used as a back-up, or if soap and water are not available.

       

These articles, on the Vox website and the MarketWatch website explain why soap and hot water work so well.

  • Additional answers to frequently asked questions about self-isolating and what to do if you feel unwell have been added to the main coronavirus webpage.
  • Yesterday there were 21,651 visits to the main coronavirus webpage and 1,794 visits to the Mandarin Chinese version. There were also 2,325 visits to askUCL.  

      

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. Our webpage also contains a link to the Foreign & Commonwealth Office (FCO) Travel Advice pages, which you can use to check on their regular revisions to Category 1 or 2 countries and regions (to which the FCO advises against all but essential travel.)

      

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.  


5. Potential impact on UCL

The working group of Deans, Heads of Department, members of the senior management team and Students’ Union UCL continues to meet daily, and Education Committee weekly, to mitigate the short and longer term impact of the virus on UCL. Their work covers the 15 separate but related strands of activity listed in Tuesday’s daily update. More detailed information is being shared regularly with Deans, VP offices, PS Directors, DOOs and Faculty Tutors.

      

If you have any questions or comments for the working group, please submit them via our online form.


6. Support available for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have the virus, please self-isolate for seven days in line with the current Government advice. You can also find advice on the NHS website.


If you are worried about the coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager in the first instance. You can also access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
