1. UCL’s response to new Government national restrictions from 5 November 2020

You should have received a message from the Provost outlining UCL’s initial response to the Government announcement about a new national lockdown in England, starting this Thursday, 5 November.


The message sets out what we propose to do, including keeping laboratories and key areas of campus open, such as libraries and study spaces, supporting our key workers who are required to be on site, and reviewing the amount of face-to-face teaching provision offered.


We are working closely and consulting with Students’ Union UCL and our trade unions, and will be in touch once we have further information and guidance. We will increase the frequency of these Coronavirus update emails to give you the information you need as soon as possible.


You can read the Provost’s message on UCL Staff News.

                         

2. Enrolment for Term 1 wellbeing courses closes soon: Don’t miss out


UCL Organisational Development and Workplace Health have collaborated to provide a series of bite-sized interactive webinars aimed at empowering staff to build resilience and manage their wellbeing during the pandemic. The courses are centrally funded and open to all staff, and include:


  • Mental resilience in uncertain times – a guided 7-week course starting 19 November. A self-guided version is also available.
  • Financial wellbeing – a one-day online course on 17 November
  • Mental health first-aid – a one-day online course, various dates
  • Leading Wellbeing conversations – a one-day online course, various dates.


Explore the full range of training available on the Learning and Development webpage.

                                   

3. Your Wellbeing: Complete the survey for a chance to win £50

Please complete Week 20 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


HR are currently offering three £50 Amazon vouchers to winners who will be picked from members of staff who complete four consecutive Your Wellbeing surveys – find out more on the HR website.


Please do also take a look at our new suite of self-paced resources and interactive webinars covering all aspects of wellbeing, which have been centrally funded and launched as a result of Your Wellbeing survey results.

                                   

4. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – Will a national lockdown save the Christmas break?

This week, we're back on home soil and looking at the issue of national vs regional lockdowns. Vivienne Parry speaks to UCL experts from the Institute for Global Health, The Bartlett School of Planning and the UCL Department of Geography to explore the row about lockdowns, the rationale behind them and whether or not a second national lockdown could have been avoided.


This episode was recorded before the UK Government’s announcement about a second national lockdown in England.
