1. UCL’s new Community Commitment to Protect Each Other and resources for staff

We’ve been working closely with Students’ Union UCL to develop messaging that emphasises our shared responsibility for all our safety. Central to this is the newly developed Community Commitment to Protect Each Other. This outlines what is expected of everyone – staff and students alike – with expectations about respecting and protecting each other, reporting sickness and keeping safe on campus and in the wider community.


Information about the Commitment, alongside a welcome message from the Provost and Students’ Union sabbatical officers, is being sent by email to all new and returning students today via Portico.


As well as this, we have published:


  • Visiting Campus Checklist for students: The Visiting Campus Checklist has been published to help students plan their visits to campus.
  • Student disciplinary procedures: Guidance to help staff deal with breaches of safety requirements. Please see Student Disciplinary Procedures and non-compliance with social distancing measures on the SRS webpages.
  • New Educating on Campus webpage: The Educating on campus 2020–21 webpage brings together guidance for staff delivering education on campus. It includes information on managing teaching spaces, risk assessment procedure for in-person education in non-generic teaching spaces, using digital platforms in on-campus teaching, how to deal with breaches of guidelines and further guidance about safety on campus.
  • RegisterUCL, the new central attendance system: The Registrar has written to Faculty Tutors and DOOs explaining how attendance must be captured in the coming year to ensure we are complying with Public Health England’s regulations to keep our campus safe. Full information about RegisterUCL.                                                                                                          

2. The NHS COVID-19 app and reporting coronavirus symptoms via UCL Connect to Protect

The NHS has launched a new contact tracing app, which notifies individuals if they have been in significant proximity with anyone who later tests positive for coronavirus, without personal data leaving your smartphone. You can download the app now from https://www.covid19.nhs.uk/.


If you do start to feel unwell with coronavirus symptoms please also report your symptoms using UCL’s Connect to Protect reporting tool, which is designed to work alongside the NHS Test and Trace system. Please follow our step-by-step guidance on how to report and manage potential cases of coronavirus at UCL.


3. Free flu vaccination vouchers available for UCL staff

UCL is supporting a seasonal flu vaccination programme for all staff in the UK during the Autumn Term in partnership with Boots Pharmacies.


It is particularly important this year that people are vaccinated against flu – not only for our own protection, and the health of our families and friends, but to help reduce the burden of respiratory diseases on the NHS.


Find out how to get a voucher to use in any participating Boots store and hear from Professor Graham Hart, Dean of the Faculty of Population Health Sciences, about the importance of getting a vaccination.


4. USS Pensions: Consultation and Town Hall reminder

Members of USS can hear about the background and content of the current employer consultation at this Town Hall event chaired by the Provost on 1 October at 12.30. Speakers will outline the possible outcomes and the potential impact for members and employers, with an opportunity for you to ask questions.


If you would like to attend this event, please register in advance using Eventbrite where you can also find a link to submit questions before the event. USS members can also submit their feedback on the consultation by following the link on the HR website.


5. Student Success Advisers: Secondments available

UCL is recruiting 32 Student Success Advisers (SSAs). These are new positions within UCL as a way of providing additional support to first-year students, and will be offered initially on a secondment basis at Grade 7.


Find out more about the role and how to apply on the HR website. The deadline to apply is 5 October.

We’re offering three £50 Amazon vouchers to winners who will be picked from members of staff who complete all of the next four weekly Your Wellbeing surveys. Find out more on the HR website, and don’t forget to complete Week 15 of the survey.


7. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – how do we cope with isolation?

This week we're talking about isolation – coming out of it, going back into it, and how to cope mentally and emotionally. What can astronauts teach us about dealing with isolation? How can we enable safe interaction among young people? Can digital touch help? Join Vivienne Parry as she talks to Dr Kevin Fong (Consultant Anaesthetist at UCL Hospitals, Senior Lecturer in Physiology), Professor Peter Fonagy (Head of the Division of Psychology and Language Sciences, Brain Sciences) and Professor Carey Jewitt (Professor of Learning and Technology, IoE).
