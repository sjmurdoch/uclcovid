1. Updated travel advice: Self-isolation and returning to campus

UCL has started moving through our phased and measured return to campus. While the majority of staff should continue to work remotely, we know that many colleagues are thinking about what they may have to do ahead of coming back to campus.


Staff currently living abroad or wishing to travel abroad should be aware that the Government has introduced a two-week self-isolation period for anyone entering the UK on or after 8 June (this does not apply to travellers from Ireland, the Channel Islands or the Isle of Man if they have been there for 14 days or more).


Staff currently abroad

If you have been living abroad during the lockdown period, you should discuss your planned return to campus with your line manager and ensure that you return to the UK at least two weeks before you are required, so that you can complete the self-isolation period.


During self-isolation you will need either to:


  • work from home, or
  • if your job does not lend itself to working from home, volunteer for a role that can be carried out from home via UCL Together, or
  • continue on furlough, if you were on furlough


If none of these scenarios is possible, you will be granted paid leave.


Staff who wish to travel abroad

The Government advice remains that you should not travel abroad unless it is essential. UCL is encouraging staff not to book travel abroad until these restrictions are lifted, and only after you have discussed plans with your line manager.


If you still wish to travel abroad for annual leave, please discuss this with your line manager and be aware that it may not be possible to release you for annual leave plus the two-week self-isolation period. If your line manager agrees to you taking annual leave plus the two-week isolation period, then during the self-isolation period you will need to either:


  • work from home, or
  • take annual leave, or
  • take unpaid leave.


This information is included in HR's FAQs about leave and absence related to coronavirus.


2. Guidance for using face coverings on campus

As the first members of staff start to return to campus as part of our pilot schemes, many of you have been asking about the use of face coverings on campus. The UK Government is advising that people should wear a face covering in enclosed spaces where social distancing is not possible and where they come into contact with others that they do not normally meet.


It’s important to reiterate that, at this point, we are still expecting the vast majority of staff to work remotely. Unless you are a key worker or taking part in one of the pilots as part of our phased and measured return to campus, you should continue to work remotely. UCL’s guidance for those on campus is:


  • A face covering can be very simple and just needs to cover your mouth and nose. Importantly, a face covering is a precautionary measure and not the same as personal protective equipment (PPE), which includes surgical masks and tight-fitting face masks. PPE must continue to be reserved only for those who need it.
  • Face coverings should be worn whenever you may come into close contact with someone, for example when moving around campus or within buildings, and in spaces where social distancing is difficult.
  • Assuming you can work 2 metres from others, face coverings are not required in your working area and can be removed when you arrive at your desk or other workstation. Colleagues working in reception or support areas where a high degree of interaction takes place may need to keep face coverings on.
  • Where face coverings or PPE is identified as a mandatory requirement through risk assessment, UCL will provide face coverings to staff through normal department procurement routes. However, everyone should aim to provide their own face covering for general purposes.
  • Staff and students should buy or make their own face coverings, where possible. For step-by-step instructions on how to make your own face coverings, visit the UCL Institute of Making website. Public Health England has also shared a guide to make your own face coverings, simply by using a t-shirt.
  • We acknowledge that it may not be possible for some people to wear face coverings comfortably, for example, if you have a respiratory condition. Deaf people or those with hearing loss may also be prevented from lip reading by the use of face coverings. In these instances, please make sure you follow social distancing measures as far as possible and speak to your line manager or Workplace Health about what support is available for you.


For more information about what UCL is doing to keep everyone safe on campus, and how you can make a difference, please visit the Keeping Safe on Campus webpages.


3. UCL’s new student recruitment campaign

Despite the uncertainty that all of us are living through as a result of the pandemic, it’s still vitally important that UCL continues to attract the best and brightest students from across the world to study with us. Teams from across UCL have been working together to showcase what we have to offer for prospective students and persuade them to join us next year.


Yesterday saw the launch of UCL’s largest ever postgraduate taught student recruitment campaign, targeted at recent graduates in London and the South East. The campaign covers a huge range of digital channels including Google, social media and various online study platforms like Prospects and FindAMasters, and has been put together using insights from prospective PGT students in order to understand some of the motives behind applying for study and the barriers involved in doing so. You can take a look at the campaign website on the Prospective Students website.


The campaign will run until 28 August – if you have marketing budget and would like to promote your subject area alongside the campaign then please contact your Faculty marketing team or representative.


4. Bartlett Hacks: Climate solutions in a time of pandemic, 24–26 June 2020

The Bartlett Faculty of the Built Environment has just launched Bartlett Hacks – an online hackathon designed to foster collaboration and develop ideas for the urgent climate emergency we still face during the coronavirus crisis and help build resilience for a post-pandemic world.


Bartlett Hacks is open to everyone. From energy systems to travel, urbanisation, health and wellbeing to digital innovations, we want your ideas; if you have an idea how to tackle these issues, this hackathon is for you. Find a team and get ready to make a change in the world – the best solution will win a prize of £1,000.

6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
