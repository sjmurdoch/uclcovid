1. Working remotely and reopening campus

At UCL, your health and wellbeing are paramount and we are working through the latest Government guidelines, to ensure campus is as safe as possible before the first members of staff can return to work onsite. As well as studying the guidelines we are drawing on the expertise of our researchers, including specialists in health, education, human behaviour and infection prevention, to develop a bespoke plan that protects our community.

        

The situation is complex and it will take some time to draw together and implement all the elements of the plan. Thank you for your understanding.  Just as the Government is finding nationally, unlocking needs to be much slower and more nuanced than the act of lockdown.

        

Our plan is to reopen a small number of buildings and research areas for staff to work in from June 2020. Based on the experience of these first pilot schemes, we will be able to judge the appropriate pace at which to phase the re-opening of campus.  

        

Returning to campus, even for a pilot, will not be right or possible for everyone at the moment. Please rest assured that no one will be compelled to return to UCL for the pilots if they do not wish to do so. You may be concerned about your health or commute to work, have childcare or caring responsibilities. We are committed to making sure you come back only when you’re confident that it is right for you.

        

Finally, remember that our position on returning to campus has not changed; unless you are one of our key staff who has worked onsite during the lockdown, please continue to work remotely.

                      

2. Register your COVID-19 research project

UCL’s researchers are applying their cross-disciplinary expertise to help us all to recover from COVID-19 and to make the world more resilient and equitable in the future. Our community is urgently researching new ways of tackling every aspect of the pandemic, including improved treatments, vaccine development, rapid testing and tracking systems, as well as addressing the economic and social impacts. Over a hundred projects are already underway and many more are in development.

       

To help identify and support projects related to the pandemic, we are asking all Principal Investigators undertaking COVID-19 research activities to let us know what they are doing by completing our new Project Form. It shouldn’t take you more than 10 minutes.

      

The information you enter will help us to coordinate support for researchers and also allocate funds. Where appropriate, it will help connect you with the Communications and Marketing team who can advise on working with the media. If you are applying for external funding please complete this form together with UCL’s standard processes.

       

If you have previously informed healthofpublic@ucl.ac.uk of any applications submitted or awards received, you do not need to enter your information again, but we may be in touch with you in the coming week for more details about your project.

                      

3. UCL Press: Call for submissions

The rapid changes brought about by the pandemic are already having visible effects on the environment. As part of the UCL’s open scholarship journal, UCL Press are inviting submissions for “COVID-19 and interactions with our environment”. This special series aims to address some of these issues and shape the debate about the effects on the environment and our subsequent recovery from COVID-19.

    

Find out more, including how to submit a paper, on the UCL Press website.

        

4. International Day Against Homophobia, Biphobia and Transphobia

Even though we can’t mark the day in person or fly the rainbow flag over the Wilkins Building, this Sunday 17 May, UCL will be showing our support for our LGBTQ+ community, some of which can be seen here from our Senior Management Team and Council members.  

  

Right now is a great time to show your support for LGBTQ+ colleagues who may be living alone or in non-supportive environments, and which may be affecting their wellbeing. To find out more about UCL’s LGBTQ+ Equalities Advisory Group (LEAG), including upcoming events, please visit the LGBTQ+ Equality webpages.

6. Online advice and engagement

  • Yesterday there were 4,905 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 116 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 1,802 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


7. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
