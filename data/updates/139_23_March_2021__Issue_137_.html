1. The future of work at UCL – tell us your views


A message from Fiona Ryland, UCL’s Chief Operating Officer:


As we start to think about welcoming more staff back onto campus, I want to know what you think – what has worked about working from home and what hasn’t? What activities do you feel are best carried out on site and what type of flexibility would you prefer around how we work in the future? What you tell us now will help us decide what areas we should invest in for our future, what changes we should make to how we work and how we can optimise our workspaces to deliver UCL’s ambitions for years to come.


Please take two minutes to complete the Future of Work pulse checker.


This one-off survey contains seven questions and you have until 18 April 2021 to have your say.


2. UCL’s roadmap for welcoming more staff back to campus

UCL has been considering the Government’s four-step roadmap to ease lockdown restrictions and what it means for our plans for the coming months. We are now creating our own version of the roadmap for the university, underpinned by our people-first approach and commitment to maintaining our symptom-free testing service and the Connect to Protect reporting tool. It also includes what policies we need to put in place to support staff, including colleagues who are Clinically Extremely Vulnerable and not able to work remotely.


We know many staff haven’t been on campus (or in central London) for over a year and our return to onsite activity needs to be approached with caution. We will introduce these changes carefully and in step with the Government roadmap:


  • No earlier than Monday 29 March: The next step in the Government roadmap will see minor changes, with groups of six (or two households) able to meet outdoors.
  • No earlier than Monday 12 April: When we return from the Easter closure period, we are planning for more spaces on campus to reopen, including Bloomsbury Fitness, some study spaces, and food outlets for dining outside. Most staff should continue to work from home if possible, but staff are being recalled to support the reopening of the above spaces and we will be encouraging more staff to start visiting campus occasionally, in an organised way and in line with Government guidance.
  • No earlier than Monday 21 June: The final step of the Government roadmap, when we hope all staff will be able to return to working on campus. Please remember to complete the Future of Work survey to tell us what you’d like to see when we come back.


We will share more information with you as our plans develop, and we will continue to update our Keeping Safe on Campus webpages regularly.
