1. UCL welcomes Government’s reversal on A Level algorithm use

We welcome the Government’s decision that A Level results will be now determined by teacher-predicted grades, rather than the Ofqual algorithm.


We are delighted to accept all students who meet the terms of their original offer.


We are working with Deans and Professional Services staff to determine what this means for teaching and student services next year and also what this might mean for programmes with an externally-determined cap (such as medicine).


Our priority is to be as fair as possible to all applicants, recognising that they have worked hard during unprecedented circumstances and deserve their first choice university.

                                                                                               

2. Pre-enrolment for new students and coming to campus

After A Level results day last week, re-enrolment for returning students and pre-enrolment for new students starts this week – you can find out more on the enrolment pages of the UCL Students website. The Students website has also been updated with extensive information for new students.


We know many of you are receiving queries about whether students will be able to join us on campus. Our current advice is:


  • In Term One, new and returning students have the option of studying on campus or wherever they are based around the world. As the situation is constantly evolving, they may need to change their mind so we will remain flexible – so for example, if students can't arrive on campus for the start of term, they can join us later. Similarly, if they come onto campus but then decide or need to return home, they can do so with no impact on their studies.
  • All Term One core teaching (i.e. everything students need to complete a module successfully) will take place online, including lectures, tutorials, seminars and assessments. There will be highly interactive sessions in real time, as well as engaging learning activities designed so students can study at their own pace and in their own time zone. We are also making sure that dissertations and major projects can be carried out online.
  • Campus will be open but opportunities to join us on campus will be limited, and UK Government guidelines and social distancing requirements will determine how much teaching and other activity can take place. We’re currently anticipating a maximum of 1-2 hours a week of face to face teaching time, although this will vary depending on programme. If students can't join us in London immediately, we will provide alternative content so they can achieve the same learning wherever they are in the world.


You can see the latest information for students about next term on the UCL Students website. Please direct any students with queries to this page, which will be updated regularly.


3. Keeping Safe on Campus webpages: Updates for students

Our Keeping Safe on Campus website has been updated with new information and FAQs specifically for students. The new section highlights information about getting to campus, guidance for disabled students and has an expanded range of FAQs covering teaching and assessment arrangements, services available on campus and advice for students arriving in the UK for the new academic year.


You can direct students to this site if they have any questions about returning to campus in September and what we’re doing to keep everyone as safe as possible.

                                                                                               

4. Your Wellbeing survey – week 9

Please tell us how you’re doing this week by completing Week 9 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey. It takes less than five minutes to complete and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping with the coronavirus crisis. Find out more and complete the survey now on the HR website.
