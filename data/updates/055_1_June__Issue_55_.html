1. Information for students about the new academic year

Following a meeting of Academic Committee on Friday, we are sending messages to all our new and returning undergraduate and postgraduate taught students today, outlining plans for teaching in Term 1 of the next academic year. The Temporary Operating Model for 2020-21, setting out at a high-level how teaching will be operating next year, is available on the coronavirus information hub.


Many students – whether they are coming back to UCL or joining us for the first time – are feeling anxious about what next term will look like. It is important that we are as clear as we can be with them about what they can expect. The Office for Students (OfS) and the Universities and Colleges Admissions Service (UCAS) have also asked universities to be as transparent as possible with students about what their experience will be like this autumn, before the upcoming UCAS deadline.


We want to reassure new and returning students that UCL will not compromise academic standards and they will graduate with an excellent degree that is valued around the world. Despite the difficulties of the pandemic, we are making a promise to students that we will do everything we can to ensure they receive the best possible education and university experience. Social distancing requirements will guide how much teaching can be delivered in person, and students’ learning will be supported through a range of online and interactive teaching, a range of course materials, and access to library services and extra-curricular opportunities.


We are sending these messages via Portico today and you can see a copy of the email to current students on UCL Student News as well as copies of the message to offer holders. We have updated the frequently asked questions for students, prospective students and schools and you can see these on the coronavirus information hub.


We are encouraging returning students to contact askUCL with any questions, but we anticipate many will contact their departments directly. For offer holders, we have set up a dedicated telephone helpline, 0208 059 0939, which will go live on Tuesday morning.


Adapting our teaching to take account of the pandemic is a huge task and we would not be able to do it without the incredible efforts being made by everyone. In order to understand your needs and how we can best support you, we have set up a consultation on education principles for 2020/21. We’ll use your feedback to inform the shape and delivery of the next academic year. The consultation is open to all staff and will run until 1 July 2020, and you can choose to include your name or contribute anonymously if you prefer.

Join writer, broadcaster and UCL graduate Vivienne Parry for this special edition of the podcast, made for the Cheltenham Science Festival @ Home. You can listen to Coronavirus: The Whole Story on the UCL Minds website or wherever you find your podcasts. If you would like to take part in a future episode, or if you’ve got a question about the pandemic you’d like UCL researchers to answer, please get in touch by emailing UCL’s Communications and Marketing team on minds@ucl.ac.uk – we’d love to hear from you.


3. How UCL staff and students are tackling the pandemic

UCL researchers are joining a new coalition of more than 500 scientists from 18 countries to share data on COVID-19, gathered using mass spectrometry techniques which examine people’s blood and other biomarkers.


Coordinated by the University of Manchester, the COVID-19 MS Coalition will look at the ways in which the coronavirus is present in patients’ blood. It aims to find out more about the structure of the virus – a crucial step in the development of a vaccine. It could also be possible to design targeted treatment for patients by looking at how individual people are likely to respond to the virus, based on their biomarkers.


You can find out more on the UCL News website.

                                                                      

4. Online advice and engagement

  • Over the weekend there were 2,053 visits to the coronavirus information hub, 216 visits to the UCL Covid-19 research website and 170 visits to the askUCL landing page.


Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


5. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
