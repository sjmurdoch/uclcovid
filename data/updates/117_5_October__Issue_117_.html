1. UCL’s management structure and Public Health Advisory Panel

The Provost has taken the decision to stand down UCL’s crisis structure and revert to the university’s normal ways of working, with the Senior Management Team continuing to function as an advisory body for the Provost. Details of the senior leadership team can be found on the UCL website.


The senior leadership team continues to be supported by delivery groups including the Education and Operations Delivery Group (EODG) and the Research and External Engagement Delivery Group (REED), as well as the new Public Health Advisory Panel (PHAP).


The Public Health Advisory Panel will serve as an expert advisory group to help inform UCL’s planning and co-ordinate public health-related activity around COVID-19.


The Panel is chaired by Professor Graham Hart (Dean, UCL Faculty of Population Health Sciences). The membership includes UCL academic staff as well as representatives from UCL Planning, Estates, Communications & Marketing, Student & Registry Services, VP Offices and Human Resources. The Panel’s Terms of Reference and full membership list can be found on the UCL coronavirus information hub.


2. COVID-19 testing at UCL

As reported on UCL News, UCL has established a COVID-19 antigen testing facility, which is providing home self-test kits to symptomatic students based in UCL halls and symptomatic staff whose role requires them to work on campus.


You can find out more, including details about eligibility, how to report coronavirus symptoms, how to request a test and how the test works, on the UCL website.


If you think you may have coronavirus, please follow the step-by-step guide to reporting a potential case and register your symptoms through UCL Connect to Protect.


3. Zoom webinar training and security

One-hour training sessions are now available for anyone that wants to learn how to schedule and host a Zoom Webinar – please sign up here.


Zoom Webinars are only needed for broadcast events where there is limited interaction between attendees.  For collaborative sessions with more than 300 participants, you can request a Zoom Large Meeting Licence – find out more on the ISD website.


Please remember: Don’t share your Zoom meeting link or Personal Meeting ID on social media or other public forums. Doing this makes your event open to misuse, as anyone with the link can join your meeting. If you are organising an event aimed at the public, either use a Zoom Webinar, where participants have limited control, or ensure that only valid meeting attendees are sent the details after a registration process. For more information, visit the Staying safe on Zoom webpage.

5. Coronavirus: The Whole Story – Could COVID be the great equaliser?

This week, we’re talking about inequality – a theme we’ve touched on before. We’re joined by an anthropologist, an engineer and a political scientist to explore inequality – and possible solutions – from a global perspective.


Join Vivienne Parry as she speaks with UCL experts Professor Dame Henrietta Moore (Founder and Director of the Institute of Global Prosperity), Professor Cathy Holloway (Professor & Academic Director, Global Disability Innovation Hub), and Dr Tom Pegram (Associate Professor in Global Governance).
