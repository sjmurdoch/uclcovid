1. Supporting students in financial need

We know that many students are experiencing severe financial hardship this year. The situation for some is so critical that they are being forced to consider leaving UCL.


Some colleagues have asked how they can help our students financially and in response, we have set up a Student Support Fund.


Support will be available both to new and returning students. Special consideration will be given to ensure support is available for priority groups who are at risk of being disproportionately affected by COVID-19, including students from low-income families and BAME backgrounds, disabled students, and those with experience of care or homelessness.


Earlier this year, the Provost committed to donating 20% of his salary to the fund. Now, you can contribute if you wish to. You can choose to give as little or as much as you want and every pound will go directly to students in need. For more information and to make a donation, please go to the UCL website.

                                                  

2. Student Success Advisers

UCL is appointing a team of Student Success Advisers to support the new intake of Undergraduate students this year and support excellence and consistency across our student experience. These roles will be embedded in academic departments and coordinated by Student Support and Wellbeing, and their work will complement that of Teaching Administrators as well as Academic and Personal Tutors.


Each Adviser will be responsible for around 200 students, acting as their first point of contact for any questions or problems they may have, and proactively contacting them before and after they arrive. We hope that through this work, we can make sure each and every student feels known as an individual and that UCL cares about them, their wellbeing and success.


3. Staying safe online

Since the start of the coronavirus outbreak, there has been a significant increase in cyber-attacks on universities, with some reports showing an increase of 37% above previous levels. ISD and the Information Security Group (ISG) are working hard to protect UCL, but we need every member of staff to play their part too.


Phishing emails are an extremely common way to break into an organisation. The UCL email system does scan attachments and websites linked to in emails for threats but, as new threats are continuously emerging, this is never going to be 100% effective. Please follow the following tips to help keep UCL and your work safe online:


  • Think twice before clicking on links or attachments – especially if an email is trying to create a sense of urgency
  • If this is from a regular contact, does it sound like them? If in doubt, check with the sender before proceeding – but remember not to use email to contact them, in case their account has been compromised
  • If you think you might have been phished, change your UCL password immediately and email isg@ucl.ac.uk or phone 020 7679 7338 (internal 37338) for help.


For more advice and tools to help you online, please visit ISD’s Stay Secure webpages or email isg@ucl.ac.uk.

5. Booking study spaces

Students and staff who are unable to work remotely are now able to reserve individual study or work spaces by location through the Library Services booking system. Morning and afternoon slots are available for each space, with a 30 minute break in between to allow for cleaning.


Spaces are now available in the Student Centre and the Science Library, and further locations will become available over the coming weeks. Please note that the Student Centre is fully booked until 13 September 2020.


Please visit the UCL Library website to book a space and find out more.
