1. Asymptomatic COVID-19 testing for students


Asymptomatic COVID-19 testing for UCL students starts today, as part of the national testing programme for universities to support students to travel over the winter break. The testing centre at Bidborough House uses rapid Lateral Flow Tests (LFT), which typically provide results within two hours. Testing is free for students and voluntary.


If you are a member of UCL staff, please do not book an appointment for an asymptomatic test. The Government has introduced the national testing programme specifically to support students who wish to leave university for the end of term. We are looking into options for offering tests to staff without coronavirus symptoms in the future and will update you if the situation changes. Staff who have coronavirus symptoms should follow the guide to reporting potential cases.


We have also launched our new travel advice pages for students, which set out:

  • What the Government guidance says about travelling in the UK and overseas;
  • How to request a coronavirus test, the different types of test available and choosing the most appropriate one;
  • What students can do to stay safe and help protect friends and family.

                                                                                   

2. Changes to bookable work spaces in Bidborough House


Floors 5 and 6 of Bidborough House are currently unavailable to book while the building is being used as our asymptomatic testing centre.


Staff are still able to book desks on the 2nd (East) floor at Bidborough House, and within 1 St Martin’s Le Grand – please see the Working on Campus pages for more details.


3. London to remain in Tier 2 (High Alert) restrictions from 2 December


The current national lockdown period in England is due to end this Wednesday, 2 December, after which London will be in Tier 2 (High Alert) restrictions. Find out more on the Government website: Full list of local restriction tiers by area and what you need to know about the local restriction tiers.

6. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – What can we learn from Africa’s response to the pandemic?


At the start of the global pandemic, many predicted that Africa would be severely affected by COVID-19. But several months on, and the continent hasn’t seen the levels of infection or COVID-related mortality that were originally expected. This week, Professor Ijeoma Uchegbu (UCL Pharmaceutical Science) and Professor Ibrahim Abubakar (Global Health) join host Vivienne Parry to discuss why this might be, and what we can learn from how the pandemic has affected countries in Africa.
