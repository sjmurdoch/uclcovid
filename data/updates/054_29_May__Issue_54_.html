1. Working remotely and reopening campus

We are all currently working and studying remotely, except for a very small number of buildings and research areas. As you will know from last week’s daily updates, a number of pilot schemes have been planned, where some areas of the university will open up on a test basis, as part of a measured and phased return to campus. The first phase of these pilots will begin next week.


The pilots are being carried out in line with UK Government guidance, drawing on the advice of UCL’s recognised unions and the expertise of our researchers, including specialists in health, education, human behaviour and infection prevention. They are intended to give UCL a chance to test the principles of how we reopen buildings and implement safety measures, and what we learn from these pilots will help us to get things right as we begin to welcome more staff back to campus.


As the pilots begin, we are asking staff to continue to work remotely and not attempt to come onto campus if you have not been given specific instructions to do so. Unless you are one of our key staff who has worked onsite during the lockdown, or you have been contacted about taking part in one of the pilots, you should continue to work remotely.


We will be introducing a range of measures to protect those on campus, including a range of comprehensive risk assessments, efforts to reduce the number of people in spaces and encouraging new, flexible ways of working to avoid commuting at peak times. We will also be encouraging temperature checks, and asking all staff to wear a face covering when moving around campus. You can find out more, including who to contact if you have any questions, on our new Keeping safe on campus site.


From next week, pilots are planned for the Cancer Institute, Queen Square Institute of Neurology and Chemical Engineering. Staff in these areas have been contacted directly. The Day Nursery has been open throughout the lockdown period for a very small number children of key staff working onsite; we are now beginning to increase the level of service, but this will take some time as we think carefully about how to introduce these safety measures in a childcare setting. We are in touch with parents and carers and will share more information as soon as it is available.


There will also be a limited pilot at the Student Centre. This is intended for staff who are unable to work from home in line with Government guidance. Staff will be invited to pre-book a space, with a maximum of around 100 spaces available. Reopening the Student Centre is a big job, involving deep cleaning and maintenance to ensure that the building is fit for occupation and that staff can comply with safety and social distancing guidelines. We plan to welcome a very limited number of Library and Estates staff at the beginning of the week to test that everything is working before we invite more colleagues to join them. If you would like to request a space to work in the Student Centre, we’ll share information about who to contact via this update next week.


Finally, we’d like to remind you that we know returning to campus, even for a pilot, will not be right or possible for everyone at the moment. No one will be compelled to return to UCL for the pilots if they do not wish to do so. You may be concerned about your health or commute to work for example, or have childcare or caring responsibilities. We are committed to making sure you come back only when you’re confident that it is right for you.


For the latest information about our plans for a gradual return to campus, visit the Keeping safe on campus site. These webpages will be updated with details about the pilot schemes, how to keep safe on campus, and advice and guidance for staff, so please check back regularly.


2. UCL healthcare workers invited to take part in COVID-19 vaccine trial

UCL staff who have a healthcare role – for example as a member of staff at one of our partner hospitals – are invited to take part in a UCLH trial of the COVID-19 vaccine developed at the University of Oxford. The study is looking for clinical and non-clinical healthcare staff, except those whose work is office-based.


The trial will be proceeding as quickly as possible, so those interested in taking part should register their interest urgently – find out more on UCL Staff News.


3. Let the Government know about the impact of COVID-19 on your research

The Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) has launched a survey to gather evidence to understand the implications of COVID-19 on the activities of researchers and research groups. Evidence from the survey can help to inform BEIS’ ongoing policy analysis on research sustainability and R&D talent.


All researchers are invited to complete the survey – it should take about 15 to 20 minutes. The closing date is Tuesday 9 June.

5. Online advice and engagement

  • Yesterday there were 2,885 visits to the coronavirus information hub, 298 visits to the UCL Covid-19 research website and 309 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
