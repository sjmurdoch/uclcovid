1. Quick guide to staying safe while working remotely or on campus

Due to the current restrictions, anyone who can work remotely at this time must do so.


The Safety Services team have provided a guide to the latest advice and resources to keep colleagues safe, whether you’re working remotely or your role requires you to work on campus. You can find the guide on the Safety Services website.


2. Reminder about Connect to Protect

UCL’s Connect to Protect reporting tool was designed to help us monitor and respond to coronavirus cases amongst staff and students. Please report your status via Connect to Protect if you:


  • have coronavirus symptoms;
  • have tested positive for the virus;
  • are waiting for the results of a coronavirus test;
  • need to self-isolate.


Connect to Protect does not replace NHS Test and Trace. It helps UCL:


  • respond to cases and provide support to individuals where it is needed;
  • inform and improve our onsite testing programmes;
  • report coronavirus case figures to Camden Council and the Office for Students, which we are legally required to do;
  • identify and tackle outbreaks promptly, protecting UCL and the wider community.


3. Changes to risk assessments and safety procedures on campus

In light of the recent increase in coronavirus cases and the tightening of Government restrictions, UCL is now mandating 2-metre social distancing as our default position in all settings, having provided some flexibility for critical lab-based activity if accompanied by a risk assessment.


All line managers responsible for activity which is continuing on campus must now review and update their local risk assessments. You can find UCL’s generic institutional assessments here and adapt these for your specific requirements.


If you need to work closer than 2 metres for any period (for example, during practical work in dentistry), you must ensure this decision has been approved by your Head of Department, that the work cannot be carried out in any other way, and that risk assessments are up to date with appropriate additional control measures in place.


4. Support for parents and carers

UCL is committed to supporting all our staff affected by school closures, or who have additional caring responsibilities.


If this applies to you, please speak to your line manager about what options are available, such as changing your work routine, delivering more learning asynchronously, reprioritising your objectives, reducing the number of hours you are able to work or potentially being placed on the Government’s furlough scheme or flexible (part-time) furlough. In addition, the maximum five-day period for carer's leave within a twelve month period will be temporarily suspended. Anyone in these circumstances will continue to receive full pay.


Under Government guidance, university staff are considered critical workers and are therefore eligible to send their children to school for in-person teaching, although we ask that you think carefully when you are deciding whether to request a place for your child.


Updated advice for staff members who are parents and carers can be found on the HR website.


5. COVID-19 tests for UCL staff

UCL and contracted staff who are working on campus and who do not have COVID-19 symptoms are strongly encouraged to get tested twice a week at UCL’s testing centre in Bidborough House. The centre is part of the national testing programme for universities and uses rapid Lateral Flow Tests (LFTs), which typically provide results within two hours. Testing is free and voluntary. UCL staff can use their UCL UserID to book a test. We will contact contracted staff with details about how to book theirs shortly.


6. Government definition of a coronavirus contact

The UK Government has changed its definition of what is a “contact”, which is used to notify people through the NHS Test and Trace system.


A contact is a person who has been close to someone who has tested positive for COVID-19 with a PCR test. Under the new guidance, this now includes anyone who has been within two metres of someone for more than 15 minutes, whether in a single period or cumulatively over the course of one day.


Information about contacts, and what to do if you have been identified as a contact of someone who has tested positive for coronavirus, can be found on the Government website.
