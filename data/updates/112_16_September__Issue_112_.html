Wednesday 16 September 2020

Coronavirus update: issue 112


Dear colleagues,


As you will have seen in the media, many people are finding it difficult to access coronavirus tests through the NHS – and so I am pleased to tell you today that UCL is planning to offer tests to staff and students who are experiencing coronavirus symptoms.


This is the culmination of several months' work by teams across UCL and UCLH, including our own clinicians and researchers working in this area. In August, we delivered a successful pilot testing programme in the Paul O'Gorman Building for a limited number of staff, linked to the NHS. Now we plan to use what we have learned from the pilot to expand our testing capacity, in line with the Government's recent guidance for universities. We will provide up to 1,000 tests every day for symptomatic staff and students across UCL by the start of term. Testing of asymptomatic staff or students will only be undertaken where our public health advisers consider this appropriate, and will not be offered routinely.


Our aim is to complement the national testing service, not replace it, and anyone who tests positive for the virus will be referred to the NHS Track and Trace system as normal. We are also developing Connect to Protect, an online tool for you to notify us if you think you may have coronavirus. This will help everyone in our community report any cases so we can monitor and respond appropriately. We will, of course, continue to work with Camden and Islington public health teams and with the London Coronavirus Response Cell.


These new initiatives will augment the wide range of safety measures that we have already introduced across campus. By combining UCL's testing provision and this new reporting tool, together with the national and local systems already in place, we are working to make sure our campus is as safe as possible for everyone.


The limited face-to-face activity that has been planned for next term will take place within these guidelines and we will monitor any positive coronavirus cases at UCL very carefully. We are also continuing to follow the latest advice from our public health experts and Government guidance, and we will adapt our response depending on regional and national developments as they emerge.


Work on rolling out testing for UCL staff and students is underway and I am meeting regularly with our public health colleagues who are overseeing its progress. I will update you again as soon as more details are available.


With very best wishes,


Professor Michael Arthur
President & Provost

1. What to do if you or someone you know may have coronavirus

As the Provost writes above, we are developing a new online reporting tool called UCL Connect to Protect, which will be available from tomorrow. You will be able to find out more about the tool and log-in to notify us if you think you have coronavirus on the Keeping Safe on Campus webpages.


In the meantime please follow UCL's step-by-step guidance for what to do if you, a student, or someone you manage may have coronavirus. Remember you must stay at home if:


  • you think you have coronavirus;
  • you (or someone you live with) has a new continuous cough, high temperature or a loss of or change in your sense of taste or smell;
  • you have tested positive for the virus;
  • you are waiting for the results of a coronavirus test;
  • or you have been contacted by NHS Track and Trace and asked to self-isolate.

3. Today’s Teams Tip

If you’re in a large meeting with multiple video feeds, you can select and pin participants to keep them on your screen.


Top Tip: to pin a video feed, right click on the ellipsis (…) next to the attendee’s name and select ‘pin’. This keeps that particular feed prominent and on screen at all times. You can pin as many videos as will fit on your screen and you’ll know which feeds are pinned as there will be a small pin icon at the bottom. To unpin, follow the same process and select ‘unpin’.


Teams crops some videos to make them fit your screen better. If you want a different view of a particular video – for example, if someone’s cropped out of the video or it only shows part of their face – click the ellipsis next to their name and select ‘Fit to frame’ to see the entire video.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #TeamsTips. For more information, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, contact the IT Services Desk.
