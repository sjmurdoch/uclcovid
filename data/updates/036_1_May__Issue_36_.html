1. Financial support for funded and self-funded PhD students

As we move into the second month of lockdown, it is clear that the impact of the pandemic on our Postgraduate Research community is already considerable, and as yet we cannot predict what the eventual outcome will be. Colleagues from the Doctoral School have been in contact with all PhD students today to confirm the following measures to support funded and self-funded PhD students:


  • UCL will support funded PhD students by underwriting final year student stipends at the minimum UKRI rate for up to three months in the first instance (this does not include those already in Continuing Research Student status). This is being kept under review as the external situation changes. Students do not have to take any action at this time.
  • We recognise that self-funded PhD students may be experiencing immediate financial difficulties. As a result, the third and final instalment of tuition fees has now been extended from 2 May until 31 July 2020.
  • PhD students who are experiencing financial hardship can apply to the Financial Assistance Fund for a grant of up to £3,000 at any time up to 31 July 2020. We particularly encourage self-funders to apply to this scheme and their applications will be prioritised. With the current crisis not yet over and the likelihood of it having further effects, we will continue to review what support we can offer to those who face financial difficulties beyond that date.


Finally, we recognise that students not in their final year are also experiencing disruption. We are encouraging all PhD students to record what they have or haven’t been able to achieve during the disruption in the Research Student Log. Evidence in the log can be used when considering future requests for flexibility when they reach their final year. More information about logging disruption to research is available on the Doctoral School website.

                                                                                        

2. Volunteering opportunities for staff

There’s a lot of interest in volunteering at the moment, as some of us have more time on our hands and are perhaps looking for different ways to help during the coronavirus pandemic.


Students’ Union UCL’s Volunteering Service have put together a guide for safe volunteering, featuring lots of opportunities for UCL staff, as well as students, to get involved, whilst observing social distancing requirements. The guide includes details of virtual volunteering and how to stay safe if you’re helping out in person.


Staff and students can also find coronavirus-related volunteering opportunities on the Students’ Union UCL website (UCL login needed).


3. Last chance to register for Grand Challenges: Covid-19 initiative

One of the most urgent questions now facing humanity is how we recover from Covid-19 and prepare to take the first steps toward a more resilient and equitable world.


UCL Grand Challenges invites you to engage with our new initiative to address the urgent issues posed by the coronavirus outbreak. We are seeking to rally the collective expertise, skills and ideas of the many UCL researchers from our full range of disciplines who are currently unable to continue their research as normal.


The deadline for expressions of interest is this coming Monday, 4 May 2020. To find out more and register, please visit the UCL Grand Challenges website.


4. UCL's life-saving breathing aid

Designs for the UCL-Ventura CPAP breathing aid were released online at no cost on 7 April to support the international fight against Covid-19. In the 24 days since they were published, the designs have been downloaded by over 1,800 teams in 105 countries, representing non-profits, manufacturers, health experts and research institutes. Our interactive world map displays where downloads have taken place. In addition, teams have locally manufactured prototypes for testing in Mexico, South Africa, Canada, Bulgaria and Russia.


The designs and manufacturing instructions can be downloaded and licensed for free through UCL Business' e-lucid platform. You can find out more about the international download process on the UCL Ventura website.


5. Communications & Marketing: CAM Plus network

On Wednesday, UCL Communications & Marketing held a Microsoft Teams meeting with over 230 people from the CAM Plus network. Using the interactive digital platform Slido, they were able to join a live Q&A with the Provost, as well as hearing an update on the UCL-Ventura CPAP device and the latest about student recruitment activities.


If communications or marketing is part of your role at UCL and you’re interested in joining the CAM Plus network, you can sign up via one of our four Communities of Practice (Student Recruitment, Digital Communications, Internal Communications and Events Management). Alternatively, visit the CAM Plus Portal and request access to the site by completing the required information.

8. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
