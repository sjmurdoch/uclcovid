1. Welcome back

We hope that you all had a restful and relaxing break over the Easter closure – welcome back.


As lockdown restrictions begin to ease in line with the Government’s roadmap, we are still asking all staff to continue working remotely where possible, in line with Government guidance. Please only come back to work on campus if you have been advised to do so by your manager.


A guide to what’s open on campus can be found on the UCL Students website.


2. Symptom free coronavirus testing

The Government have announced that access to free, regular, rapid coronavirus testing will be available to everyone in England from 9 April. We are encouraging people to use this service twice a week – find out how to get tested at a local centre or order a test to take at home on the NHS website.


Staff and students who need to be on campus can still get tested at UCL’s Bidborough House testing facility –find out more on UCL's coronavirus pages.


3. TFL travel advice

Government advice remains to keep journeys to a minimum. If you do need to travel by public transport, please check the Safer travel guidance on the TfL website for information about safety and hygiene, travelling at quieter times, tube and bus travel and service levels.


4. Supporting staff and students celebrating Ramadan

Today sees the start of Ramadan, the holiest month in the Muslim calendar. During this month, Muslims around the world refrain from eating and drinking, including water, during daylight hours, meaning that UCL staff observing the fast may benefit from flexibility in working patterns during the period. Please see UCL Staff News for advice on supporting Muslim staff and students during this period.


We have also been advised by the Department of Health and Social Care, who have consulted with the British Islamic Medical Association about COVID-19 testing during Ramadan. Their position is that LFT and PCR COVID tests do not invalidate the Ramadan fast. Find out more on the British Islamic Medical Association website.


5. Future of work survey – have your say

If you haven’t already, please complete the Future of Work pulse checker to let us know what has and hasn't worked about working from home, and what you would like to see in the future. This short, one-off survey contains seven questions – nearly 4,000 of you have already had your say, and you have until 18 April 2021 to give us your views.


6. Your Wellbeing: Complete this week's survey

Please complete Week 40 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


In March, April and May, there will be three prize draws with a prize of £50 in John Lewis vouchers, with winners picked from staff who have completed the Your Wellbeing survey every week – find out more about the prize draw on the UCL HR website.


7. Coronavirus: The Whole Story – Why should you get tested for COVID-19?

Two pillars underpin hope for a post-COVID future — vaccines and testing. This week, host Vivienne Parry OBE discusses the benefits and drawbacks of testing with guests Becky Witham, Sian Minett and Professor Irene Petersen, who have been instrumental in setting up testing programmes both here at UCL and across the country. Listen now on UCL Minds.
