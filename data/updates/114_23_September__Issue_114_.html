1. Wearing face coverings on campus

UCL’s face coverings policy has been updated to highlight the importance of wearing face coverings as part of a range of safety measures to ensure that staff and students are as safe as possible on campus:


  • All staff, students and visitors are expected to wear face coverings, if they can, when on campus, both inside buildings and when moving around UCL's outdoor spaces;
  • Face coverings must be worn if you are within 2 metres of someone else;
  • Most people should use their own face covering. UCL will provide face coverings where a risk assessment recommends it, and we will provide coverings with clear panels to teaching staff and student facing services;
  • If you are not able to wear a face covering, please use the Government's exemption badge, an exemption card from TFL or a Sunflower lanyard.


For more information about face coverings, please visit the Keeping Safe on Campus webpages.

                                                                                                                     

2. Teaching large online classes

If you are planning to teach groups of more than 300 people on Zoom, you will need to request a Zoom Large Meeting Licence by emailing ZoomSupport@ucl.ac.uk. We will respond to requests as quickly as possible, but please provide 48 hours’ notice. You will need to confirm the date, how many participants you are expecting, and whether it is a one-off or recurring event. We are putting arrangements in place with Zoom so staff won’t need to request these licences in the future. Thank you for your patience while we find a solution.


If you wish to use Blackboard Collaborate for more than 250 participants, please follow these instructions. For help choosing the right platform, find out more about planning a virtual event.

5. Key Travel student travel portal – reminder

UCL has extended its partnership with Key Travel to all students so they can book flights at competitive rates and benefit from additional baggage allowance and greater flexibility with cancellation or amendments. The service is available to all UCL students throughout their time at UCL, for all their journeys, including travelling home for the vacation periods for instance.


Once students have pre-enrolled or re-enrolled, they will receive an email from Key Travel to inform them of this service, and they can register using their UCL credentials.


More information can be found on the International Students webpages.

7. Today’s Teams Tip

This week sees a new feature now in preview in Teams: the ability to set up breakout rooms for larger meetings. The icon for breakout rooms can be found in the meeting control bar next to ‘raise hands’. You can use this function if you are the meeting organiser (if you are an attendee, you won’t see the icon).


Breakout rooms are a great way to facilitate smaller group discussions within a meeting. The organiser can assign participants to a separate room and call them back to the larger group when the breakout is complete. Please note: because this feature is still in preview, MS support is currently unavailable.


Top Tip: you can practice most of the meeting functionality in Teams – including breakout rooms – on your own without involving others.


Click ‘Calendar’ on the left rail in Teams, then in the top right, click ‘Meet Now’. Finally, click 'Join Now'. You will be in your own individual meeting space and can investigate the breakout rooms and other features here.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #TeamsTips. For more information, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, contact theIT Services Desk.
