1. UCL medical students joining the NHS frontline

More than 300 of UCL’s final year medical students are being fast-tracked through graduation this week, ready to be made frontline NHS doctors, as part of the Government’s response to the coronavirus outbreak.


Two hundred students are already volunteering and most of our graduates will now formally join the NHS. UCL is very proud of all of our staff and students on the frontline, and wishes you well in the coming days and weeks.


There is more information about the students volunteering to treat coronavirus patients on the UCL News website.


2. Cancelling pay deductions for March strike action

We recognise the huge efforts that UCL’s community is making to protect student learning and modify courses and assessments in response to the coronavirus outbreak and its impact on the university.


Everyone is demonstrating exceptional commitment to recover lost learning, and working hard to make sure students can continue their studies in the weeks and months ahead.


At this moment of national crisis, when so many people are worried about their future and their security, we want to acknowledge the significant additional effort being made and to help ease uncertainty. We have agreed to cancel the payroll deductions for all strike action taken by staff in March, in recognition of the incredible contribution that our people are making in these very challenging circumstances.

  • coronavirus-related absences
  • pay and benefits
  • advice for parents and carers
  • new regulations about visas and right to work checks
  • volunteering opportunities


4. GDPR and data protection

One of the challenges of working remotely is managing data securely, especially while we are getting used to unfamiliar software and finding new ways to connect to resources online.


UCL’s Data Protection Office, Information Security Group and the GDPR Programme have pulled together answers to the most frequently asked questions about data security. You can find them here on the UCL Data Protection website.


5. Accessibility online – update on Microsoft Teams captions

On Tuesday, we let you know that automatic captioning is available on Microsoft Teams. If you are using this function, you can now activate captions when starting a call from chat, and not just during a scheduled meeting.


More information about using live captions is available on the Microsoft Office website.


6. Online advice and engagement

  • Answers to frequently asked questions about support for staff have been updated on the main coronavirus webpage.
  • Yesterday there were 4,133 visits to the main coronavirus webpage and 551 visits to the askUCL landing page.

                   

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.


If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


7. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.


If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or visit our online support hub. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

          

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
