1. UCL Together and volunteering during the pandemic

As each week passes, many of us are finding new and different challenges coming our way. For some teams, this includes taking on more work or a reduction in available resource.

         

If you or your team are struggling to manage activities with the resource you currently have, you may be able to secure additional temporary resource via UCL Together.

        

We already have a number of volunteers from around UCL and we will do our best to match you as quickly as possible with someone with the right skills and availability. If we already have someone suitable, we’ll put you in contact with them straight away.

         

If you want to find out more or discuss if UCL Together will be a suitable solution, sign-up to one of our UCL Together Open sessions. These will take place on the following dates:

  

  • Friday 15 May, 11-11.30am
  • Monday 18 May, 3-3.30pm
  • Tuesday 19 May, 1-1.30pm
  • Wednesday 20 May, 10-10.30am

  

Email UCLTogether@ucl.ac.uk to let us know which session you would like to attend and you’ll receive an invite.

  

If you’re interested in volunteering during this time, HR Services have put together a list of resources, including answers to frequently asked questions, which we hope you’ll find helpful too.

        

2. Looking out for our students: Phone call campaign

Since we launched our new outreach campaign at the end of April, we’ve been joined by 70 members of staff who are calling students to help them feel cared for during the pandemic.

  

With so many volunteers, we’re now able to speak with hundreds of students each week, to check how they are coping, if they are in touch with family and friends, and whether they are able to exercise and look after themselves.

  

All colleagues are welcome to volunteer if they’re able to commit at least one day per week through May and June. Training and support will be provided. Please ask your line manager for approval to be released before you sign-up and contact Lina Kamenova, Deputy Director of Student Support and Wellbeing to register your interest.

  

Please encourage students to request a call through this online form.

  

More information about the campaign is available on the UCL Student News website.

        

3. UCL Political Science podcast

UCL’s Department of Political Science has launched a new podcast, featuring academics and practitioners talking about the politics and policy of COVID-19.

The first episode asks how the pandemic could change the way nations govern their economies and societies, and is available to listen now on the Department of Political Science website. You can also submit your own questions for the team to discuss in future episodes.  

        

4. Online advice and engagement

  • New FAQs about laptop loans for students have been added to the coronavirus information hub.
  • Yesterday there were 2,463 visits to the coronavirus information hub and 319 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


5. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
