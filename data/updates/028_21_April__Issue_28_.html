1. Learning Opportunities Fund for students

The Learning Opportunities Fund has reopened for applications from students whose learning was affected by the strike action that took place between 24 February and 20 March 2020.


Students whose learning was affected by the strike action can apply for grants of up to £500 each to buy extra resources to help replace lost learning time, e.g. purchasing new books, online classes or extra tutoring.


The deadline for applications is midnight on Monday 11 May, and we aim to notify all applicants of our decisions by Friday 5 June 2020. More information about the fund and how to apply is available on the Student News website.

                                                  

2. Core services to help departments and faculties

UCL provides a range of software and digital services for staff and students, with dedicated support available from ISD and Communications & Marketing. In almost all cases, options should be available through UCL that mean you will not need to buy licences or services directly from a supplier.


Please make sure you use these centrally supported services wherever possible so colleagues can help you make the most of these tools and resolve any issues you may come up against. By using our preferred suppliers, rather than making one-off purchases, you’re also helping UCL achieve better value for money.


A list of available software packages can be found on ISD’s Software Database. If you still need to make a one-off purchase, get in touch with the IT Purchasing team for advice; please note that these are unlikely to be approved except in exceptional circumstances.


If you are working on social media, digital advertising, virtual events or webinars, it’s also likely that we will have institutional licences or agreements with suppliers that you can benefit from. Please speak to your department marketing lead in the first instance, or email Julie Rolls, Head of Business Partnering in the Communications & Marketing team.


If you have a question or you’re looking to purchase another type of product or service, please visit the UCL Procurement Services website.


3. Remote, not Distant at UCL: Free meditation and wellbeing resources

UCL’s Covid-19 study, the UK’s largest study on adults’ wellbeing and mental health during the coronavirus outbreak, has shown that one in five people are experiencing severe stress related to the pandemic.


If you’re feeling worried, you might want to check out the Headspace meditation app, which has just released a series of free mindfulness exercises created especially to support people during this time of crisis. You can listen to the series, called “Weathering the Storm” now on the Headspace website.


Please also take a look at the free support and resources available to support your mental health and wellbeing on our Remote, not Distant webpages.


4. Online advice and engagement

  • Information about our new podcast, Coronavirus: The Whole Story, has been added to the coronavirus information hub.
  • Yesterday there were 2,048 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 488 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 325 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


5. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or visit our online support hub. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
