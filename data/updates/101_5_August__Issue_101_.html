1. No detriment principle for staff

Many colleagues are worried about the impact that COVID-19 has had on their performance at work (both academic and otherwise). We want to make sure that all members of staff are treated equitably, making necessary adjustments to compensate for the challenges caused by the pandemic.


We estimate that around 40% of employees have been caring for children and loved ones, while others will have been experiencing increased anxiety levels or won’t have been able to work as effectively as usual because of their remote working set-up. UCL leadership have therefore set out clearly how we are going to ensure that all this is taken into account in our No Detriment Principle for Staff.


The principle focuses on promotions rounds and appraisals, and describes how staff will be invited to explain their individual circumstances in order that decisions are based on all the facts.

                                               

2. Online workstation assessments: A reminder

To ensure you’re comfortable while you’re working remotely, UCL has asked all staff and postgraduate researchers to complete an online workstation assessment. This is designed to reduce the risks of ill-health associated with using display screen equipment (DSE).


Thanks to those of you who have already completed the assessment. If you haven’t yet done so, please do so as soon as you can – it doesn’t take very long.


To complete your assessment please follow the instructions at the Safety Services website. If you have any problems accessing your assessment, please email the riskNET Helpdesk.


3. Remote, not Distant at UCL: What’s new

This week, the Remote, not Distant at UCL Spotlight is on:


  • This month the Pearn Kandola Race & Wellbeing in Organisations webinar series will address topics such as race, wellbeing and performance, tackling unconscious bias with mindfulness, and why our social environment at work matters. These webinars are free and open to everyone.
  • UCL’s Employee Assistance Programme provider Care First is offering daily webinars on a range of wellbeing topics. Some previously recorded webinars, as well as useful articles and access to emotional and practical support, are available on the Care First website.

                                               

4. Today’s Teams Tip

Following your feedback, we’re now sending coronavirus updates twice a week, and the former “Team Tips Tuesday” will now appear on Wednesdays. Today’s tip is a new release feature that should help stop endless scrolling back through messages.


Top Tip: You can now search within a single channel or chat. Go to the chat or channel in question, press Ctrl + F (or Cmd + F on Mac), then type what you want to search for. Your results will be pulled from just that chat or channel.


This feature works for both one-to-one and group chats.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #teamstips. For further information about using Teams, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, please contact the IT Services Desk.
