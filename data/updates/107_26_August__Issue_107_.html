1. Using UCL offices as student study spaces

Social distancing put in place to ensure the safety of everyone on campus means that the number of student study spaces on campus will be much reduced compared with the 6,000 we had last year. This, coupled with strong growth in the number of postgraduate students applying to study with us and the exceptionally large first year undergraduate intake resulting from the last-minute government U-turn to using Centre Assessed Grades, means we expect pressure on study spaces to be very high.


Although we have explored a range of options to find a solution, this is proving challenging. Therefore in addition to securing more buildings and pop-up spaces, we are also releasing office space in Bidborough House and Torrington Place to provide additional student study space. From the early part of Term One, students will have access to the ground, first, second, third and fourth floors at Bidborough, and floors six–ten in Torrington Place.


With many staff working remotely, plus the bookable space on the fifth and sixth floors of Bidborough House and St Martin’s Le Grand, we believe we can accommodate all colleagues who need to return to work in the office. We will continue to monitor the situation, and please speak to your manager if you have any questions about your individual circumstances.


We have not taken this decision lightly and we recognise it will have a significant impact on staff. However, we hope that you agree that we need to put our students and their learning first at this time. By continuing to work remotely and flexibly, you are directly helping our students to thrive. We would like to thank you in advance for pulling together for the benefit of our student community and UCL as a whole, in what will be a most extraordinary year.


We would also like to remind colleagues working remotely that you can claim tax relief on expenses incurred and complete a DSE self-assessment to ensure you have the right equipment and workstation at home. You can find more guidance and support on the Remote, not Distant at UCL webpages.


With very best wishes,


Fiona Ryland, Chief Operating Officer

Professor Deborah Gill, Pro Vice Provost for Student Experience

                  

2. CAS numbers for students

We have updated our FAQs for Undergraduate and Postgraduate students waiting to receive their Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) numbers.


It is taking us longer than usual to process applications, due to high numbers of applications and the impact of the UK Government's recent action on A Level exam results.


We are aiming to issue all outstanding undergraduate CAS numbers to international undergraduate students holding an unconditional offer within the next week. We are asking all international undergraduate students holding a conditional offer to confirm the details we have on record are correct. Once they have responded and their offer is confirmed, we will issue their CAS numbers.


We are asking all international postgraduate unconditional offer holders to ensure they have updated their Tier 4 information so we can process their application as quickly as possible. We are asking international postgraduate conditional offer holders to be patient while we check their documents before we can issue an offer.


Please note that enrolment for new students is open until 16 October.


3. Zoom support for staff and students from 2 September

UCL has purchased a 12-month licence for Zoom, as it is currently the most reliable platform for access from China. UCL Zoom accounts will be supported by ISD from Wednesday 2 September for staff and students, alongside our existing recommended digital platforms such as Blackboard Collaborate and Microsoft Teams.


From 6pm on Tuesday 1 September, we will begin work on launching the UCL account and migrating existing accounts to the central UCL account. As a result, there will be some disruption to Zoom accounts and events during this time. Please see the Staff News website for more information, including guidance about choosing the right platform for online teaching and what to do if you have already registered for a Zoom account with your UCL email address.

5. Today's Teams Tip

You may have noticed some changes to the way your Teams looks of late. Microsoft has rolled out a new Meeting and Call experience in the last few weeks. They have moved things around and added some useful (and entertaining) features, with more on the way – we’ll pick up on some of these over the next few weeks.


Top Tip: adding a background in Teams is now a lot easier. Simply:


  • Save an image you want to use in your files
  • When you are about to join a meeting, select the background icon (small figure with diagonal lines across) next to the microphone icon. This will open the ‘Background settings’ pane on the right side of the screen
  • Click on the ‘+ Add new’ link at the top of this pane
  • Navigate to where you stored the picture in your files and click ‘open’.


Teams will make this your default meeting background until you choose to change it.


NB: If the image doesn’t upload, it might be because the file size or resolution isn’t high enough.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #TeamsTips. For further information about using Teams, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, please contact the IT Services Desk.
