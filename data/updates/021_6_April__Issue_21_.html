1. Disturbance allowance for key workers

With the majority of us now getting more used to working from home, itâ€™s easy to forget that some colleagues are still travelling in and working onsite. In recognition of the exceptional challenges they are facing and the additional disruption they may be dealing with, our onsite key workers will receive a disturbance allowance in addition to their usual pay. This allowance will be paid to those staff whose work has been identified as critical to take place onsite, and who have been issued with an onsite key worker letter, including clinical staff now working for the NHS, temporary staff and outsourced workers engaged exclusively on UCL work with Axis and Sodexo.


The allowance will be paid monthly at the following levels:

  • Â£350 for staff onsite for at least half the working days in the month
  • Â£175 for staff working onsite more than two days but less than half the working days in the month
  • Â£75 for staff working onsite for 1-2 days per month


In addition to the disturbance allowance, we are also making available a limited number of parking spaces to key workers onsite, and colleagues will be able to claim parking costs if a UCL space is not available. We are getting in touch directly with all of our onsite key workers with more details about both the disturbance allowance and how to apply for a parking space.


2. Remote, not Distant at UCL: Healthy homeworking

Professor Anna Cox (UCL Psychology & Language Sciences) and PhD researcher Dave Cook (UCL Anthropology) have written their top five tips to help achieve healthy homeworking during the coronavirus outbreak.


Drawing on research from the worlds of psychology and anthropology, they have looked at strategies for using digital technology, maintaining a work-life balance and protecting yourself from burnout, as well as specific suggestions for colleagues who are caring for children at home.


You can find more advice and support, including links to wellbeing resources and development opportunities, at Remote, not Distant at UCL.

4. Dissertation and research support

Understandably many students would have expected to visit our libraries in person as part of their research for dissertations or other projects. Weâ€™d like to reassure students and staff that our extensive online resources, together with virtual support provided by Library Services staff, are there to help them during this time.


The team has created a new Dissertations and Research Project guide, to help users access our online resources and connect students to staff if they need research advice. The guide includes:

  • Links to online skills content on literature searching, referencing and reference management  
  • Guidance on how to identify online resources and collections, including content from UCL, other libraries and open access resources
  • Ways to contact the Library Services team for help


We hope our whole community will use this resource, alongside our expanding catalogue of digital books, journals and databases, which are also discoverable via Explore.
