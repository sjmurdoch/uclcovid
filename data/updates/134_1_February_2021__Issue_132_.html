1. UCL teaching to remain online for Term 2

In response to the latest guidance from the UK Government, we are now planning for all teaching and other face-to-face activities to remain online for the remainder of Term Two, except for a small number of exempt courses.


A message to students from the Provost has been published on UCL Student News, and emailed to all students. UCL has also confirmed that a ‘no detriment’ package is being developed to support progression and graduation, and protect the value of a UCL degree.


2. UCL Supports: Advice, guidance and resources for all staff

Professor Sasha Roseneil, Pro-vice Provost (Equity and Inclusion) and Executive Dean of the Faculty of Social and Historical Sciences has recorded a message for staff highlighting how the latest lockdown is breaking down the boundaries between the professional and the personal, and how this calls for us all to scale-back our expectations of what can be achieved during this period. Good enough, is good enough, at this time: if you are struggling with balancing caring responsibilities and work, do what you can, whatever that looks like for you.


This message marks the launch of UCL Supports, an initiative from HR providing advice, guidance and resources to help staff through the difficult circumstances within which many of us are living and working. Reinforcing UCL’s people first approach, UCL Supports encourages all staff to consider carefully the expectations that we are placing on others, and ourselves, and to speak up if you are not getting the support you need.


You can also find resources to support you through the pandemic and working remotely on the Being Well at UCL webpages.


3. Free face masks for staff who need to commute to campus

Anyone who can work remotely at this time must do so. If you do need to be on campus, UCL will be providing free face masks to wear during your commute from Wednesday 3 February until Monday 8 March when we will review the approach in line with Government guidance. Masks will be available for collection from a number of locations in Bloomsbury. Staff working at sites further away from campus will be able to order masks for delivery. Find out how to collect your masks on the coronavirus information hub.


4. Asymptomatic COVID-19 testing for staff

Due to the current restrictions, anyone who can work remotely must do so. Staff who are required to work on-site, and who do not have COVID-19 symptoms, are expected to get tested twice a week.


Testing centre at Bidborough House

UCL staff can use their UCL UserID to book a test at UCL’s testing centre in Bidborough House, which is open on weekdays from 8.30am–3pm. The centre is part of the national testing programme for universities and uses rapid Lateral Flow Tests (LFTs), which typically provide results within two hours. Contracted staff that may not have UCL User IDs should contact their line manager for more information about how to book an appointment.


If you work outside of Bidborough House opening hours

Staff who are working on campus but who are unable to attend the Bidborough House testing centre due to their working hours can register to collect a PCR home test. If this applies to you, please speak to your line manager in the first instance.


If you're required to work at the Royal Free campus and are not part of the NHS testing scheme, you will have received information about UCL’s testing service by email. You can also check your local Council pages for access to community LFT testing, and the eligibility criteria that applies (for example, Camden Council lists four LFT testing sites).


Please contact uclcovidtesting@ucl.ac.uk if you have any queries.


If you have coronavirus symptoms, you must not come to campus or the testing centre at Bidborough House. Please follow these steps to find out what to do next and request a test.


5. Fieldwork update

In the 5 January update we wrote that researchers should review any current fieldwork arrangements (including in-person research) and verify whether fieldwork in the UK can be carried out remotely or should be suspended for the duration of the restrictions.


It's important to keep in mind that people in the UK should stay at home during the current restrictions. While university staff are considered critical workers, this is mainly for the purposes of being eligible to send their children to school. There has to be a ‘reasonable excuse’ for leaving home and researchers should determine how this affects them, other staff and participants in any fieldwork. Researchers should also take into account that anyone involved in fieldwork can be affected by being clinically vulnerable or having caring responsibilities.


In the exceptional cases where researchers may be proposing to conduct in-person fieldwork in the UK during the lockdown, they must carry out a fresh risk assessment and obtain approval for it beforehand. They must provide the approver with a justification for conducting in-person research during the current restrictions and how travel by staff or participants falls under the definition of ‘reasonable excuse’.  


For more guidance, please visit the research, teaching and risk assessment webpages.


6. Enrolment for Term 2 wellbeing courses

UCL Organisational Development and Workplace Health have launched a new series of bite-sized interactive webinars to help staff build resilience and manage their well-being. The courses are centrally funded and open to all staff, and include sessions on mental health, financial wellbeing, parenthood, coping with loss and more.


Enrol for these courses and find others on the Learning Opportunities for Staff webpage, and explore the full range of training and support available to staff on the Learning and Development webpage.


7. COVID-19 Career Support Scheme: Funding available

This scheme provides funding or other support to staff whose careers are at risk of being negatively affected by the pandemic due to equity and inclusion-related circumstances. It is open to all colleagues, including those in academic, research and professional services roles.


Applications for round 5 of the scheme are open and will close at 5pm on Thursday 4 February 2021. In light of the current lockdown, two further rounds have been added, and the final deadline for applications is 18 February 2021 (subject to funding) – find out more on the Equality, Diversity and Inclusion website.
