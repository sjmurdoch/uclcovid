1. Using face coverings on campus

Face coverings must be worn by all staff, students and visitors on campus when walking between or around buildings and where it is not possible to maintain 2 metre social distancing from others.


You can find more information on our Keeping Safe on Campus – FAQs for staff page. This includes a link to our face coverings policy, which has advice about what to do if you or a member of your team have difficulty using face coverings, or are prevented from communicating effectively by the use of face coverings. Colleagues or students with an invisible disability can also obtain a Sunflower lanyard to indicate that they may need extra help.


Face coverings are intended to protect against transmission of COVID-19 to others if you are an asymptomatic carrier, and should be used alongside social distancing, hand and respiratory hygiene. If you have symptoms of COVID-19 (cough, high temperature, loss of taste or smell) please do not come onto campus – you should isolate at home.

                                                                                                                      

2. Guidelines for student inductions in September

Student Support and Wellbeing have created new guidelines to support staff with the planning and delivery of welcome and induction events for new students in September. The guidelines include information on central Professional Services induction communications and events and online induction modules being developed by central teams, with further detail to come about scheduling, accessibility and inclusion, platforms and suggested activities.


You can also join a Student Induction forum for updates, questions and the exchange of best practice. Find out more on UCL Staff News.


3. Attracting the brightest and best postgraduate taught students

UCL’s largest ever postgraduate taught student recruitment campaign, launched last month, is aimed at recent graduates living in London and the South East. It covers a huge range of digital channels including Google, social media and online study platforms like Prospects and FindAMasters, to showcase what we have to offer and persuade prospective students to join us next year.


To date, the campaign has generated 1,000 leads across all Faculties, and we’ve seen expressions of interest in Master’s study rise by over 200% compared to the same time last year.


The campaign runs until 28 August – if you'd like to follow up the leads generated by the campaign and work on conversion activity to encourage interested students to apply and enrol, please contact your Faculty marketing team or representative.

6. Further advice and support

Daily coronavirus updates

These emails are sent every weekday to all UCL staff and postgraduate research students. They contain information about the pandemic and UCL’s response, including guidance about working remotely, policies and procedures, news about teaching and management arrangements and updates about UCL’s COVID-19 research.


If you would like to submit an item about the pandemic or UCL’s response to be included in the daily coronavirus update, please email internalcommunications@ucl.ac.uk.

                                                                                                             

Your Wellbeing weekly survey

The UCL Your Wellbeing check-in survey is a short, weekly survey for staff and postgraduate research students. The data will be used to find out how our community is coping with the huge changes caused by the coronavirus crisis and inform future improvements. Complete week 3 of the survey now (UCL login required).


Coronavirus information hub and research webpages

The COVID-19 research webpages showcase the work that UCL is carrying out on coronavirus, and contain information and resources for researchers.


Working remotely

Keeping safe on campus

The Keeping Safe on Campus website provides live updates about our returning to campus pilots, as well as safety and social distancing guidelines for those on site.

Your health and wellbeing

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

                                                     

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
