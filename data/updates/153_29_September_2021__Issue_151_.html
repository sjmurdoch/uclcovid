1. What to expect on campus

Please watch our new film about what you can expect on campus this term, which covers what you need to do before and once you arrive, and what UCL is doing to keep you safe including enhanced cleaning and making sure buildings are well ventilated. The common sense approaches to infection control of giving one another space and frequent handwashing remain vital.


You can also find a summary of key policies and guidance about working on campus on the coronavirus information hub.


2. Face coverings policy and guidance

UCL's face coverings policy has been updated with further guidance about their use in teaching spaces and support available for staff.


All staff, students and visitors are expected to wear face coverings whilst indoors in all UCL buildings including during classroom teaching, unless they are exempt. Please remind each other, students and visitors respectfully of the university policy, which can be found on the HR website.


If someone is not wearing a face covering, they may have a hidden disability or other exemption, and they do not need to provide evidence if this is the case.


We will continue to monitor our position on face coverings, informed by public health guidance, and review the policy again on 22 October 2021.

           

3. Reporting and testing

Everyone should get tested twice a week for COVID-19 and report your results to help reduce the spread of the virus.


4. Support and resources for hybrid working

Our Making Hybrid Work hub has been developed to support you with a range of services and advice. The hub has guidance on:


  • how to stay connected in a hybrid working environment;
  • advice for dealing with difficult situations;
  • tips for balancing your new routine;
  • and techniques to help managers support their teams.

Questions?

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager.

If it hasn’t, UCL staff can submit a question via our online form or email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699.
