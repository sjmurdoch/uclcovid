1. UCL's life-saving breathing aid

Yesterday we announced that all the details to make the UCL-Ventura (CPAP) breathing aid had been made available online at no cost, so it can be produced by manufacturers around the world.

 

Since the website went live yesterday, we have approved 233 download requests for the designs from 25 countries, spanning Europe, Africa, Asia, America and Australasia. This is the first step to helping thousands of coronavirus patients across the globe breathe more easily and stay out of intensive care.

 

More information about the UCL-Ventura and how to download the design is available on the UCL News website.

3. Staying safe online

Since the start of the coronavirus outbreak, there has been a significant increase in cyber-attacks on universities, with some reports showing an increase of 37% above previous levels. It seems that the more publicity an organisation receives around their efforts to tackle the pandemic, the more threats they receive. Our high profile work means that we are at high risk of attack.

 

ISD and the Information Security Group (ISG) are working hard to protect UCL, but we need every member of staff to play their part too.

 

Phishing emails are an extremely common way to break into an organisation. The UCL email system does scan attachments and websites linked to in emails for threats but, as new threats are continuously emerging, this is never going to be 100% effective. Please follow the following tips to help keep UCL and your work safe online:

 

  • Think twice before clicking on links or attachments â€“ especially if an email is trying to create a sense of urgency
  • If this is from a regular contact, does it sound like them? If in doubt, check with the sender before proceeding â€“ but remember not to use email to contact them, in case their account has been compromised
  • If you think you might have been phished, change your UCL password immediately and email isg@ucl.ac.uk or phone 020 7679 7338 (internal 37338) for help.

For more advice and tools to help you online, please visit ISDâ€™s Stay Secure webpages or email isg@ucl.ac.uk.
