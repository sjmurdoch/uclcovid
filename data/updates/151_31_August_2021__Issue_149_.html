1. Wearing face coverings on campus

Unless you are exempt, we expect everyone on campus to wear a face covering in indoor spaces, including in lectures, seminar rooms, study spaces, offices and labs.


By wearing a face covering inside, you are helping to reduce the potential spread of infection and protect others around you, including those who could become seriously ill if they catch COVID-19. Please be considerate of others and remember not everyone will feel comfortable telling people if they are at higher risk.


Please bring your own face covering with you to campus. If you have a hidden disability that means you are exempt, you can also pick up a Sunflower lanyard from campus or ask your department to collect one for you if you wish.


We are listening to staff who have raised concerns about wearing a face covering for a full day, which can be uncomfortable and make communication more difficult, and the issue was discussed by our Senior Management Team last week. However, we have determined that our current approach should be maintained, because it is an important way to help keep our community as safe as possible as larger numbers of staff and students return to campus. We will continue to monitor our position on face coverings, informed by public health guidance, and review the policy again on 22 October.


In the meantime, we encourage everyone on campus to take regular breaks to allow you to remove your face covering where possible.


UCL is currently trialling the provision of transparent face coverings for people who need them, and we will share more information when this pilot is complete. As part of our trial we are consulting with the deaf community; any deaf colleagues who would like to take part can get in touch with Pip Jackson, Access and Inclusion Manager.


More information can be found on our face coverings webpage.


2. New NHS vaccination centre opens at UCL

An NHS vaccination centre has been opened at UCL’s Bidborough House site on Mabledon Place, off Euston Road, giving local residents and students the ability to book an appointment in the heart of Camden.


The centre, set up in partnership with University College Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust (UCLH), is run by staff from UCLH and welcomes people who need their first or second COVID-19 vaccination. Eligible students and staff who live locally can book an appointment via the national booking system or by calling NHS 119.
