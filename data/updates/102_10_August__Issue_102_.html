1. UCL’s Intellectual Property Policy

UCL’s Intellectual Property Policy addresses ownership and use of intellectual property, including teaching materials. It states that UCL academics own the teaching materials that they create in the course of their normal duties, except in the case of certain categories of material, which includes film and sound recordings.

This policy was devised, revised and approved before the COVID-19 pandemic, and so some of you have had concerns related to the requirement to develop remote teaching materials, including film and sound recordings, for the academic year 2020/21.

In order to allay these concerns, UCL has committed, notwithstanding the position in the UCL Intellectual Property Policy, not to use teaching-related film and sound recordings created during academic years 2019/20 and 2020/21 without the originators’ consent.

                                                                

2. Emergency Assistance Fund for postgraduate research students

The UCL Student Funding office has resumed accepting applications for the Emergency Assistance Fund for postgraduate research students (EAF-PGR). The EAF-PGR provides support to UCL postgraduate research students from specific priority groups who are in financial need due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The closing date for receiving applications is 11 September 2020. Please share this information with your postgraduate students who may not receive the Coronavirus update emails.


3. The COVID-19 Wellbeing Study

The COVID-19 Wellbeing Study looks at perceived coercion and psychological wellbeing arising from the COVID-19 pandemic, in both the general population and key workers.

By exploring individuals' lived experiences and perceptions arising from the COVID-19 pandemic, the research team aim to understand how these experiences, and other background factors, have impacted on people’s perceptions of coercion and psychological wellbeing.

If you would like to participate in the study, or find out more information about the research and the team behind it, please visit their website.

5. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – What's happening to healthcare during the pandemic?

With many areas of healthcare threatened by the pandemic, we're exploring the impact on patients who haven't caught the virus, but who are sick with something else instead. In this week’s podcast episode, find out what is causing more people to die in the UK this year other than coronavirus, how cancer diagnosis and treatments are being managed, and how some neurologists are looking after patients online. Join Vivienne Parry as she speaks with Dr Amitava Banerjee, Professor Nick Ward and Professor Georgios Lyratzopoulos.


We hope you’ve enjoyed the first series of Coronavirus: The Whole Story. We’re taking a short break over the next few weeks and will be back at the end of August.
