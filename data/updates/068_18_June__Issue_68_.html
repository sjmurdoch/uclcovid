1. Support for moving education online

At the start of June, we let returning students and offer holders know that much of their teaching next term will be delivered remotely and enhanced with in-person activities on campus where it is possible to do this safely.


The Teaching and Learning Portal now has a comprehensive update on the support and guidance available to help staff to prepare to deliver programme content online. This includes the launch of our new staff development course Connected Learning Essentials, staff resources, tools and platforms.


Assessment planning and confirming your assessment modes

It is highly likely that we will be unable to deliver face-to-face invigilated exams next year, and students need clarity about how they will be assessed. We are developing our digital assessment capability to offer short, timed, online and un-invigilated exams. Guidance for online assessment and an assessment tariff will shortly be published on the Assessment planning pages of the Portal.


To comply with consumer legislation, we will need to update the Module Catalogue in early August, before students register for their modules. Staff need to indicate any changes to the assessment components they are going to offer by the deadline of 24 July. Find out how to confirm your assessment modes for 2020-21.


Campus and timetabling

We are acutely aware that departments need clarity about what can be delivered on campus and what the timetabling process will be for 2020/21. The challenges involved are significant. An Emergency Timetabling Policy has been approved by the Crisis Management Team and our Timetabling FAQs provide some further granularity about how the Temporary Operating Model will operate in practice. A revised Timetabling Process and timeline has also been developed. More information will be available on Monday. The Central Timetabling Team will be working closely with faculties and departments over the coming weeks.

2. Ways to improve your working environment

Most of you are currently working remotely and this may continue for an extended period of time. To ensure you can work comfortably, UCL is asking all staff and postgraduate researchers to complete an online workstation assessment. It doesn’t take very long to complete and is designed to reduce the risks of ill-health associated with using display screen equipment (DSE).


The assessment consists of a brief tutorial that will give you information about optimising your workstation. You’ll also be asked to tell us about your working environment such as your chair, screen, work surface and keyboard, including any problems you may be experiencing. Any issues you raise in the assessment will be forwarded to your line manager or supervisor, who will consider any actions necessary to make sure you can work as comfortably as possible. If you have completed an assessment on riskNET since 25 May 2020 then you do not need to repeat it now.


To complete your assessment please follow the instructions at the Safety Services website. If you have any problems accessing your assessment, please email the riskNET Helpdesk.

4. COVID-19 Social Hackathons

If you’d like to help a charity solve an issue that they’re facing in the wake of COVID-19, apply to take part in our Social Hackathons this July, organised by Students’ Union UCL’s Volunteering Service.


Working collaboratively and remotely with UCL staff members and students, you’ll offer your skills and knowledge to make a huge difference – all within one working day. Find out more on our social hackathon webpages. Social Hackathons are part of the UCL Listen & Respond initiative.


5. Further advice and support

Daily coronavirus updates

These emails are sent every weekday to all UCL staff and postgraduate research students. They contain information about the pandemic and UCL’s response, including guidance about working remotely, policies and procedures, news about teaching and management arrangements and updates about UCL’s COVID-19 research.

                                                                    

If you would like to submit an item about the pandemic or UCL’s response to be included in the daily coronavirus update, please email internalcommunications@ucl.ac.uk.


Coronavirus information hub and research webpages

The information hub at ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus is updated regularly with information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions about coronavirus as well as links to advice from other parts of UCL.


The COVID-19 research webpages showcase the work that UCL is carrying out on coronavirus, and contain information and resources for researchers.

                                                                    

Working remotely

The Remote, not Distant at UCL pages contain advice and resources to help staff adapt to remote working, including self-care and wellbeing, digital communication resources and guidance for managers.


Keeping safe on campus

The Keeping Safe on Campus website provides live updates about our returning to campus pilots, as well as safety and social distancing guidelines for those on site.


Health and Safety advice
The Working Safely During a Pandemic website from Safety Services provides guidance on how to manage the health and safety of staff at UCL during the pandemic. Topics include using display screen equipment, working with COVID-19 samples and risk assessments for returning to campus.


Your health and wellbeing

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.


If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.


Face coverings

You can find information on face coverings, including how to make your own, on the UCL Institute of Making website and the Public Health England website.


Questions?

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager in the first instance.


If you have a more general question, please check that it has not already been answered on the websites given above. If it hasn’t, members of UCL staff can submit a question about keeping safe on campus via our online form. Alternatively you can email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699. Lines are open 9am-5pm, Monday-Friday.
