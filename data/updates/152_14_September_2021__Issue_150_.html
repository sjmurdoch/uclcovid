Tuesday 14 September 2021

Coronavirus update: Issue 150

                                               

                                               

Dear colleagues,

               

From the start of term on 27 September, UCL expects all remaining staff to return to work from our buildings. We are committed to coming together again as safely as possible in person, to rebuild our community where students and staff can share experiences and learn from one another face-to-face. To support this, we anticipate that staff will work on campus for at least 40% of the time, but please discuss your individual circumstances with your line manager.

               

This message outlines UCL’s key COVID-19 policies and latest guidance to support staff, what campus will be like, and how to help protect yourself and each other. If you have a question that hasn’t been answered here, please visit UCL’s coronavirus information hub, where you can also find information that we are sharing with students before the start of term.

               

1. Before you come to campus

If you feel unwell, you must stay at home, whether or not you have COVID-19 symptoms.


Before you leave home, please download the free NHS COVID-19 app. The app tells you if you have been in close contact with someone who has tested positive, and lets you order tests and check into venues.


Health assessment tool

Managers should ask all their direct reports if they are worried about returning to campus because they are at higher risk of becoming seriously ill with COVID-19.


If an individual does have concerns, their manager can use the individual health assessment tool to consider their level of risk, put mitigations in place and refer them to Workplace Health if they need further support.


COVID-19 and flu vaccinations

The most important thing everyone can do to protect yourselves and others from COVID-19 is to get vaccinated as soon as you are eligible. Read our supporting statement on UCL’s HR webpages.

     

UCLH has opened an NHS COVID-19 vaccination centre at UCL’s Bidborough House for UCL students and eligible staff who live locally to get their first or second vaccination.


UCL has also made funding available for staff to get free flu vaccinations from Boots. Find out more and request your free voucher on the HR webpages.


2. What to expect on campus

Face coverings

Unless you are exempt, we expect everyone on campus to wear a face covering in indoor spaces, including in lectures, seminar rooms, study spaces, offices and labs.


By wearing a face covering inside, you are helping to reduce the potential spread of infection and protect others around you, including those who could become seriously ill if they catch COVID-19. Please be considerate of others and remember not everyone will feel comfortable telling people if they are at higher risk.


Although this is UCL’s position, the Department for Education has said that students can choose not to wear face coverings and they must not miss out on educational sessions if they do so.


Please bring your own face covering with you to campus. UCL is currently trialling the provision of transparent face coverings for people who need them. If you have a hidden disability that means you are exempt, you can also pick up a Sunflower lanyard from campus or ask your department to collect one for you.


We are listening to staff who have raised concerns about wearing a face covering for a full day, which can be uncomfortable and make communication more difficult, and we will review the face covering policy again on 22 October. In the meantime, we encourage everyone on campus to take regular breaks to allow you to remove your face covering where possible.


Read more in UCL’s face covering guidance.


Building ventilation

Colleagues in the Estates team are working hard behind the scenes to optimise ventilation and limit the spread of any COVID-19 infection:


  • In rooms with mechanical ventilation, air recirculation has been turned off and full fresh air is being supplied;
  • Estates services have brought forward all planned maintenance activities on equipment that introduce fresh air, ensuring that these are complete prior to the start of autumn term;
  • We meet regularly to review best practice and respond to changes in guidance and legislation;
  • Naturally ventilated spaces have been checked to ensure that they are functional;
  • Any concerns about ventilation in specific buildings can be flagged in local Risk Assessments and these will be passed to Estates to action;
  • We are trialing CO2 monitoring to find out whether this is a useful tool in assessing the effectiveness of our ventilation.

     

Read more about how UCL is reducing the risk of transmission through cleaning and building ventilation.


Campus signage

Colleagues in the Estates team and local departments are replacing signage across campus to reflect changes to UCL’s policies, including the removal of social distancing and one-way systems.


Designs for updated signage can be found on the coronavirus information hub.


Visitors on campus

The COVID-19 Visitor Policy is no longer in effect. Visitors will still need to sign-in when arriving onsite and should follow the same guidance as staff and students to help keep campus safe.


Meetings and events

We will be opening room bookings for ad-hoc activities such as meetings, collaborative sessions and social events from Monday 20 September.


Further guidance on room bookings and a new policy for events on campus is available on the Estates website.

                  

3. COVID-19 testing and reporting

Getting tested for COVID-19 and reporting your results (both positive and negative), or if you’ve been asked to self-isolate, helps to reduce the spread of the virus and protect people at higher risk of infection.


COVID-19 tests

Around one in three people infected with COVID-19 do not experience symptoms, which means they can still pass the virus on to others, including those who could become seriously unwell.


If you don’t have symptoms, you should get tested for COVID-19 twice a week. Anyone in England can order rapid lateral flow tests (LFTs) to be delivered to your home address and you’ll get your results in 30 minutes. Tests can be collected from pharmacies, the UCL Student Centre and Workplace Health (123 Grays Inn Road). There is also a small testing facility at the Student Centre.


If you do have symptoms, you must self-isolate immediately and order a polymerase chain reaction (PCR) test. These tests are checked by a lab and results take 1-3 days.


Self-isolating

The Government’s guidance around self-isolation has recently changed and there are some instances where, if you are a close contact of someone who has tested positive for COVID-19, you no longer have to self-isolate. More information can be found on the NHS website.


Reporting your test results

Every time you take a rapid lateral flow test (LFT), you should report your results to the NHS and UCL’s Connect to Protect online tool, to help the university monitor and respond to potential cases.


You should also use UCL Connect to Protect if you:

  • Have symptoms of coronavirus;
  • Need to self-isolate;
  • Have received your test results (LFT or PCR).

     

Find out more about Connect to Protect and how the team responds to potential cases amongst staff and students on the coronavirus information hub.


4. Further policies and support for staff

Resources for staff and managers

Following the positive feedback from colleagues about UCL’s Remote not Distant resources, we have developed our Interim People Management guidance to support staff and managers until at least the end of the calendar year.


Remote and hybrid working

UCL has developed a pilot Remote Working Policy and answers to frequently asked questions about hybrid working. These are designed to support existing staff guidance and policies about working remotely and on campus.


Help shape future UCL guidance and resources

UCL’s weekly People Pulse survey (formerly our Wellbeing survey) and the responses from colleagues across the university have guided UCL throughout the pandemic so far. We want to continue to listen to you and take into consideration your hopes and ambitions for the future – as well as any concerns you may have – as more of us return to working on campus.


It takes less than five minutes and we hope you will continue to complete the survey each week. Your responses will help the Provost and leadership team understand how staff are managing as the university transitions into this new model of hybrid working and we can all be together again.
