1. Support for parents and carers

We know that many of you will be affected by the Government’s closure of schools, or you may have additional caring responsibilities that make it harder for you to work. UCL is committed to supporting all our staff affected by these changes.


If this applies to you, please speak to your line manager about what options are available, such as changing your work routine, reducing the number of hours you are able to work or being placed on the Government’s furlough scheme or flexible (part-time) furlough. Anyone in these circumstances will continue to receive full pay.


Under the latest Government guidance, University staff are considered critical workers and are therefore eligible to send their children to school as usual. This is because we are being asked to keep universities open, and to continue to deliver learning (even if remotely). Please present your UCL staff pass as evidence. If the school requires further documentation a template letter is available for your manager to complete.


UCL’s Parents and Carers Together (PACT) network has an online community on Microsoft Teams, offering support and information for those balancing new or ongoing caring responsibilities with their work. You can find out more information and join up on the PACT website.


2. Information for staff working on campus

If you can work from home during this period, you must do so. However, campus will remain open and some colleagues will be required to work onsite, for example, staff in our estates and libraries teams, and with our cleaning, catering and security partners.


Staff who are considered clinically extremely vulnerable should work remotely and not come onto campus. Staff who are considered clinically vulnerable are strongly advised to follow the Government’s safety guidance, including washing your hands frequently, wearing a face covering when you’re around others, and staying 2 metres apart wherever possible. If you are clinically vulnerable and your role requires you to work on campus, please speak to your manager about what adjustments can be put in place to make sure you are as safe as possible.


Researchers and research students who require specialist facilities for their work can also return to campus. Please speak to your Head of Department about local arrangements for your department before you arrive, and ensure that you are aware of, and abide by, safety guidelines. Please note that researchers should review any current fieldwork arrangements and verify whether fieldwork in the UK can be carried out remotely or should be suspended for the duration of the restrictions. For more information please visit the research and teaching webpages.


If you are working onsite, please continue to follow the measures we have put in place to keep everyone on campus as safe as possible.


3. COVID-19 tests for UCL staff

UCL and contracted staff who are working on campus and who do not have COVID-19 symptoms are encouraged to get tested twice a week at UCL’s testing centre in Bidborough House. The centre is part of the national testing programme for universities and uses rapid Lateral Flow Tests (LFTs), which typically provide results within two hours. Testing is free and voluntary. UCL staff can use their UCL UserID to book their first test now. We will contact contracted staff with details about how to book theirs shortly.

Some library buildings and study spaces remain open, although services will be reduced. Please see the UCL Library Services website for details about what is available.


5. Staying safe and well

Whether you’re working remotely or coming onto campus, you must continue to follow the latest Government guidance, including:


  • Washing your hands frequently;
  • Wearing a face covering around others;
  • Staying at least 2 metres apart wherever possible.


Watch Professor Ivan Parkin’s film about the science behind hands, face, space, and how these measures help reduce the spread of infection.


You can find out more about UCL’s response to the pandemic on our Coronavirus information hub. If you’re working remotely, please also check our Remote, not Distant at UCL webpages for further resources and support.


6. Protecting UCL from cyber attacks

There has been a significant rise in targeted cyber attacks against universities around the world during the pandemic, and UCL’s high profile makes us particularly attractive to criminals.


Follow our five tips to help keep UCL and our work secure:

  1. Back up critical files, and store the backups in a physically separate and offline location from the originals;
  2. Always think twice before clicking on links or opening attachments;
  3. Keep your devices, apps and browsers patched and up to date and ensure your device is running Anti-Virus software;
  4. Protect your passwords, and use multi-factor authentication wherever possible;
  5. If it’s suspicious, report it! Tell the UCL Information Security Group at isg@ucl.ac.uk or call 0207 679 7338 (internal 37338).
