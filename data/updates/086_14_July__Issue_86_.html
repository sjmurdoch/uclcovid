Dear colleagues,


COVID-19 is presenting UCL, like the rest of the Higher Education sector, with huge financial challenges. The course of the pandemic remains uncertain, and it is difficult to predict how many students will choose to join us in London this autumn, and therefore what our income from student fees will be for the next academic year. I am clear that we need to approach this challenge from a people-first standpoint and take steps to protect the least well-off in our community as a priority.


With your support, we have already implemented a number of cost-saving measures to reduce expenditure and protect jobs. These have included introducing a recruitment freeze, placing staff on furlough leave and identifying ways to improve procurement across the university.


In addition to these changes, Council has now confirmed three new measures to reduce UCL’s payroll expenditure. First, we have taken the difficult decision to postpone any performance-related pay awards for grade 10 staff until August 2021. I appreciate that our senior colleagues are making an extraordinary contribution to UCL during the pandemic, and this decision was not taken lightly. However, this decision allows us to prioritise intergenerational fairness in a very difficult time and enables us to protect our staff at lower grades, who will not be affected by this.


Later this month we will also launch a Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) for staff aged 66 and over. I must stress that this scheme is entirely voluntary, but it is intended to provide staff who may already be considering retirement with an additional payment in recognition of the contribution they have made to UCL. This scheme allows us to plan for a future reduction in salary costs so that we are able to preserve the jobs of those at earlier stages in their careers, if we do find ourselves in very difficult financial circumstances next year.


Finally, it’s clear that the pandemic is bringing additional pressures for some, and has prompted others to rethink their priorities. We are therefore inviting all colleagues who wish to request a temporary change to their working hours to explore this with their teams: whether to take unpaid leave, pursue a career break, or make the switch to part-time working on a temporary or permanent basis. I hope that this will give those who need it most the flexibility they need to respond to changing circumstances. Again, it is entirely voluntary – but if you think you would benefit then I would encourage you to discuss further with your line manager. HR will be supporting managers to review and respond to requests.


We have agreed these proposals with Council to ensure that we are taking prudent steps and working with our community as far as we can to support people.


I would like to thank all of you for your continued efforts to reduce expenditure across the institution. Through your dedication, ingenuity and resourcefulness, I am confident that UCL will withstand the challenges facing us and emerge stronger and more resilient than before.


With very best wishes,


Professor Michael Arthur

UCL President and Provost

1. Voluntary early retirement and temporary unpaid leave, career break and part-time working options


Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS)

This scheme is for staff aged 66 and over. The aim is to reduce the amount spent on staffing while allowing eligible staff who may be considering retirement to do so with an additional payment.


This is a temporary and purely voluntary scheme that is open now and will end on 31 October 2020. To be eligible, staff must be aged 66 or over on 1 October 2020. More information about the scheme including the complete eligibility criteria can be found on the HR website.


Voluntary temporary unpaid leave, career break and part-time working options

Staff who wish to explore these options can find more on the HR website, including the criteria for staff who wish to apply. Please do discuss your situation and the various options available with your line manager before making an application. 


Applications for a temporary career break or temporary reduction in hours will not affect the longer term viability of a post – the scheme is intended to make short-term savings but will not influence the position of the substantive post in organisational structures. Staff can also combine any move to part-time working hours with flexible retirement options within the USS scheme.


2. Teams Tips Tuesday

Today is Teams Tips Tuesday, with hints and ideas from ISD. This week, we have another etiquette tip plus a new feature announcement.


A lot of people are finding that in large, presenter-led meetings, the chat panel can become distracting and pull focus from the person talking – a bit like whispering in the back row. Chat can’t be disabled for regular Teams meetings, so it’s worth telling your audience how you’d like to use it at the start. When should questions be asked – during the presentation or at the end? Can anyone reply to the questions or should they wait until you’re finished so you can respond directly?


Top Tip: we’ve found best practice is to have someone independent of the presenter monitor the chat, keep track of questions and then ask the presenter during agreed breaks in the meeting.


For very large meetings or events, especially if they involve participants external to UCL, you can use Teams Live for Events instead. With Teams Live, only the pre-invited presenters have video and audio feeds available and chat can be disabled before the meeting starts. An additional tool like Slido or Mentimeter can be used for capturing Q&A or interactive polling, although again we recommend having a separate administrator on hand to monitor traffic.


New feature: you can now download an attendance report for your Teams meeting, which is really helpful for larger meetings – but you have to download whilst the meeting is in progress. You can find out more details about this new feature on the Teams SharePoint site.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #teamstipstuesday. For further information about using Teams, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, please contact the IT Services Desk.

Your health and wellbeing
