1. Changes to extenuating circumstances

With exams and assessment starting soon, changes have been made to UCL’s Extenuating Circumstances process, to reduce any burden on our students. Students can now self-certify and no longer need to provide evidence when applying.


More information can be found on the Academic Manual website.


2. Your wellbeing during the lockdown period

As week six of lockdown continues, it’s more important than ever to take care of our mental and physical wellbeing. Please take a look at Remote, not Distant at UCL, where you can find support and resources for mental, emotional and physical health, including:


  • 10 Minute Mind, an online meditation programme
  • Confidential counselling services from Care First
  • SilverCloud, an online Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) programme
  • Resources to help you stay active, including online workouts and some simple exercises that you can do at home.


Please do also remember that, if you feel unwell (for reasons unrelated to Covid-19), you should still take time to rest and fully recover just as you would under normal circumstances. If you feel that you are too ill to ‘come to work’ then you should take sick leave as usual, following the procedure set out in the UCL Sickness Absence Policy.


You can find out more about UCL’s leave and absences policies related to coronavirus on the HR website.


3. Claiming tax relief on expenses incurred whilst working remotely

Some of you have asked if you can claim tax relief on expenses incurred whilst working at home (for example, heating and lighting).


If UCL requires you to work at home, you can claim for tax relief on a flat rate of £4 per week for the tax year 2019/2020 and £6 per week for the tax year 2020/2021.


HMRC will consider claims from employees working at home due to coronavirus measures if your usual workplace is closed. You can claim more than the flat rate if your costs are higher but you will be asked to provide evidence of these higher costs.


You can find out more, including guidance on how to make a claim to HMRC, on the HR website (under the “Expenses” heading).


4. Online advice and engagement


  • Yesterday there were 1,334 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 238 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 335 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


5. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
