1. COVID-19 mitigation package for 2020-21 assessment

UCL has today published details of its COVID-19 mitigation measures to support our students through their assessment in 2020-21. We have worked with Students’ Union UCL to develop a ‘no detriment’ package to make sure our students are not disadvantaged by the pandemic.


Find out what we’re saying to students on UCL Student News and read further notes for staff on the Teaching and Learning Portal.


2. Asymptomatic COVID-19 testing: Self-isolating after a positive Lateral Flow Test

Symptom-free staff and students on campus are expected to get tested regularly at UCL’s Lateral Flow Test (LFT) centre at Bidborough House. In line with new Government guidance, anyone who has a positive LFT result no longer needs to take a confirmatory Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) test afterwards. You and everyone in your household must self-isolate from the day you get the positive LFT result until you are no longer infectious.


Find out more about how to get a COVID-19 test at UCL, including information for those who can’t attend the Bidborough House centre, on our coronavirus webpages.


3. Free face masks now available for those using public transport

At this time, anyone who can work remotely must do so. If your role requires you to work onsite or your research requires specialist facilities on campus, UCL is providing free face masks to wear while using public transport until Monday 8 March when we will review the approach in line with Government guidance.


Masks are available for collection from a number of locations in Bloomsbury. Staff and postgraduate research students at sites further away from campus will be able to order masks for delivery. Find out how to collect your masks on the coronavirus information hub.


4. COVID-19 Career Support Scheme: Funding available

This scheme provides funding or other support to staff whose careers are at risk of being negatively affected by the pandemic due to equity and inclusion-related circumstances. It is open to all colleagues, including those in academic, research and professional services roles.


Applications for the final round of the scheme are open and will close at 5pm on Thursday 18 February 2021 – find out more on the Equality, Diversity and Inclusion website.


5. Upcoming PACT webinars

The UCL Parents and Carers Together (PACT) network have two upcoming webinars:


  • Friday 12 February (12.00–12.45): Leave provision and furlough support at UCL, which will outline the different types of leave options available to staff members, parents and carers.
  • Wednesday 24 February (13.00–13.45): Managing arguments with children – nothing but the truth, which will present three case studies about managing and responding to arguments with children and young adults.


6. Your Wellbeing: Complete this week's survey

Please complete Week 32 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


Over the next 12 weeks, there will be three prize draws with a prize of £50 in John Lewis vouchers, with winners picked from staff who have completed the Your Wellbeing survey every week – find out more about the prize draw on the UCL HR website.


7. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – is public transport safe?

This week, join host Vivienne Parry as she speaks to experts from UCL Primary Care and Population Health, the UCL Institute of Health Informatics and the UCL Medical School to talk about public transport. In this episode, we find out if it's safe to travel, how to protect ourselves when travelling, and hear more from our experts about what we need to know when making longer journeys.
