1. UCL Together

Four weeks ago we launched UCL Together to help address some of the workload imbalances created as a result of the coronavirus outbreak. To date, we have provided 497 days of additional support to teams across the university by matching volunteers to those departments who have requested help.


We would like to say a huge thank you to everyone who has given up their time to support the UCL community so far, and we’re still looking for volunteers and departments in need of extra help. If you’d like to get involved:


  • Leaders: To request additional temporary help for your team (up until 30 June), submit your request through UCL Together. We’ll work to find you a volunteer with the right skills as quickly as possible.
  • Volunteers: Please complete a Volunteer inside UCL or Volunteer outside UCL form through UCL Together. Don’t forget to speak to your manager first.


If you have a question about UCL Together, please contact UCLtogether@ucl.ac.uk.

                                                               

2. Laboratory safety guidance

The UCL Genetic Modification & Biosafety Committee has approved new guidance for those planning to work with samples from confirmed or suspected coronavirus patients, or those planning to work with the virus directly.


Please make sure you consult this document before preparing any research proposals. It's important there is a consistent approach across UCL and that all laboratory research with patient samples or viral cultures is carried out to the same high standard.


More information about safety for research, teaching and engagement is available on the UCL Safety Services website.


3. Marking religious festivals

UCL is aware of the impact that the coronavirus pandemic is having on religious festivals and holy days around the world and your ability, from home, to observe traditional dates in the year.  


Ramadan 2020 begins this evening, Thursday 23 April, and lasts until the evening of Saturday 23 May. It is the holiest month of the Muslim calendar, and marks the time of the revelation of the Qur’an. During the month of Ramadan, Muslims around the world fast during daylight hours, meaning that UCL staff observing the fast may benefit from flexibility in working patterns during the period. Please read UCL’s guidance on supporting Muslim staff during Ramadan for more information.


Like other religious festivals, Ramadan is affected by the coronavirus restrictions that are curtailing normal congregational worship. In response, the Muslim Council of Britain has published #RamadanAtHome guidance, including advice on working from home during Ramadan, which you can view on their website.


More information, including ways to support your colleagues, is available on UCL’s Religion and Belief Equality webpages.


Ramadan Mubarak to all of our Muslim staff and students at UCL.


4. Over 3 million UCL Press downloads

UCL Press book and journals have now surpassed 3 million downloads, with a global readership from over 230 countries and territories. We’d like to say a huge thank you to all of our authors, editors and readers who’ve helped us reach this milestone.


Since launching in 2015 as the UK’s first fully open access university press, it has published over 140 academic books, including monographs, edited collections and textbooks.


Explore the full range of publications available to download on the UCL Press website.


5. Online advice and engagement

  • Answers to frequently asked questions about UCL’s support for postgraduate research students have been added to the coronavirus information hub.
  • Yesterday there were 1,760 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 411 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 338 visits to the askUCL landing page.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or visit our online support hub. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
