1. Moving teaching online for the rest of Term 1

Just as numbers of COVID-19 cases are rising throughout the UK, including the new Omicron variant, so too are they on campus.  


Whilst campus remains open for face-to-face teaching and other activities until term ends on Friday, you may move your teaching online for the remainder of this week. If this is appropriate for your programme, and following consultation with your Head of Department, please notify your students as soon as possible. There is no requirement to move teaching online if you consider that face-to-face teaching can continue. We are also sharing an update with all students today.


In line with guidance from the Department for Education, we intend for campus to be fully open for face-to-face teaching for the start of Term 2. We are keeping this guidance under review and will be in a better position to update you next week about what to expect in January.


2. Vaccinations and boosters

Please get your COVID-19 vaccination or booster dose if you can. Recent data shows that having a booster increases protection against the Omicron variant.


Following the Prime Minister’s announcement on Sunday, everyone aged 18 and over in England will have the chance to get a booster before the New Year.  


People aged 30 and over can already get a booster dose. Booster appointments for those aged 18-29 will be available to book from Wednesday 15 December; this service is not yet available at all walk-in vaccination sites. Find out how to book a vaccine and your nearest vaccination centre on the NHS website.


The NHS vaccination centre at Bidborough House is open 9am–6pm daily. The centre may temporarily suspend walk-in vaccinations at busy times, and you are recommended to make an appointment if possible.


3. Working remotely

From today (Monday 13 December), the UK government has advised that office workers should work from home if they can, with exemptions for Higher Education staff.  


At UCL, we expect colleagues who need to be on campus to deliver and support face-to-face teaching, research and other student-facing services to continue working onsite (unless you are moving your teaching online, see above). All other office workers at UCL who can work remotely should do so from today.  


Please remember that you must not come onto campus if you feel unwell (even if you do not have COVID-19 symptoms) or you have been asked to self-isolate.


4. COVID-19 testing

Getting tested for COVID-19 is crucial to reducing the spread of infection.  


  • If you don’t have symptoms, you should get tested at least twice a week with a free Lateral Flow Test (LFT), and before mixing with people in crowded indoor places. If you cannot order LFT kits for delivery due to high demand, these are available to collect from the Student Centre and Workplace Health.
  • If you do have symptoms or you have received a positive LFT result, you should self-isolate immediately and get tested with a free Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test.


Whether you have taken an LFT or PCR, please report your negative or positive test results to the NHS and UCL Connect to Protect.


5. Extra closure day: 23 December

UCL's winter closure has been extended by one day to include Thursday, 23 December 2021 in recognition of our community's hard work over a difficult year, and will now run from 5.30pm, Wednesday 22 December 2021 to 9am, Tuesday 4 January 2022.


Before you finish for the winter break, please inform security of any out-of-hours access requirements and potentially hazardous operations, and ensure that you have closed down your workstation.


Find out more about our closure dates including deadlines, key contacts, and library and café services opening times on Staff News.
