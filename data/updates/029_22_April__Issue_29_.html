1. Remote, not Distant at UCL: Supporting each other

We are incredibly proud of the way our colleagues and our students continue to support each other during the coronavirus outbreak.


These are challenging times and many of us are under extraordinary pressure at work and at home. Those around us may be feeling unwell or worried about loved ones, and sadly some of our colleagues may have experienced a bereavement.


If you are worried about your own wellbeing, please speak to your line manager or access Care First, our free Employee Assistance Programme. You might also find it helpful to look at the free support and resources available for staff.


If you’re concerned about someone you work with, please consider checking in with them to see how they are feeling. It can make a big difference to know that someone is looking out for you.


For practical tips for adapting to this new way of working, including strategies for managers to support their teams, please visit the Remote, not Distant at UCL webpages.

                                                       

2. Bespoke backgrounds for Microsoft Teams

It’s now possible to upload your own background image on Microsoft Teams video calls. If you’re meeting with people from outside UCL or making an online presentation, please use one of our official images of the university campus. There are lots of photos on Imagestore to pick from. We’ve also created a range of UCL branded backgrounds ready for you to use. You can find them in the Brand Resources area.


Before you start you’ll need:


  • Your chosen image saved onto your computer in JPEG format. The dimensions should be 1920 x 1080 pixels with a resolution of 72dpi.
  • To have downloaded the Microsoft Teams app. The browser version of Teams does not currently have this functionality.


Then follow these instructions to upload the image:


  • Windows: Just copy your custom background into the Uploads folder here, where [USER] is your UCL user ID: C:\Users\[USER]\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Teams\Backgrounds\Uploads
  • macOS: Select “Connect To Folder” from the Go menu in the Finder. Then copy and paste this file directory address and press “Go”: ~/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Teams/Backgrounds/Uploads The “Uploads” folder will then appear. You might be able to navigate to this folder manually, but often the user Library folder is hidden.


Colleagues in ISD are working on a more permanent solution where images will be available to select directly on the Teams platform, but for now we hope this will bring our UCL campus a little closer while we’re working remotely.


3. UCL entrepreneur offers virtual haircuts to raise money for the NHS

A UCL entrepreneur has launched a new website offering £15 video appointments with professional barbers during the coronavirus lockdown.


UCL alumnus Daniel Silvertown and his cofounder Jeroen Sibia created Lockdown Haircut, where anyone with their own electric clippers, comb and webcam can book a 20 minute video appointment with a professional barber.


Consultations can be booked online and the money is split between the barber and the NHS. Barbers can also choose to donate their portion and customers can add an extra £5 to go towards the NHS.


Find out how UCL can support staff, students and alumni interested in starting their own venture on the Innovation & Enterprise website.


4. Online advice and engagement

  • Answers to frequently asked questions about student accommodation have been added to the coronavirus information hub.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


5. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or visit our online support hub. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
