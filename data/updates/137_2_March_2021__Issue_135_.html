1. Return to Campus Roadshows

We are holding a series of five Return to Campus Roadshows following the Government’s announcement of the phased easing of lockdown and what this means for more activity on campus,.


The roadshows will give you the chance to hear about UCL’s response to the roadmap and what this means for the university. The panel will share plans about our practical and safe return to campus, your well-being and what we are doing to prepare for the next phase of the pandemic. You’ll also be able to put your questions to the panel in an ‘ask anything’ format.


Find out more and register:


  • BEAMS, Chaired by Professor Nigel Titchener-Hooker: Thursday 25 March, 10:00–12:00: Register for BEAMS
  • SLASH, Chaired by Professor Piet Eeckhout: Monday 26 April, 11:00–13:00: Register for SLASH
  • IOE, Chaired by Professor Sue Rogers: Thursday 6 May, 10:00–12:00: Register for IOE
  • Professional Services, Chaired by Fiona Ryland: Thursday 13 May, 10:00–12:00: Register for Professional Services
  • SLMS, Chaired by Professor Dame Anne Johnson: Thursday 27 May, 11:00–13:00: Register for SLMS


Please note that the events will be recorded.


2. Students return to campus

As part of the Government’s four-step roadmap to ease lockdown restrictions, from 8 March 2021 UCL can resume in-person teaching for students on practical or practice-based courses and who require specialist equipment.


Information for students about the roadmap, including advice about what happens next and what students should do, has been published on Student News.


Students who are eligible to return to campus for face-to-face teaching are being contacted directly through their programmes.


3. PACT event: Autism and COVID-19

Speakers from the Institute of Education will discuss autism, COVID-19 and the experiences and needs of autistic people, their parents and carers on Friday 12 March, 2-3pm. Find out more and sign up via Eventbrite.


4. Your Wellbeing: Complete this week's survey

Please complete Week 35 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


In March, April and May, there will be three prize draws with a prize of £50 in John Lewis vouchers, with winners picked from staff who have completed the Your Wellbeing survey every week – find out more about the prize draw on the UCL HR website.


5. Made At UCL Podcast – Voice

Coronavirus: The Whole Story is taking a break this week, but the latest episode of our monthly #MadeAtUCL podcast has just been published. Themed around Voice, it includes a story of three medical students who are sharing their experience of providing and receiving healthcare during the pandemic through poetry. Listen now on Made At UCL.

Questions?                          

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager.


If you have a more general question, please check that it has not already been answered on the websites above. If it hasn’t, UCL staff can submit a question via our online form or email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699. Lines are open 9am–5pm, Monday–Friday.


If you would like to submit an item about the pandemic or UCL’s response to be included in the coronavirus update, please email internalcommunications@ucl.ac.uk.
