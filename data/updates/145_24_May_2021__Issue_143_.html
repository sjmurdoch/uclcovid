1. Results from the Future of Work survey

Thank you for contributing to the survey; just over a third of staff took part to share your experiences of lockdown and how you would like spaces and ways of working to change in the future.  


Lockdown has been challenging. People are missing the atmosphere on campus and many have found it difficult to build relationships with colleagues or have experienced practical issues with working from home. However, more than half of staff reported an overall improvement in their work-life blend and colleagues said they enjoy commuting less and the positive financial impact. The majority of staff who completed the survey said they want more flexibility around working hours and arrangements to work from home in the future.


These results allow us to use what we have learned from working remotely – the good and the bad – to improve how we work when we are all able to come back together again.


You can see a summary of the key insights from the survey and a detailed breakdown of the responses we received on the HR website.


2. Plans for welcoming more staff back to campus

UCL is developing our roadmap for all remaining staff to return to campus, taking into account the feedback we have received through the Future of Work survey, the latest advice from the UK Government and UCL’s Public Health Advisory Panel.


Timeline

We are planning to welcome back remaining staff in two phases:

  • 21 June–September 2021: Staff who have been working remotely may return to work on campus, but we do not expect everyone back at this stage. If you wish to work on campus, please agree this with your line manager.
  • September 2021 onwards: Subject to Government guidance, we expect all remaining staff to return to work on campus as their default workplace for at least part of the time (please see notes on flexible working below).


Flexible working

We are seeking to balance colleagues’ requests for greater flexibility with the value of all being together in the same place. This will be difficult to get right: we want to make sure individuals and teams have the freedom to adapt their working arrangements where possible to suit their personal circumstances and local needs, while also maintaining a vibrant sense of community on campus.


Reflecting the responses to the Future of Work survey, and with a view to maintaining our sense of community on campus, we will typically expect all colleagues to spend a minimum of 40% of their working time onsite from September. The exact proportion for each individual will depend on their role and the needs of their department (e.g. whether their role can be performed remotely for all or part of the time), but this should provide a starting point for conversations between staff and their line managers.


Working onsite

Some staff will be required to work wholly onsite, and others will prefer to continue working in this way, even if their role means it’s possible for them to work remotely for part of the time. Assuming Government guidance does not change, UCL will not require any staff to work remotely, and all colleagues may access a place to work onsite at any time during their working hours. Please speak to your line manager if you need help accessing space on campus.


  • Read the full Returning to UCL Campus – Interim People Management Guidance on the HR webpages.


We will give you as much certainty as possible, while remaining flexible so we can respond to the latest Government advice and feedback from colleagues. We will share more information with you as soon as we can.
