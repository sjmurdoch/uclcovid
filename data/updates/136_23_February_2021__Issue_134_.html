1. New Government roadmap to ease lockdown restrictions

Last night the Prime Minister announced a four-step roadmap to ease lockdown restrictions in England, including guidance for the Higher Education sector.


Universities can resume in-person teaching for students on practical or practice-based courses and who require specialist equipment and facilities from 8 March. Colleagues are now working to confirm which programmes can restart face-to-face teaching and we will share more detailed plans with staff next week, as well as guidance for students.


For now all staff who can work remotely should continue to do so and those who are required to work onsite should get tested for COVID-19 twice a week. Updated information on testing, including guidance on what to do if you have tested positive in the last 90 days, is available on the UCL website.


2. Arriving in the UK: Guidance for students

Information about preparing to arrive in the UK for students has been updated on the UCL Students website. The pages include information about recent changes in COVID-19 rules, including:


  • Tests for all people arriving in the UK
  • Hotel quarantines for those arriving from red-list countries
  • Travel bans to the UK – which countries
  • Fines or prison sentences for concealing where you’ve travelled from


3. Changes to UCL's senior team

UCL’s President & Provost Dr Michael Spence has reviewed the portfolios of the current senior team, to ensure we have the right capacity and balance of expertise for the next phase of UCL's strategic development.


UCL’s Council has agreed proposals to adjust the Vice-Provost portfolios and introduce a number of Vice-President roles, with the changes taking effect from 1 April 2021. The new roles will be key enablers for the next phase of UCL’s development. Find out more about the context for these changes and a message from the Provost on the UCL website.


4. New resources and webinars to support you at work

A suite of new resources are available from HR to help you organise your work, manage meetings effectively and develop your leadership and people management skills. Find out more:


  • Working Smarter: A new series of self-paced resources and interactive webinars from UCL’s Learning Academy to help you feel more in control of your work, reduce anxiety and help achieve a healthy work-life blend. Find out more on UCL Staff News.
  • Making Meetings Matter: Guidance around effectively organising and managing virtual meetings so that everyone gets the most out of them. Find out more on the Remote, not Distant at UCL website.
  • Lead at UCL: A new bespoke, online learning platform for developing your leadership and people management skills for UCL’s academic, research, teaching, professional services and technical leaders and managers. Find out more on the HR website.


5. Your Wellbeing: Complete this week's survey

Please complete Week 34 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


In March, April and May, there will be three prize draws with a prize of £50 in John Lewis vouchers, with winners picked from staff who have completed the Your Wellbeing survey every week – find out more about the prize draw on the UCL HR website.


6. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – can we have a summer holiday this year?

In this week’s episode of the podcast, host Vivienne Parry speaks to three UCL experts to try and get to the bottom of Boris Johnson’s “roadmap out of lockdown”, what it might mean for international travel during the pandemic, and what we can expect in the future.
