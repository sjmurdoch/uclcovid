1. COVID-19 vaccines: UCL Public Health experts on how they work and why they are safe

UCL public health experts Professor Ibrahim Abubakar (Professor of Infectious Disease Epidemiology) and Professor Robert Aldridge (Professor of Public Health Data Science), along with Dr Claire Elliott, GP at Ridgmount Practice, answer some questions about the COVID-19 vaccine in two new videos.


UCL supports the rollout of the COVID-19 vaccines. Find out more about the vaccines and how to book yours on the NHS website.


2. Support your new ways of working

The Organisational Development team are hosting The Supporting Change programme: a series of change management focused workshops for managers. These will provide tools and techniques to support teams through the transition to new ways of working as more of us return to campus.


The training will be held over a series of three workshops and will be hosted on Zoom. Find out more and sign up here. Registration closes Tuesday 29 June.


3. Fieldwork update

Researchers may be planning to resume fieldwork after the planned easing of lockdown restrictions on Monday 19 July.


Researchers are reminded that they should continue to plan and manage their fieldwork in line with UCL’s current guidance, to help ensure that fieldwork is safe for staff, students, and participants. Please be aware that the guidance may change as the situation evolves and researchers are encouraged to check it regularly.


Researchers should also keep in mind that the UK’s devolved administrations are easing restrictions at different speeds and what may be permitted in England may not be the case elsewhere. Restrictions on travel outside the UK remain in place and can change at short notice.


Guidance on fieldwork is available from the research, teaching and risk assessment webpages.

6. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – Latest episode and finale event

Host Vivienne Parry talks to Professor Francois Balloux (Chair in Computational Systems Biology and Chair of the UCL Genetics Institute) and Dr Keri Wong (Assistant Professor in Psychology in the Department of Psychology and Human Development at UCL IOE) about the lockdown extension, a potential third wave in the UK and what this means for us mentally and physically. Catch up now on UCL Minds.


With the end of lockdown in England in July, our Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast is also coming to an end. Join us for our live finale on Wednesday 21 July, where we’ll revisit some of the amazing work that’s taken place across UCL over the last year. There’ll also be a panel of experts on hand to answer your COVID-related questions. Register to attend the event and submit a question to the panel.

Questions?

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager.


If you have a more general question, please check that it has not already been answered on the websites above. If it hasn’t, UCL staff can submit a question via our online form or email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699. Lines are open 9am–5pm, Monday-Friday.
