Daily coronavirus (Covid-19)
update: issue 27


Dear colleagues,


Many of our staff and Postgraduate Research students are worried about potential delays to their research, disruption to supervision and financial hardship during the coronavirus outbreak. Although I know that our active and entrepreneurial community will do all they can, I am only too aware there are some disciplines where it is impossible to continue meaningful research remotely, and others in which the extent of remote work will be significantly constrained. I understand that this will raise pressing questions for you about how your grant funding and contracts are affected, and want to reassure that you we are working hard to find a solution.


Fundamentally, this is not a problem that UCL can solve on its own. Even a short-term hiatus in research in some disciplines will have long-term implications, and we need support from Government and from funders. Research-intensive universities need to ensure that we can weather the immediate Covid-19 storm; we also need to preserve the research base so that we can inform and shape the international reconstruction effort that must follow. For this, we need to secure the position of our researchers at every career stage. This is only possible if we work collectively at the national level, with and through partners and sector bodies, to develop a sustainable plan that puts our local interests in a wider national and international context, making the case for short-term research contingency and for longer-term capacity building.


UCL is therefore actively involved with the Russell Group and Universities UK’s lobbying of Government on the implications of this lockdown for our national research capacity and its resilience, and we are doing everything we can to represent your interests and to secure the certainty that our community urgently needs.


You may be aware of the proposals from Universities UK for sector-wide support. Initial feedback from the Minister of State for Universities, Michelle Donelan, confirms that the Government will make it a priority to provide stability for the sector during this difficult time. However, full details of where and how the Government can support will take some time to emerge, in part because it will require multi-department co-ordination.


Grant-funded researchers

One piece of new guidance may help us to support grant-funded researchers directly. Last Friday, the Government updated the Coronavirus Job Retention Scheme (CJRS), which sets out which organisations and employees are eligible for furlough, and we believe that there is now flexibility for Higher Education Institutions to use this scheme to offer researchers the support they need. I have asked Matthew Blain, our Executive Director of HR, to review our options urgently. There are a number of complexities that we will need to work through, so I must ask you to bear with us while we review the implications of the proposal. This is a top priority for us and we will provide an update as soon as we can.


PhD students

Whilst we continue actively to lobby the government and funders on your behalf, we are doing whatever we can to offer support to our student research community in the short-term. As you may be aware from previous messages from the Doctoral School, we have increased the maximum amount payable to PGR students through the Financial Assistance Fund (FAF) to £3,000 and set-up a new Emergency Assistance Grant (EAG) for awards of up to £500. We are also providing all PhD students with an automatic writing-up (CRS) extension to cover the closure period.


We particularly urge self-funded students to apply to the FAF and EAG for financial support. Fee payments can also be deferred.


For funded PhD students in their final year of normal registration, UCL will underwrite stipend payments at the minimum UKRI rate on a monthly basis. This support will be reviewed each month, but at this stage it will be for up to a maximum of three additional months.


We are doing further work to identify whether we can do more to support researchers who are not covered by the schemes now in place for UKRI and Wellcome-funded researchers, and we hope to have more on this for you within the next week.


More details about UCL’s coronavirus response plan for PGR students is available on the Doctoral School website, plus the latest advice from funders and specific information for clinical researchers. Please check these pages regularly, as they will be updated to reflect and respond to changes in the external environment as they arise.


I appreciate that this is a time of considerable anxiety for many researchers, whether PhD students, at an early stage in research careers, or as a PI with a team whose interests you wish to protect. Thank you for your patience and flexibility as we put these plans in place. I will ensure that the team do all we can to respond quickly and communicate directly with you as plans emerge.


With very best wishes,


Professor Michael Arthur

UCL President & Provost

1. A new Grand Challenges initiative: Recovery from Covid-19

The most urgent question facing humanity now is how we recover from Covid-19 and prepare to take the first steps toward a more resilient and equitable world.


UCL Grand Challenges invites you to engage with our new initiative to address the urgent issues posed by the coronavirus outbreak. We are seeking to rally the collective expertise, skills and ideas of the many UCL researchers from our full range of disciplines who are currently unable to continue their research as normal.


The deadline for expressions of interest is 4 May 2020. To find out more and register, please visit the UCL Grand Challenges website.

                                                    

2. UCL's new Coronavirus podcast

Coronavirus: The Whole Story is UCL’s new weekly podcast that explores the pandemic from the perspective of researchers across UCL.


Presented by writer, broadcaster and UCL graduate Vivienne Parry, each episode will look at the coronavirus outbreak through a different lens, covering history, psychology, social sciences, arts, engineering, economics, law and politics.


In our first episode, Professors Hugh Montgomery and Mervyn Singer, Intensive Care experts from UCL’s Division of Medicine answer the question: What Happens to You in Intensive Care?


You can listen on the UCL Minds website or wherever you find your podcasts. If you’d like to be part of a future episode, we’d love to hear from you. Please get in touch by emailing minds@ucl.ac.uk.


3. Online advice and engagement

  • Answers to frequently asked questions for prospective students have been updated on the new coronavirus information hub.

  • Over the weekend there were 1,394 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 405 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 1,555 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


4. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or visit our online support hub. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
