1. Looking out for our students: Phone call campaign

Colleagues from across UCL have launched ‘Looking out for our students’, a new phone call campaign to keep in touch with our students and help them feel cared for during the coronavirus outbreak.

            

Staff can volunteer to call students and check how they are coping, if they are in touch with family and friends, and whether they are able to exercise and look after themselves. The response from students has been incredibly positive so far, with many saying how grateful they are to hear from the team.

            

All colleagues are welcome to volunteer if they’re able to commit at least one day per week until the end of May. Training and support will be provided. Please get permission from your line manager before you sign-up and contact Lina Kamenova, Deputy Director of Student Support and Wellbeing to register your interest.

            

More information about the campaign is available on the UCL Student News website.

                                                                                            

2. UCL tops the list of UKRI Future Leader Fellowships

Seven candidates from UCL have been awarded Future Leader Fellowships from UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) and will receive Government investment to support their research.


These prestigious fellowships identify and nurture the research leaders of tomorrow. The scheme is designed to offer longer-term support to enable researchers to explore complex problems and establish new research teams.


The seven UCL academics are:


  • Dr John Hales (Biochemical Engineering)
  • Dr Pearse Keane (Institute of Ophthalmology)
  • Dr Elly Martin (Medical Physics and Biomed Engineering)
  • Dr Selena Nemorin (Institute of Education)
  • Dr Marco Palombo (Centre for Medical Imaging Computing)
  • Dr Midhat Talibi  (Mechanical Engineering)
  • Dr Sarah Wise (Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis)


More information about the Future Leader Fellows is available on the UKRI website.

              

3. Remote, not Distant at UCL: Keeping your energy up

It may be harder to maintain healthy habits while we’re working remotely. You can find some tips for maintaining your energy levels and keeping your physical activity up on our Remote, not Distant at UCL webpages.

Exercising remotely, but with friends and colleagues, can help you stay motivated and connected with others. Take a look at our list of online workout classes, including yoga, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) and sessions for kids. Most are free to take part in or accept donations on a “pay what you can” basis.

You might also find that getting up and stretching regularly helps you feel more comfortable. Check out these exercises recommended by UCL’s Workplace Health physiotherapist for some ideas.


4. Online advice and engagement

  • Information about UCL’s planning and response to the pandemic has been updated on the coronavirus information hub.
  • Over the weekend there were 1,667 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 416 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 279 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

 

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.

 

5. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
