1. Extending remote working

This will be implemented with immediate effect, and by 5.30pm on Friday 20 March 2020 at the latest. Thereafter, the only activities that should still take place onsite are those where UCL academics are conducting research that is in the immediate national interest, or where on-site working is essential to the continued operation of the university. To facilitate the latter, the Student Centre, UCL Accommodation services, some student residences and buildings linked to essential research will remain open. All other UCL buildings and libraries will be closed. Further details are available on the UCL Estates website.

If your job requires you to work onsite after Friday afternoon, we will make sure you are given as much support as possible. For office-based staff this will include temporarily encouraging staff to sit at fixed desks (i.e. no longer â€˜hot-deskingâ€™), 2 metres apart (typically only occupying every other desk).  Staff should continue to make use of sanitiser and wipes to keep their workspace clean.

The university will remain on a remote working footing until 15 April 2020, after the Easter closure. This will be kept under review.

This is not a closure period, but rather a period of remote working necessitated by unprecedented circumstances. During this time, teaching and assessment for this term will continue and research activity should continue as far as is possible. Other professional services will also switch to remote working. Faculties will co-ordinate the work needed to shut down experiments and to wind-down other local research-related activity. You should consider with your line manager how this move to remote working will affect you.
