1. Working remotely and reopening campus

Many of you have been asking when UCL will reopen, now that the lockdown restrictions put in place by the Government are being slowly relaxed. Our plan remains to reopen a small number of buildings and research areas for staff to work in from the start of next month. We will use what we learn from these first pilot schemes to inform the next phases of re-opening campus.


At the moment, we are intending to proceed with pilots as follows, although please be aware that these plans may change given the constantly evolving national picture:

  • Pilot 1 (w/c 1 June): The Cancer Institute, Queen Square Institute of Neurology, Chemical Engineering and the Student Centre (for staff who cannot work remotely)
  • Pilot 2 (w/c 8 June): Main Library, Institute of Child Health, MRC LMCB, Chemistry


Staff in these areas are being contacted directly about taking part in the pilots. We will be implementing social distancing measures and increasing cleaning across all areas of campus as we start to re-open buildings. In addition to this, we will be asking everyone on campus to wear face coverings when moving around or when social distancing is not an option, check their temperature, wash their hands frequently and use a one-way system onsite.


Re-opening all of our buildings is a complex process that will take time. We are using a tailored approach, drawing on advice from our own experts in health, education, human behaviour and infection prevention. We need to balance specific departmental requirements with the need to be careful and not place anyone at risk. Our priority at all times is to minimise the risk to everyone onsite, and comply with Government, PHE and NHS guidelines – and if those guidelines change, we will need to change our plans accordingly.


Lots needs to happen. Water and power supplies need to be checked, fire safety, security and first aid requirements need to be taken into account, and we must make sure that cleaning and maintenance can be carried out regularly and safely. This is all happening but it’s not instantaneous, so thank you for your understanding as we pull together all of the strands in this complicated plan.


Returning to campus, even for a pilot, will not be right or possible for everyone at the moment. We’d like to reiterate that no one will be compelled to return to UCL for the pilots if they do not wish to do so. You may be concerned about your health or commute to work for example, or have childcare or caring responsibilities. We are committed to making sure you come back only when you’re confident that it is right for you.


Finally, please remember that our position on returning to campus has not changed; unless you are one of our key staff who has worked onsite during the lockdown, or you are contacted about taking part in one of the pilots, please continue to work remotely for now.


2. How you can help UCL save money and protect jobs

There has been a lot of coverage in the media recently about the financial pressures facing the Higher Education sector all over the world. UCL is no different. These pressures are completely unprecedented and – crucially – not yet easy to quantify. We are therefore planning for the prospect of a sizeable reduction in our income for at least the next year and probably well beyond.


We are working to cut back significantly on non-pay expenditure across the university as a matter of urgency. We have already taken action to reduce our outgoings by introducing a recruitment freeze of new and replacement posts, except where there is an immediate and urgent priority and pausing or stopping significant elements of capital expenditure where we can do so without incurring extra costs.


Now we need your help to support budget holders and the Procurement team to secure the best possible value for money at all times. You can make a real difference by using existing agreements with UCL’s preferred suppliers and, where possible, delaying all large expenditure until our financial position is clearer following student enrolment in September.   


Colleagues in Finance are talking to all budget holders to identify ways to reduce spend. The Procurement team is working with colleagues across every area of the university to determine how we can ‘buy better’ (for example through negotiating better prices and introducing terms that are benchmarked against market rates) and ‘spend better’ (such as reducing complexity in our supply chains and improving our internal processes).


We also want to hear your suggestions for how and where UCL could reduce non-pay expenditure further. Any idea, big or small, will help.


Thank you for playing your part.

  • Yesterday there were 3,369 visits to the coronavirus information hub, 2,523 visits to the UCL Covid-19 research website and 248 visits to the askUCL landing page.

Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


6. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.

You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
