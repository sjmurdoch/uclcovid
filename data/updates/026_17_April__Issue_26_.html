Daily coronavirus (Covid-19)
update: issue 26


Dear colleagues,


I hope you had a relaxing break over the Easter weekend and were able to enjoy some time with your loved ones.


I am writing with an update on UCL’s response to what is proving to be a seismic and utterly unprecedented international crisis. The landscape is changing rapidly, and the range and nature of the challenges that the coronavirus pandemic presents for UCL are broad and considerable.


I firmly believe that the universities that come out of this well will be those that show the greatest humanity, and where staff and students are able to pull together and support each other. Our top priority has been to ensure the safety of our community, and we have acted quickly in response to Public Health England and Government guidance. We are also working hard to protect the wellbeing of our staff and students, and to maintain academic standards.


We’ve reached an important milestone with the confirmation of the assessment programme for this year. Thanks to the hard work of so many colleagues, we now have robust and workable plans in place for a full programme of remote examinations this summer, and we have also been able to put in place a ‘no detriment’ policy that ensures no student's long-term future will be negatively affected by the circumstances in which they will sit their assessments. Professor Anthony Smith and I are enormously grateful both to those of you who have worked at speed to review examination plans and adapt them for current circumstances, and to the teams who have led on the co-ordination of this huge piece of work. In total, 3,333 assessments across 2,367 modules have been adjusted, equating to 130,000 individual student assessments – or 31% of the total. Of the 2,000 invigilated exams that would have taken place at the Excel Centre, 237 have been excluded (because module teams already have enough information about student achievement), 1,061 have been changed to coursework and 667 will be timed exams taken over a 24-hour period. Only 40 exams have been deferred.


Now that we are making such good progress on the most immediate of the challenges that Covid-19 presents, I am working with staff across the university to plan for the future, to determine how to put UCL in the best possible position for the 2020-21 academic year and beyond. We are committed to ensuring that each of you receive the support you need and our students continue to experience the best possible education during this difficult time.


We have moved swiftly into crisis management mode to ensure that UCL can continue to function smoothly and make informed decisions at speed over the weeks and months ahead. A new Gold / Silver / Bronze structure, led directly by me and endorsed by UCL Council, our governing body, has been in place for several weeks and is functioning well. It enables us to respond quickly to the challenges that are emerging across business continuity, education, research and external engagement.


Within this structure, we have been able to take a number of important decisions at pace, including the move to online teaching; introducing remote working for the majority of colleagues; equalising sick pay for our contractors; cancelling accommodation fees for students in term 3; overturning pay deductions for striking staff; and agreeing a disturbance allowance for key workers who are still onsite.


We will continue to update you as the situation unfolds. In the meantime, more details about how we are responding to the pandemic and planning for UCL’s future is available on the coronavirus information hub, where you can find out who is working on different areas and submit any questions you may have.


We are also conscious of the great work our staff are doing in response to the Covid-19 pandemic, and the breadth of our research expertise on questions relevant to long-term international reconstruction planning. I’ve therefore asked Professor Dame Hazel Genn and Professor Graeme Reid to lead our efforts to engage constructively with government on these issues, and to support me in ensuring that UCL is well-represented in the national debate. They will be closely involved with the work emerging from the Gold/Silver/Bronze structure around our future priorities for research activity and for our external engagement with national and international partners. We are also planning to develop a Grand Challenge strand focused specifically on Covid-19, with more information about how you can get involved with this in the coming weeks.


In the meantime, I continue to be inspired by your hard work, your support for colleagues and our students and your contributions to the national effort. Your resilience and continued commitment will stand us in good stead for the weeks and months ahead.


With very best wishes,


Professor Michael Arthur

UCL President & Provost

3. Online advice and engagement

  • Answers to frequently asked questions about UCL’s planning and response to the pandemic have been updated on the coronavirus information hub.
  • Yesterday there were 2,908 visits to the coronavirus information hub; 1,745 visits to the UCL-Ventura breathing aid landing page; and 1,109 visits to the askUCL landing page.


Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

 

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered on the UCL website, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


4. Support for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.


If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak. You can also find useful tips about looking after your wellbeing or if you’re feeling anxious on the Every Mind Matters website.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
