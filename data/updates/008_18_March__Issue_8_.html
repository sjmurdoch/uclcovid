1. New structure for the Coronavirus Mitigation Group

The Coronavirus Mitigation Group is assuming a new structure to allow us to continue our meetings effectively and make timely decisions while staff are working remotely. Led by the Provost, the group will be split into a number of subgroups including colleagues from across the institution, plus representatives from trade unions and Students’ Union UCL. We are also taking expert advice from Professor Andrew Hayward, Professor of Infectious Disease Epidemiology and Inclusion Health Research, and the UCL Institute for Risk and Disaster Reduction. Together we are focusing on both the immediate impact the virus could have on UCL, and looking forward to 2021 and beyond.


2. Building closures

By 5.30pm on Friday 20 March, most UCL buildings will be closed and the only activities that should still take place onsite are those where UCL academics are conducting research that is in the immediate national interest, or where on-site working is essential to the continued operation of the university.


This is a step above our usual closure arrangement for Christmas or Easter. Buildings will only be open where the Faculty has confirmed that critical research is being carried out. You will not be able to access laboratories unless your work is on that list. If you are not able to work from home for personal reasons, the Student Centre will be open for staff and students.


The Science Library and the Main Library (Wilkins building) will be open from 09:00 – 17:00 until Friday 20 March. All other library buildings are now closed.


We arranged yesterday to renew all books on loan until 5 June and all recalls have been suspended. Students do not need to bring books back or worry about fines. For more information about library closures and loans, please visit the UCL website.


Directors of Operations have been contacted about any essential research or business critical activities so arrangements can be made to keep these buildings open. In all other cases buildings will be closed and there will be no access in any other way. More information about building closures will be added to the UCL Estates website.


Any staff who cannot work remotely for personal reasons or because of the nature of their role should work at fixed desks at least 2 metres apart. You must also complete UCL’s online fire safety training, to ensure we comply with health and safety regulations.


Our Fire Safety eLearning package is available via UCL MyHR. Please select the left-hand menu / UCL My Learning (home page) / My Learning / Learner Home. Under the course catalogue search class and select Fire Safety course.


3. Advice for staff working remotely

Wellbeing and self-care

Many of us may be working or studying from home with limited interactions with others. It’s important that all of us take care of ourselves during this difficult time.


Please visit our new online resource hub including top tips for working remotely, technical guidance, wellbeing advice and links to further support available for staff.


Online learning

UCL’s MyLearning modules and LinkedIn Learning gives staff access to a range of online learning options. If you are working from home, please use these tools to help you improve your skills. There are also online courses designed to support your wellbeing.


Volunteering to support work across UCL

As we extend remote working across the university, you may find that you need to work very differently to the way you would in the office. This provides a great opportunity for staff to get involved in work you wouldn’t usually have the chance to support.


We are currently working on a way in which people across the organisation can volunteer to support with work that is ongoing elsewhere at UCL. We are developing a new system that will connect volunteers who are looking to either share existing skills or try new things with departments who request a little extra support during this time. Alongside this, individuals will have the chance to volunteer with important community support, the emergency services, or the NHS. We will share more information about these opportunities next week.


Annual leave

It’s important that you take your annual leave as planned, even if you are working remotely. We want to make sure our staff are well-rested and have a good work-life balance, especially during the challenging circumstances we find ourselves in.


With this in mind, we are not changing our existing annual leave policy, and staff will not be able to take forward more than the maximum of five days from one annual leave period to the next (other than in exceptional circumstances). If you are concerned about taking annual leave, please speak to your line manager.


Before you leave campus

We would also like to remind staff who are working remotely to make workspaces safe and secure before you leave. The steps you need to take are similar to those required before the Christmas and Easter closures, and include:


  • Switching off all non-essential equipment
  • Clearing fridges
  • Securing the building


Further guidance will be updated on the UCL Estates website.

6. Support available for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have the virus, please self-isolate for seven days in line with the current Government advice. You can also find advice on the NHS website.

         

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager in the first instance. You can also access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.

         

Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
