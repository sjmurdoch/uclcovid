3. Advice for staff working remotely

ISD have created a guide to remote working, which includes information about setting up at home, tools to help you work remotely and tips for how to use them. Although ISD has significantly increased capacity of our systems, the move to remote working is putting many of these tools and services under pressure. Please follow these steps to help reduce the impact on our network and your home internet connection.


  • If you only need to access email, calendar, MS Office applications (Word, Excel etc), Teams or are browsing the internet you do not need to be connected to VPN or Desktop Anywhere. All of these services are accessible through any home internet connection.
  • Use VPN as the first choice for remote access to UCL systems â€“ the capacity is greater than Desktop@UCL Anywhere so unless you need access to applications on Desktop Anywhere, VPN will be faster.
  • If you are using Microsoft Teams for conferencing, consider using audio rather than video, as this uses much lower bandwidth. Or only switch to video when you are speaking. You can also blur the background of a video call to use less data and help viewers focus their attention on the speaker.
  • Consider using telephone conferencing instead of using Microsoft Teams if you have access to contact numbers for everybody who needs to be on the call.


Many of us will be working remotely with limited interactions with others, or working very differently to the way we would in the office. Itâ€™s important that we look after ourselves. Please keep checking our online resource hub for advice about wellbeing and self-care during this difficult time.


Please continue to take your annual leave as planned, even if you are working remotely. We want to make sure our staff are well-rested and can still achieve a good work-life balance. If you feel unwell and cannot work (whether from coronavirus or any other reason), you should report this to your line manager and record it on MyHR in the usual way.


Any staff who cannot work remotely for personal reasons or because of the nature of their role should work at fixed desks at least 2 metres apart.
