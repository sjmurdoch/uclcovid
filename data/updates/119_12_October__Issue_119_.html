1. Reporting confirmed cases of COVID-19 at UCL

UCL has published a webpage showing the numbers of confirmed cases of COVID-19 among UCL’s staff and students.


It shows cases confirmed in the last 24 hours, cases confirmed in the last 7 days, and the total number of cases confirmed since the start of Term 1 on 28 September. New cases are defined as new confirmed positive test results reported to UCL’s Public Health Daily Review Group.


These data will be updated by 9am Monday to Friday. Figures are correct as of 5pm the previous day. Figures received over the weekend will be included in Tuesday’s report.


For more information, visit the Current Confirmed Cases page of UCL’s Coronavirus information hub.


2. Teaching using online platforms

An incredible amount of hard work has been put in to transform our teaching offer and ensure that UCL students continue to receive a high-quality education as much of the teaching moves online. As term gets fully underway, staff and students alike have been adapting to our new ways of working, with record use of our various online platforms. So far this term:


  • 5 October was the busiest online day so far, with nearly 59,000 Moodle users logging over 166,000 visits on this day alone. There were also over 2,800 Zoom sessions and 763 Blackboard Collaborate sessions.
  • On 6 October over 2,800 users used the China Connect platform, which provides enhanced internet connectivity for users in mainland China.
  • Nearly 250 “Virtual Common Rooms” have been set up, including International Common Rooms, set up to help international students interact with peers in their region.


For advice and support in setting up virtual learning environments please look at the Online teaching: Guidance, tips and platforms page on the Teaching and Learning website.
