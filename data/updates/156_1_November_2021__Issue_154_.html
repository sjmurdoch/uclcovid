1. Stopping the spread of COVID-19 at UCL

No single safety measure is 100% effective against COVID-19. UCL has launched a new video featuring the “Swiss Cheese” model, which explains why combining different measures is the best way to stop the spread of the virus.


You can play your part by:


  • Getting vaccinated for COVID-19, if you can;
  • Being thoughtful and wearing a face covering whilst indoors, unless you’re exempt;
  • Staying at home if you feel at all unwell;
  • Self-isolating and ordering a Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test if you have coronavirus symptoms;
  • Getting tested twice weekly with a rapid Lateral Flow Test (LFT) if you don’t have symptoms and reporting your results to the NHS and UCL Connect to Protect;
  • Being considerate of those around you, especially if they’re wearing a ‘Please Give Me Space’ lanyard or badge; and
  • Washing or sanitising your hands frequently throughout the day.


By taking these steps, you create multiple lines of defence against the virus and ultimately reduce the chances of COVID-19 spreading.


You can watch the video and read more about the safety measures on campus on the Staff News pages.
