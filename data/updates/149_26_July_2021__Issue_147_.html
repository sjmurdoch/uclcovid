1. Easing of coronavirus restrictions from 19 July

Many coronavirus restrictions have now been lifted in England in line with Stage 4 of the Government's roadmap.


In line with Government guidance and advice from UCL’s Public Health Advisory Panel, some coronavirus restrictions have been lifted on campus. We are keeping a number of measures in place to protect you and the wider UCL community:


  • Unless you are exempt, we expect everyone on campus to wear a face covering in indoor spaces, including in lectures, seminar rooms, study spaces, offices and labs. More information can be found on our updated face coverings guidance page.
  • One-way systems are being removed and social distancing requirements relaxed, which means most spaces can return to their normal capacity. It will take some time for the Estates team to reopen all spaces and update signage – the Estates website is being regularly updated to show the latest status of buildings.
  • Staff who have been working remotely may start returning to campus, but we do not expect everyone back onsite at this stage. From September, we expect all remaining staff to return to campus for at least part of the time. More information can be found on the Working on Campus webpages.


If you’re working on campus please remember:


  • You must not come on to campus if you feel unwell;
  • You are expected to bring a face covering to wear in indoor spaces;
  • You should get tested for COVID-19 twice a week, even if you don’t have symptoms;
  • If you do have symptoms you must self-isolate and immediately and order a test from the NHS. You should also report your symptoms via UCL Connect to Protect and NHS Test and Trace – find out more
  • You are encouraged to get vaccinated for COVID-19 and flu when you are eligible;
  • You should wash your hands frequently. Hand sanitiser is freely available on campus.


Further information can be found on the Keeping Safe on Campus webpages.


UCL is committed to looking after everyone’s mental and physical wellbeing and ensuring staff and students still protect themselves and others. We are working hard to make sure all our buildings and spaces are as safe as possible and continuing to follow UK Government guidance, as well as the expertise of our own specialists in health, education, human behaviour and infection prevention.


Colleagues who have been shielding or feel anxious about people getting too close may choose to wear a Please Give Me Space lanyard or badge. Please look out for these and be mindful of people’s wishes. Disabled or clinically vulnerable staff can find out more about support available on UCL’s Coronavirus webpages.


2. UCL COVID-19 testing centre to move from Bidborough House to Student Centre

The Lateral Flow Testing (LFT) centre on the ground floor of Bidborough House will close on Friday 30 July. A new LFT facility is due to open at the UCL Student Centre on Monday 2 August, providing a smaller-scale service for those symptom-free staff and students that prefer to test on-site or that require assistance. Please note that anyone in England who does not have symptoms can also get regular rapid lateral flow tests delivered to their home address.


Once you receive your results, whether positive or negative, please report your results through NHS Test and Trace and Connect to Protect reporting tool.  


Details of the new testing facility will be posted in due course on the Coronavirus webpages.


3. Travel service and UCL’s provision

Last week we launched our new travel service with Clarity. Any new travel bookings for national or international travel should be made via them. Additional guidance can be found on the UCL Travel Pages. With the continued uncertainty UCL’s travel position remains the same. Staff and students may travel on UCL business to countries that are on the Green or Amber lists for essential reasons only. This is aligned to the fieldwork framework and helps ensure the safety of staff and students.


Whilst travel levels are low it does provide an opportunity to identify and implement improvements. Over the summer months we would really value hearing feedback on experiences with the Clarity service, what may be needed to support your return to travel and any additional training requirements you may have. Feedback can be sent to the Travel Project Team.
