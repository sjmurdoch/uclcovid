Dear colleagues,


I am acutely aware that this is a very unsettling time for UCLâ€™s research community and especially for those grant-funded staff working on projects where funding is coming to an end.


Colleagues in HR have been working hard to determine whether and how we might use the Governmentâ€™s Coronavirus Job Retention Scheme (CJRS) for those on grants with end dates in the near future. I am now able to confirm that we will be providing support as follows.


In the first instance, Principal Investigators have been reviewing project budgets to reallocate any unspent funding within that grant to extend contract finishing dates wherever this is possible and within the funderâ€™s guidance. UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) has indicated that it will be encouraging this flexible approach of moving money around to cover payroll and we hope other funding bodies will follow this example. PIs have also been considering whether they have access to other sources of funding outside the specific grant that might be used to extend contracts for staff nearing the end of a fixed-term contract.


We recognise that this has not been feasible in all cases, so the following arrangements will apply for staff who cannot be supported directly by residual grant funds or other budgets:


We will offer those grant-funded staff who were expecting to depart UCL during May and June 2020 the opportunity to be furloughed, and have their leaving date extended until the end of June. This offer applies to all eligible staff supported by a grant and employed by UCL, including researchers, technicians and professional services staff.


Those staff who take up the option of furlough will be paid their full salary. The Government would nominally pay 80% of costs, but due to the conditions of the scheme and pension arrangements within higher education, this amount falls to about 60%. UCL will make up the difference. The CJRS scheme currently only runs until the end of June and we are not therefore able to offer support beyond that date.


It is important to note that any staff on furlough cannot undertake any work for UCL. This means that activities such as writing up results obtained at UCL and analysing UCL data are not allowed.


For those grant-funded staff who are due to leave UCL during July and August 2020, we will extend their leaving date until the end of August. They will also receive full pay until the end of this period, which UCL will cover. As the furlough scheme is not available after June this will be paid for from core funding in the hope of bridging staff who may be able to move onto new grants if lockdown is lifted and such grants are able to start in the autumn.  


Whilst opportunities in the near term will be limited, the HR Services team in the PS Hub will regularly notify all affected staff of vacancies at UCL via our redeployment list.


We will write to those impacted by this decision shortly. We will also contact Deans and Heads of Department as soon as possible so that they know how the process will operate. I am writing to you all now in advance of those communications, as I recognise that many of you will be worried and looking for greater certainty about your circumstances.


The expectation is that all other options will have been fully explored first before core funds are used. We hope that these arrangements will provide reassurance to staff who may be changing posts at a difficult time. Whilst we canâ€™t prevent redundancies where grant funding has come to an end, we will do all we can to extend employment and give people a little more time to find new appointments if at all possible.


Thank you for your patience and understanding as we work to support our community, despite the unprecedented and fast-moving circumstances we find ourselves in.


With very best wishes,

Professor Michael Arthur

UCL President & Provost
