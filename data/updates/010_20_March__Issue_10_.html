Dear colleagues,


As we come to the end the most extraordinary week, I want to thank everyone for the enormous effort and dedication you are putting in during this unprecedented crisis. I am drawing inspiration from your exhaustive commitment to working through new and changing circumstances to support our community and beyond. In that spirit, I’d also like to recognise the difficult decision that UCU members have taken not to continue with their strike action.


I am incredibly proud of our colleagues who are contributing to the national interest across a variety of fields. Today, we launched our new website that sets out the many ways that UCL is advancing public knowledge, advising world leaders, providing expert comment and urgently researching new ways of tackling coronavirus. I encourage you all to look at this.


I am grateful for the many messages of support. Much of the work we are undertaking right now, calls on us to work above and beyond normal capacity, but every day I encounter many examples of our remarkable resilience and community spirit. This is UCL at our best. I’m sure like me you are finding these new ways of working quite alien, and physically and mentally exhausting. Do please look after yourselves and draw strength from your loved ones as best you can.


These are extraordinary and unsettling times for everyone, but I know that by working together we will conquer this challenge.


With best wishes,


Professor Michael Arthur
UCL President & Provost

1. UCL cleaning, security, portering and catering staff

From this Monday 23 March 2020, staff employed by UCL’s suppliers Axis, Sodexo and Aramark will be able to access sick pay from the first day of an absence, in line with staff employed directly by the university. This harmonisation had been agreed with UNISON, who are the recognised union for Sodexo and Axis, and was originally planned for July, but the decision was made to bring it forward as a direct result of the coronavirus pandemic.


UCL contractors will honour all contracted hours for their staff, whether they are required to attend work or not, and this commitment will be made regardless of the nature of their contracts. Where staff work on an ‘as and when’ basis, if they have been working a regular weekly pattern, their pay will be calculated based on their average earnings over the last 12 weeks. This will be paid whether staff are required to work or not during the coronavirus outbreak.


Special Leave will also be paid for people who are self-isolating, in line with directly employed staff. This will not be deducted from sick pay balances nor count toward triggers in absence policies.


UCL is a wide community who come together in the pursuit of our academic missions. Colleagues working in catering, cleaning, security and portering play a vital role in this, working at the front line to keep our campuses open, and operating safely and effectively. We will continue to work with UNISON throughout the coronavirus outbreak, and their representatives have joined our Coronavirus Mitigation Group.


More information about these changes, including levelling-up of pay and annual leave, is available on the UCL website.


2. How UCL staff and students are tackling coronavirus

We are incredibly proud of our staff and students, whose research and hard work is directly supporting the national effort to tackle the coronavirus pandemic.


UCL researchers are working in the vanguard of helping find cure, improving diagnosis and advising Government here in the UK and globally. In addition, they are providing expertise on issues as varied as predictions on virus spread, panic buying and stockpiling, broadband provision, and the economic and political impact of the pandemic. Earlier this week UCL confirmed that it would release all clinical academics to support NHS services during the current crisis, while our medical students are volunteering to look after doctors’ and nurses’ children and help out with other day-to-day jobs, such as dog walking and shopping. We are also making UCL’s Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) machines available to the NHS to expand the capacity for coronavirus testing.


To find out more about the work UCL is doing to tackle the spread of the pandemic, visit our coronavirus research website.


3. Student accommodation

Due to increasing travel restrictions and the national imperative of reducing the movement of people in the coming weeks, we have urged students who have access to an alternative safe and secure source of accommodation to vacate UCL halls of residences. Halls will of course stay open for all students, where staying with us is the best option for them.


UCL will not charge accommodation fees for students who won’t be living in UCL halls in Term 3. This applies to those students who have already checked out or are planning on checking out.


We are notifying students living in UCL accommodation from today. You can read our announcement for students on the UCL website.


4. Supporting staff involved with teaching

The UCL Remote Delivery Task Group, in consultation with departments and faculties, has developed guidance for the continued delivery of Postgraduate Taught (PGT) and Master of Research (MRes) dissertations, projects and placements. This guidance is issued under the Academic Manual's Material Irregularities Procedure.


UCL has agreed that students can self-certify if they have Extenuating Circumstances for the remainder of the 2019/20 academic year. See the full details of the Covid-19 Extenuating Circumstances Procedure and the updated FAQs on the student support page.


The Teaching Continuity pages continue to be updated daily with information and resources to support the changes to teaching and assessment amid the coronavirus outbreak.


5. Building closures

Please ensure that you make your work space at UCL safe and secure before you leave the building, as you would before the Christmas or Easter closures. Download and complete the checklist of steps you need to take from the Estates website.


6. Online advice and engagement

  • Answers to frequently asked questions about remote working, procurement and library services have been added to the main coronavirus webpage.
  • Yesterday there were 7,760 visits to the main coronavirus webpage and 1,621 visits to askUCL.


Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we will add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

                         

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


7. Further support available for staff and students

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.


If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager in the first instance. You can also access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
