1. Update on returning to campus

We continue to encourage staff to work remotely where possible for the time being, where your work and personal situation allows, unless your manager or supervisor has contacted you directly about returning to work on campus.


Your Head of Department will decide when it’s appropriate for teams to return to work on campus:


  • If your role means you don’t need to attend site to do your work, you may be able to continue working remotely until January 2021, when this policy will be reviewed. However, if you prefer to work onsite, there will be an option to come and work on campus from 1 September, when the majority of UCL buildings will be open. If you do choose to work on campus, you will need to liaise with your line manager and follow local guidance to ensure that you’re observing the conditions of returning and to help make sure that no more than 25% of our people are onsite at any one time for social distancing purposes.
  • If you need to be onsite to do your job (e.g. to deliver face to face teaching, some Professional Services support or technical services in a laboratory), then there is a presumption that you will return to campus for the number of hours required in order to fulfil your role from 1 September.


Any colleagues who are worried about returning to work on campus should speak to their line manager in the first instance. You may be asked to complete an Individual Risk Assessment to help understand your concerns and identify any measures that might manage any risks to your health and take into account your circumstances. In some cases that may mean that you do not return to campus at this stage. 


Many of you have let us know through the Your Wellbeing survey that commuting to work is an area of concern. There are various options available, including:


  • Walking and cycling where you can – UCL is currently installing 600 new cycle racks and has started a cycling Buddy scheme, as well as the UCL Cycle Scheme, which allows you to buy a bike tax-free. You can find out more about cycling and walking to work on the Sustainable UCL webpages.
  • Travelling by public transport – our advice is to travel outside of peak times and follow TfL’s safety and hygiene measures. You could also complete part of your journey by cycle or on foot if this is possible, for example by getting off at King’s Cross or Tottenham Court Road, as both these stations have large concourses that may make social distancing easier.


You can find more guidance about commuting in the “Getting to campus” section of our returning to campus webpage.


We are committed to making sure you come back to campus confident in what UCL is doing to protect our staff and students onsite. For more information about what UCL is doing and how you can help, please see our Keeping Safe on Campus webpages.

3. Advocating for UCL’s impact in London whilst adapting to new ways of working

Professor Alan Thompson, Dean of the Faculty of Brain Sciences and Pro-Vice-Provost (London), has written about working in partnership with local communities, policy makers, local government and other stakeholders in London during the COVID-19 crisis and beyond.


Ahead of the new academic year, colleagues in the PVP London Office are working on launching a new annual programme of activities, to demonstrate our commitment to working in key areas which showcase the breadth and depth of our research and expertise. The theme of the first programme, due to be launched in October, will be mental health – find out more.


4. Further advice and support

Daily coronavirus updates

These emails are sent every weekday to all UCL staff and postgraduate research students. They contain information about the pandemic and UCL’s response, including guidance about working remotely, policies and procedures, news about teaching and management arrangements and updates about UCL’s COVID-19 research.


If you would like to submit an item about the pandemic or UCL’s response to be included in the daily coronavirus update, please email internalcommunications@ucl.ac.uk.

                                                                     

Your Wellbeing weekly survey

The UCL Your Wellbeing check-in survey is a short, weekly survey for staff and postgraduate research students. The data will be used to find out how our community is coping with the huge changes caused by the coronavirus crisis and inform future improvements. Complete week 6 of the survey now.


Coronavirus information hub and research webpages

The information hub at ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus is updated regularly with information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions about coronavirus as well as links to advice from other parts of UCL.


The COVID-19 research webpages showcase the work that UCL is carrying out on coronavirus, and contain information and resources for researchers.


Working remotely

The Remote, not Distant at UCL pages contain advice and resources to help staff adapt to remote working, including self-care and wellbeing, digital communication resources and guidance for managers.     


Keeping safe on campus

The Keeping Safe on Campus website provides live updates about our returning to campus pilots, as well as safety and social distancing guidelines for those on site.                                                                                                                                     

Health and Safety advice

The Working Safely During a Pandemic website from Safety Services provides guidance on how to manage the health and safety of staff at UCL during the pandemic. Topics include using display screen equipment, working with COVID-19 samples and risk assessments for returning to campus.


Your health and wellbeing

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

                

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.


Face coverings

You can find information on face coverings, including how to make your own, on the UCL Institute of Making website and the Public Health England website.   

                                                                      

Questions?

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager in the first instance.


If you have a more general question, please check that it has not already been answered on the websites given above. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699. Lines are open 9am–5pm, Monday–Friday.
