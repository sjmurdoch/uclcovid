1. Tier 2 restrictions in London

London has moved to a Tier 2 (high) alert level for COVID-19 and additional restrictions have been introduced to help reduce the spread of infection. Many of the restrictions are the same as before and campus remains open, with measures in place to keep everyone as safe as possible.

          

There are three important changes for everyone living within the Tier 2 area:

           

  • It is now illegal to socialise with anyone outside of your household in any indoor setting, whether at home or in a public place, unless you are in a support bubble. For students in UCL halls, their household is anyone they share a kitchen or bathroom with;
  • The ‘Rule of 6’ applies to gatherings outdoors. This means it is illegal to meet outdoors in groups of more than six people;
  • You must not travel to any areas in the Tier 3 (very high) alert level for COVID-19.

           

Our buildings and outdoor spaces remain open and face-to-face teaching is continuing as planned, in line with Tier 2 guidance, and you can still visit our libraries and museums. All organised sport and physical activity run by the Students’ Union’s sports clubs are exempt from new restrictions and will continue. Students’ Union UCL is working closely with society committees to support as much face-to face activity as possible, on and off campus and in line with UCL’s coronavirus guidelines.

           

Professor Anthony Smith, Vice-Provost (Education & Student Affairs) and Carol Paige, Democracy, Operations and Community Officer, Students’ Union UCL are writing to students today to outline the new guidelines and what they mean for UCL. You can see the full message on the Student News website.


2. Connect to Protect: a definition of a COVID-19 contact

UCL has launched Connect to Protect, a tool that allows students and staff to notify UCL if they have COVID-19 symptoms, have received a positive test result, or have been asked to self-isolate. This information is used by the UCL Connect to Protect team to ensure the appropriate support is provided to staff, students and departments, and to manage our collective response in partnership with Camden & Islington Public Health. This response includes case management, which is our version of ‘tracing’, and our internal testing provision, both of which are linked into the national ‘test and trace’ programme.

            

It is important to be aware of the definition of a ‘contact’, so that the Connect to Protect team can ensure cases are managed effectively, and that staff and students are not asked to self-isolate needlessly.


The definition of ‘contact’ is a person who has been close to someone who has tested positive for COVID-19 anytime from 2 days before the person was symptomatic up to 10 days from onset of symptoms (this is when they are infectious to others).


As we find cases of COVID-19 across UCL (most frequently in our student accommodation) the team will work with each case to understand who is a ‘contact’ and needs to self-isolate. If we continue to follow UCL’s 2m approach to social distancing it is unlikely that you will be classified as a contact.

            

The current number of confirmed cases of COVID-19 can be found here, please continue to use Connect to Protect.

         

3. Building checks by the Safety team

UCL’s Health and Safety Committee has approved a mandatory safety monitoring programme, to ensure compliance with existing implemented COVID-19 risk control measures which began in August.

         

Safety monitoring, in the form of inspections or building tours, is an important part of our organisation’s safety management system. The expectation is that departments must carry out monitoring (in the form of building inspections) at least once a month. Monitoring provides information on the effectiveness of risk control measures as defined in your risk assessments and it gives you a mechanism to check compliance and identify areas for improvement.

         

Monitoring of COVID-19 controls must be carried out using the inspection template AT0093 in UCL’s reporting software riskNET and should commence one month after reoccupation of a building. So far, 30 departmental inspections have taken place across 25 buildings at UCL which is very positive.

         

In addition to departmental inspections, Safety Services has visited 58 buildings and 197 teaching & learning spaces so far. The team will be increasing their presence in October through to the end of the year, with the aim of inspecting all UCL buildings at least twice.

         

If you have concerns about COVID-19 risk control measures, please speak to your line manager / supervisor in the first instance. You can also report a hazard observation using riskNET or contact the COVID-19 helpline by email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk.

         

Find out how to contact Safety Services, and find more information on Health and Safety monitoring, including inspections.

5. Welcome Week – what happened?

Over the course of two weeks, UCL hosted its first online Welcome Week with a Digital Welcome Fair and a range of other interactive activities. Here are some of the highlights:


  • 14,581 students digitally attended the Welcome Fair;
  • Alongside the Students’ Union Digital Welcome Fair platform, it live-streamed its own TV show, direct from the Student Centre, and had 18,600 viewers watching live;
  • By the end of Welcome Week, on 4 October, 14,871 club and society memberships had been bought by 4,515 students - a 95% increase on the same period last year.
