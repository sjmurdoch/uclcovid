2. Teams Tips Tuesday

For our Top Tips this week we are focusing on accessibility again.

   

Top Tip: in a meeting you can put closed captioning on to get live subtitles of the conversation. To do this, press the ellipsis (…) in the meeting bar and select ‘Turn on live captions’ and the words will appear beneath the video feed. This has to be done individually and captions are not saved.

   

However, if you have recorded the meeting, Teams has the ability to automatically transcribe it.

   

Top Tip: once the recording is complete:

  • open it in MS Stream
  • click the ‘update video details’ option (pencil icon)
  • update the video language to ‘English’ (although other languages are available)
  • select ‘auto-generate caption file’ and then ‘apply’

   

The next time the video of the meeting is opened, a transcript is always displayed. NB – it’s worth checking through the transcript for accuracy. Sometimes words aren’t captured correctly which can make for some highly entertaining statements!

   

If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #teamstipstuesday. For further information about using Teams, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, please contact the IT Services Desk.

Your health and wellbeing

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

                                                                                                

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.


Face coverings
