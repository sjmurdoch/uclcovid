Coronavirus update: Issue 159 –
An important update about Term 2 teaching


Dear colleagues,


UCL’s University Management Committee has met to discuss the recent changes in government rules and guidance in view of the Omicron variant, and how this may impact UCL.


Face-to-face teaching in Term 2


These are uncertain times and the scientific and public health data are still unclear as to the impact and consequences of the Omicron variant. What we can be sure about is that the start of term will be anything but normal with all of us, staff and students alike, facing some disruption. The advice from government is that universities should remain open for face-to-face education and learning support. That is also the advice from our own Public Health experts.


Our priorities are to keep you as safe as possible and minimise the risks on campus. Our plan for Term 2 is for campus to remain open and for face-to-face teaching to go ahead as it has this term, which means of course that large teaching events will continue online and all students, including students based overseas, have been told that they should come to campus.

  

We continue to encourage all staff and students aged 18 or over to get a COVID-19 vaccination or booster dose as soon as you can.


Anticipated disruption to campus-based learning


We recognise that some members of staff may face challenges in coming to campus if they have been affected by illness, need to self-isolate, or have urgent family commitments. Students based overseas have been advised to plan their arrival in the UK to attend on-campus activities from the start of term. However, as we found with the beginning of term 1, we should anticipate that the first 2-3 weeks in January will be disrupted, including some students facing delays in getting back to London. We can also expect an increase in absences among students required to self-isolate – including those students based in the UK.


We will need to use our best endeavours to support students through this and enable them to continue their studies online for up to two or three weeks until they can join us in person. Teaching and support staff are advised to work with your Head of Department and Faculty Tutor to see how you might offer this flexibility to students who cannot be on campus from the start of term. Staff should plan to contact your students early in the New Year to let them know how they can keep up with their studies if their arrival is delayed.  


A small number of regulated, practice- and placement-based programmes may have less flexibility; staff are advised to let affected students know as early as possible that is the case so they can plan accordingly.

 

See our message sent to students today.


Keeping safe on campus


Campus services and facilities will remain open, including UCL Libraries and Support & Wellbeing, although some will be operating reduced hours over the winter break. See what support and services are open during the holiday period.  


You must not come to campus if you are unwell or have been asked to self-isolate.


Please remember:


  • Get vaccinated or boosted against COVID-19 if you can. The NHS vaccination centre at Bidborough House is currently accepting walk-in appointments, although these may be suspended at very busy times.  
  • Face coverings are mandatory while indoors on campus, unless you are exempt.
  • Get tested with a free Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test if you think you may have COVID-19 and report your test results to UCL Connect to Protect.
  • Get tested at least twice a week with a free Lateral Flow Test (LFT), and before mixing with people in crowded indoor places, even if you don’t have COVID-19 symptoms. Please report your test results to the NHS and UCL Connect to Protect.


We understand that this is an incredibly uncertain time for everyone and the rising number of coronavirus cases will make people anxious.  


The health and wellbeing of everyone at UCL is our top priority. We will be keeping this under very close review over the Christmas and New Year break and we will let you know as soon as possible if anything changes.


Please check the UK Government and NHS websites, as well as UCL’s coronavirus information hub, for the latest information, especially if you are planning to travel over the winter break.


We hope you and your loved ones stay safe and well over the holidays.
