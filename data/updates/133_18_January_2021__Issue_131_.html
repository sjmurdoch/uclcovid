1. Principles for assessment support package in 2020-21

UCL is reviewing its package of support for student assessment in 2020-21. A panel of academic and administrative staff and Students’ Union UCL representatives has now agreed the principles on which the support package for 2020-21 will be based, including supporting all students individually, providing fair and equitable assessment, and protecting the value of UCL degrees. Find out more on UCL student news.


2. Changes to how you log-in to UCL systems

We are introducing some changes to our systems to make sure the data we hold are safe and secure for all staff and students. Find out more about how UCL is improving information security on the ISD website.


Changes to logging in: Multifactor authentication

Multifactor authentication, or MFA, is a feature that asks you for more than just your username and password when you log-in. It requires something you know (e.g. your UCL password) plus something you have (for example a security code) – it’s another layer of protection that stops others from accessing your account, even if they have your password.


Following a successful pilot, UCL is phasing in MFA for all staff. You will receive a direct email when it is time for you to enrol. The new system is designed to be easy to use, and it will generally only be activated when the system detects unusual use e.g. if you’re logging on from a new device or location.


reCAPTCHA user verification

The Myaccount service is subject to hundreds of thousands of attempts every week to guess passwords and infiltrate UCL systems. To significantly lower the chance of this succeeding, we are introducing the reCAPTCHA verification system on the Myaccount password service. You may have seen this elsewhere on the internet if you’ve ever been asked to “prove that you’re not a robot” by selecting the correct images.


3. Travelling to work for staff required on campus

Due to the current restrictions, anyone who can work remotely must do so. If you are required to work on campus, you can find advice on UCL’s coronavirus information hub about alternative ways of getting to work, including walking or cycling, and travelling outside peak hours.


Guidance on quiet times to travel by public transport and when individual stations and stops are busiest can also be found on the Transport for London website.


4. COVID-19 testing for staff on campus

All UCL and all contracted staff within Estates and other services who are working on campus, and who do not have COVID-19 symptoms, are expected to get tested twice a week at UCL’s testing centre in Bidborough House. The centre is part of the national testing programme for universities and uses rapid Lateral Flow Tests (LFTs), which typically provide results within two hours. Testing is free and voluntary.


UCL staff can use their UCL UserID to book a test. Contracted staff who can access the LFT testing centre at Bidborough House should contact their line manager for more information.


Contracted staff who are working at UCL but unable to attend the Bidborough House LFT testing centre during opening hours (weekdays 08.30am–3.00pm) can collect a PCR home test from the North Lodge. If this applies to you, please speak to your line manager.


If you have coronavirus symptoms, please follow these steps to request a test. You must not come to campus or the testing centre at Bidborough House.


5. New overseas travel guidance

Staff and students should not travel unless it is for a legally permitted reason. Please note that from today the Government has announced there are no longer any travel corridors.


If you do need to travel on essential UCL business, you must secure approval from your faculty, complete a valid risk assessment and register the trip with TravelCert before you book. All bookings must be made via Key Travel, and where possible flexible tickets should be purchased. Please use the pre-travel checklist on the Key Travel booking tool to help you.


When you return to England you must provide evidence of a negative COVID-19 test result taken up to three days before departure and must self-isolate on arrival.


For the latest information on quarantining and HR guidance, please view UCL’s information for staff travelling abroad.


6. COVID-19 Career Support Scheme: Funding available

This scheme supports staff, whose careers are particularly at risk of being affected negatively by the pandemic due to equity and inclusion-related circumstances, to apply for support or funding to help mitigate this impact. It is open to everyone – academic, research and professional services roles.


Applications for round 4 of the scheme are open and will close at 5pm on 21 January 2021. In light of the current lockdown, two further rounds have been added, and the final deadline for applications is 18 February 2021 (subject to funding) – find out more on the equality, diversity and inclusion website.
