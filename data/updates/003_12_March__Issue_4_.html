1. Online teaching and alternative assessments

We know staff and students are worried about the impact coronavirus will have on teaching and assessments. The university currently remains open on the advice of Public Health England (PHE) and we recommend that students, staff and visitors attend their scheduled activities as normal. However, this advice may change as the situation evolves and we are putting measures in place to ensure the safety and wellbeing of our students, staff and visitors. Understandably students are concerned about whether and how they will be examined.


This week, Education Committee agreed proposals for alternative modes of assessment, to remove the requirement for first year examinations and to make arrangements for online teaching in term 3. These proposals will be recommended to a special meeting of Academic Committee on Friday 13 March and we will be able to share information in detail tomorrow once these proposals have been confirmed.


2. Study Abroad/Affiliate/Double and Dual Degree students

Following yesterdayâ€™s announcement that staff and students should not travel internationally on UCL business until 1 May 2020, the university is formally recalling students who are taking part in Study Abroad, Double or Dual degree, or international Affiliate programmes.


Students in affected areas such as Italy have already been contacted and are returning to the UK or their home country. The Study Abroad team are in regular contact with each student and today will contact them and ask them to return. If any of them are not UK residents, they will be given the option to return to UCL or to their home country. We will resolve any implications about progression and awards in due course.


The Study Abroad/Affiliate/Double and Dual Degree team are also already in contact with students who are about to go overseas and they will advise them not to go.


Any students with questions or comments should contact our student enquiry system askUCL.


3. Notifying UCL if you are unwell

If you suspect that you have been exposed to coronavirus or anybody you live with is undergoing testing, please notify UCL Workplace Health via the online form. You should also tell your line manager.


We are asking students to tell their departments and askUCL if they think they may have the virus.


4. UCL student accommodation

We would also like to confirm that all UCL-managed halls will remain open as usual throughout the year, even if UCL is closed.


In addition, UCL will do everything it can to support its students returning early from study abroad and seeking UCL accommodation. They should contact the UCL Accommodation office by emailing accommodation@ucl.ac.uk


5. Ridgmount Practice (UCL's partner GP surgery)

On the advice of Public Health England, the walk-in service at Ridgmount Practice (UCL's partner GP surgery) is suspended until further notice. Patients are advised not to enter the building without a booked appointment.


Any students who are registered at the practice and who have concerns about their health should call +44 (0)20 7387 6306 to book an initial telephone triage appointment. To find out more, please visit the health care section of the Student Support and Wellbeing website or the Ridgmount Practice website.


Anyone who has concerns about their health can also contact the free NHS 111 telephone helpline (or find specific advice on the NHS website if you are unable to get through).

If you are worried about the coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager in the first instance. You can also access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.
