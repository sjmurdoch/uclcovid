1. Campus opening guide – updated

The UCL Estates team has updated the Campus Opening Guide with more detail about how Heads of Departments and Directors of Operations can work with Estates to reopen buildings.


Slides 11 and 12 now include a new process chart that clearly sets out the four stages of the reopening process; planning, building preparation, approval to open, and confirmation. Slide 13 contains an example of the steps in action.


Each department must nominate a dedicated person to liaise with the Estates Team throughout the building reopening process. Heads of Department and Directors of Operations should get in touch with your Area Manager who can help you reopen your department as safely as possible.

           

2. Starting or resuming fieldwork

Further support and advice is available for staff and PhD students on starting or resuming fieldwork away from campus. A framework has been developed which informs the planning, approval and management of fieldwork and works alongside the existing risk assessments.


The framework sets out guidance on the pre-conditions that should be in place before fieldwork starts and a checklist of key points to consider before seeking approval.


A dedicated group will oversee the framework to ensure that it stays up-to-date and relevant in the current fluid situation. Those involved in fieldwork are advised to check it regularly for the latest recommendations.


If you have any questions about starting or resuming fieldwork, please speak to your manager or supervisor. Please also remember to consider your personal risk and safety requirements, and ensure you are prepared for any local measures that may be in place at your destination by checking the FCO guidance.


3. Today's Teams Tip

This is a very useful feature to get someone’s attention, but is one you should definitely use sparingly: If you need to ensure someone sees your message in Teams, you can send an urgent message.


Top Tip: From inside any chat, click on the exclamation icon to select Urgent from the message status options. This will notify them of the new message every two minutes for the next 20 minutes.


MS Stream Service update

Last month, we covered how to transcribe a Teams Meeting using MS Stream. This week we wanted to flag a temporary change to the process so that users aren’t caught out.


Please note: from tomorrow (20 August) Teams Meeting recordings will need to be manually uploaded into Stream – they won’t appear there automatically.  This change will only affect meetings recorded from 20 August onwards, and will only affect us at UCL until a change in our licence agreement at some point in September. More details and a reminder of how to upload a recording can be found on the Teams SharePoint site.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #teamstips. For further information about using Teams, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, please contact the IT Services Desk.
