1. New advice for working from home

We are continuing to work closely with Public Health England and the NHS to ensure the safety of our students and staff.


Although campus remains open, all staff who are able to work remotely should do so from tomorrow, Tuesday 17 March. Please speak to your line manager to facilitate this wherever possible. If the nature of your role means you cannot work from home, campus remains fully open and in operation.


All staff aged over 70 and those with pre-existing health conditions must work from home. If you are not able to carry out your role remotely, you will be granted special leave. This guidance also applies to people who live with and / or care for older people and people with health conditions.


More information about self-isolating and working from home is available on the main coronavirus webpage. Please keep an eye on the ISD website for latest advice about working from home. We will be regularly updating the remote working page with further guidance and support materials.


In these unusual times, we also need as many members of staff as possible to complete UCLâ€™s fire safety training. Our Fire Safety eLearning package is available via UCL MyHR. 2. Cancellation of external events, closure of the Bloomsbury Theatre and all UCL galleries and museums

As of today, Monday 16 March, all external events are suspended until 1 May. This includes:


  • Prospective student offer holder events
  • Events in the Bloomsbury Theatre, all UCL galleries and museums
  • Staff recruitment (unless it can take place remotely via Microsoft Teams)


The central UCL room bookings system will not accept any further external bookings for this period.


3. UCL Day Nursery

The UCL Day Nursery remains open for children of students and staff for the time being. This decision will remain under review.


4. What to do if you feel unwell

If you feel unwell and think that you may have the virus, please self-isolate for seven days in line with the current Government advice. Please note that this has changed from previous official guidance.


You do not need to contact 111 to tell them you're staying at home. Do not go to a GP surgery, pharmacy or hospital. You only need to use the NHS 111 online coronavirus service if:


  • You feel you cannot cope with your symptoms at home
  • Your condition gets worse
  • Your symptoms do not get better after seven days


The NHS has comprehensive advice on their website about what to do if you think you may have the virus, precautionary measures and self-isolation procedures. Only call 111 if you cannot get help online. Please continue to check the Public Health England website for the latest official advice. You can also contact the Department for Health helpline on 0800 046 8687 (8am-6pm, Monday-Friday). Further advice on self-isolation is also available from Public Health England.


Please see the section below if it is confirmed that you have coronavirus.


5. What to do if you or someone in your team thinks they may have coronavirus

If you have symptoms of coronavirus or you have been in contact with someone who has symptoms, you must notify your line manager.


If one of your students or a member of your team tells you they have the virus (or you are notified by someone else)  please check the main coronavirus webpage for instructions about what you need to do next.


6. Assessments in term 3

Please note that exams due to take place in term 3 will still go ahead. In the majority of cases, Faculties and Departments should replace these exams with alternative assessments that students will be able to complete online. This applies to all forms of assessment for which students would normally need to be physically on campus. Any assessments which can be submitted online, such as coursework, will go ahead as normal with the same deadlines as currently advertised.


We are strongly encouraging students to continue prepare for their assessments by studying and revising as normal. Please see the advice we have issued to students on the UCL website.
