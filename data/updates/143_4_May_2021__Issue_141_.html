1. Plans to welcome all staff back to campus

Based on the Government’s roadmap, the advice of our own public health experts and the feedback we’ve received from staff, UCL has developed our own roadmap to welcome back the remaining staff and students who have been working and studying remotely.


It is difficult to quantify the immense value of being together in the same place, and what this means for our community and the experience we can offer our students. The face-to-face conversations and chance interactions that can only happen when we’re on campus are so important, not only for building connections, but also for sparking the ideas behind our world-changing discoveries. At the same time, we are keen to take what we’ve learned from working remotely over the past year and use this to improve how we work in the future.


In line with Government guidance, all remaining staff will be able to start returning to onsite working from 21 June, ahead of the start of the 2021/22 academic year. This will be a big step for many of us after working remotely for so long, so please talk to your line manager first.


The Estates team and departments are working hard to open up campus in time for your return and you can find out what’s already open on the UCL website. Assuming Government guidance does not change, the following steps will allow the university to be fully open and operational, with appropriate COVID-19 safety measures and risk assessments in place.


1.   From Monday 17 May all remaining students who have been studying remotely can return to campus (including paused research projects), and face-to-face teaching and supervision can restart. For staff coming in to undertake that work, we will continue to offer practical support such as parking (where available), FFP2 facemasks, and access to UCL’s symptom-free COVID-19 testing and Connect to Protect services.


2.   From Monday 21 June the university campus will be fully open and operational. Staff who have been working remotely may return to work, but we do not expect everyone back at this stage. You may be asked to work in a different UCL space, so please speak to your line manager beforehand.


3.   From September subject to Government guidance, we anticipate the majority of staff will return to work on campus for at least part of the week. This will be important for us to regain our sense of community, while also offering staff flexible working options where this is possible. Further guidance will be released in the next few weeks.


The situation is still evolving, and we will share more information about returning to campus and flexible working later this month. We will also update all students this week about what they can expect from UCL as the Government continues to lift lockdown restrictions.


As more staff and students return to campus, everyone must continue to follow the most up to date COVID-19 safety measures, which currently include washing your hands frequently, covering your face and maintaining 2m social distancing. For the latest information about what UCL is doing and how you can make a difference, please visit our Keeping Safe on Campus webpages.


2. COV-COMPARE clinical trial

UCLH is taking part in a Phase 3 clinical research study called COV-COMPARE, which compares the immune responses of different COVID-19 vaccines. Volunteers who participate will receive either a vaccine candidate or a licensed vaccine against COVID-19.


The trial is looking for volunteers who are 18 years of age or older, who have not yet received a COVID-19 vaccine. If you would like to take part, find out more on the study’s website.


3. Your Wellbeing: Complete this week’s survey

Please complete Week 43 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.


During March, April and May, there will be three prize draws with a prize of £50 in John Lewis vouchers, with winners picked from staff who have completed the Your Wellbeing survey every week – find out more about the prize draw on the UCL HR website.


4. Coronavirus: The Whole Story – What’s happening in Westminster?

It has been over one year since we launched our first episode of Coronavirus: The Whole Story – when we had no idea what challenges lay ahead of us. In this episode, we speak to Professor Jeff King (UCL Laws), Professor Meg Russell (UCL Constitution Unit) and Dr Melanie Smallman (UCL STS) to hear about how much has changed in that time, looking specifically at government and the law.


What powers have been brought in to help enforce lockdown and tackle the virus? Have they been given the correct amount of scrutiny? And how do they compare to coronavirus laws created by other countries? Tune in to find out on UCL Minds.
