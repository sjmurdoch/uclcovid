Friday 24 July 2020

Daily coronavirus (Covid-19)
update: issue 94


Dear colleagues,


As term comes to a close, and with it the end of my last full academic year at UCL, I feel incredibly proud of everything this community has accomplished in what has been a truly extraordinary year.


Of course COVID-19 has transformed our home and our professional lives, and it’s been hugely inspiring to see how colleagues have adapted to this new way of working. It has taken an enormous collective effort to reimagine how we can teach, research and collaborate in this new context so that we can continue to operate effectively despite the lockdown and social distancing restrictions.


I know that a huge amount of work is going on behind the scenes to prepare for next term, and many of you are pausing research or other work to prioritise teaching.


I would like to thank each of you for your hard work to bring teaching online. Through your ingenuity and the expertise of colleagues in the Institute of Education, in the Arena Centre and the Office of the Vice-Provost (Education & Student Affairs) and in Student and Registry Services, we have already taken huge strides towards creating a truly excellent learning offer in spite of the lockdown restrictions: from engaging international students in different time zones around the globe, to learning how to communicate and share knowledge online, to sharing practical and clinical skills, including the medical students who have been learning how to carry out injections and suturing at home using oranges and bananas.


In more ordinary times, we might have had more time to reflect on the results of the National Student Survey published last week; even in this period of completely unprecedented upheaval, the results continue to demonstrate improvement on key issues such as assessment and feedback (up 1%), Academic Support (up 1.5%) and Learning Resources (up 2.1%). It is heartening to see that the trajectory we have been on for the past few years has not been derailed by these challenges, and this is testament to the hard work that has been ongoing across the university to shift the dial in the interest of our students.


I also wanted to pass on my congratulations to the winners and nominees for the One UCL Awards, which celebrate individuals and teams and their work across our academic departments and Professional Services. Seeing how colleagues have come together to support UCL, and each other, this year was a joy and reminded me just how exceptional our community is.


This is a time of strange juxtapositions, and although our financial future is far from certain, and won’t be clear until after enrolment in the autumn, we nevertheless have a huge success story to celebrate. Nearly 10 years ago we started to lay the foundations for what would become the It’s All Academic Campaign, committing in 2016 to what were – at the time – two of the most ambitious fundraising targets in Europe outside of Oxbridge. I am delighted to share the news with you that we have now met the Campaign’s targets of raising £600 million in support of UCL’s staff, students and research. This joins the news in May that we broke through our 250,000 volunteering hour target, with alumni and advocates from around the world sharing their time, knowledge and networks for the benefit of UCL.


Achieving this, ahead of schedule and during a time when donors are thinking more carefully than ever about how they make philanthropic investments, is a huge accomplishment. Fundamental to this success has been the continued commitment and support of UCL’s academic and professional services staff. These goals would not have been achieved without your advocacy of UCL and involvement in all that we’ve done to engage donors and alumni. OVPA are working on plans for celebrating and publicly closing the Campaign later this year, which they will update you on in due course. As many of you know, the vision for this Campaign was set out under the leadership of Lori Houlihan, who retired as Vice-Provost (Advancement) earlier this year. Lori saw very clearly the potential to transform Advancement at UCL and to transform UCL through advancement, and I know that the OVPA team will continue to raise the bar as they now look ahead to the next Campaign.


This support will help us finance our ambitions, enrich our students’ experience and ensure we can deliver our academic mission for years to come. A key part of this work will be delivered through our programmes at UCL East, where construction has been progressing well, and the first four floors are already taking shape in some areas of the Marshgate building. Throughout the pandemic we have remained committed to being a good neighbour to communities in east London, especially in Newham, which is sadly one of the boroughs most affected by COVID-19. To date we have offered a number of small grants to projects co-developed with local groups to tackle issues arising from the pandemic; supported artists and researchers to engage with people in the Olympic boroughs; and we’re currently working with east London schools to help develop their teaching through projects and workshops inspired by UCL research.


Another moment of note this year came with the denaming of the Galton Lecture Theatre, the Pearson Lecture Theatre and the Pearson Building last month, following the recommendations of the Inquiry into the History of Eugenics at UCL. This is an important historical moment for UCL and marks our first steps in addressing the institution’s links with the eugenics movement. I am very grateful to everyone who played a part in bringing about this change, including the members of the Inquiry, the Buildings Naming and Renaming Committee and the many students who have been pressing for these names to be removed for some time. There has been an extraordinary response from both staff and students so far, and it’s clear that this is an issue that our community feels passionately about. The group looking at the recommendations from the Inquiry met for the first time this week, and will produce an implementation plan by December. I am also pleased to be able to confirm that I am setting up a group to consider how we can take forward the recommendations that came out of the Town Hall on Race that was organised by staff and students following the Black Lives Matter protests. I want us to build on the momentum that the Town Hall generated and I have asked the working group to report to me with a costed implementation plan before the end of the year, ensuring that this aligns closely with our existing Race Equality Charter Action Plan.


I could never have expected my last year with you all would bring anything like the challenges we have faced over the last four months. I knew that the UCL community was ingenious and adaptable, but it is true that we are being tested to the limits. It’s also true that we have a tremendous capacity to weather these changes and to learn from the challenges we’re facing. But it takes its toll on all of us. I know how busy many staff are, trying to get ready for an autumn term that will be radically different; I also know how challenging it is for others who are unable to put their focus and their ideas into action while campus is not fully accessible. For those who are working flat-out, I want to reassure you that the senior team is thinking very seriously about the steps we can take to reduce the pressure – by stopping non-urgent work and streamlining other processes. For those who are anxious to get back into the office or the lab as soon as possible, I can only reiterate that there is a team working hard on our return to campus plans, ensuring that we can return in a safe, planned way.


Those in leadership positions can respond best when we understand from you how you are coping and where your concerns lie. That is why we have set up a regular wellbeing survey and I am personally reviewing the results every week. I hope you can spare five minutes to take part and help us improve the support available for staff and PGR students.


This year has truly been ‘business unusual’. This was brought home to me so clearly when I went in to my office this week, to find papers on my desk dated 9 March. Some four and a half months have seemingly passed in what seems like a blink of the eye. In my wildest of dreams, I could not even have imagined that happening. Where did that time go?


I cannot thank you all enough for the ways that you have pulled together and the dedication that you are showing. I hope that you will be able to make some time to rest and get some time away from Microsoft Teams calls and remote working to recharge and spend time with loved ones. I understand that this may seem difficult for some facing a particularly busy summer – but we have an important term ahead of us and it’s vital that we take some time to put our health and wellbeing first. I look forward to us returning in September ready and prepared to face the challenges that next year will bring. I have a feeling that ‘business unusual’ will continue and that time will fly by again.


With very best wishes,


Professor Michael Arthur
UCL President & Provost

3. Further advice and support

Daily coronavirus updates

These emails are sent every weekday to all UCL staff and postgraduate research students. They contain information about the pandemic and UCL’s response, including guidance about working remotely, policies and procedures, news about teaching and management arrangements and updates about UCL’s COVID-19 research.


If you would like to submit an item about the pandemic or UCL’s response to be included in the daily coronavirus update, please email internalcommunications@ucl.ac.uk.

                                                           

Your Wellbeing weekly survey

The UCL Your Wellbeing check-in survey is a short, weekly survey for staff and postgraduate research students. The data will be used to find out how our community is coping with the huge changes caused by the coronavirus crisis and inform future improvements. Complete week 5 of the survey now.


Coronavirus information hub and research webpages

The information hub at ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus is updated regularly with information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions about coronavirus as well as links to advice from other parts of UCL.


The COVID-19 research webpages showcase the work that UCL is carrying out on coronavirus, and contain information and resources for researchers.


Working remotely

The Remote, not Distant at UCL pages contain advice and resources to help staff adapt to remote working, including self-care and wellbeing, digital communication resources and guidance for managers.     


Keeping safe on campus

The Keeping Safe on Campus website provides live updates about our returning to campus pilots, as well as safety and social distancing guidelines for those on site.                                                                                                                                     

Health and Safety advice

The Working Safely During a Pandemic website from Safety Services provides guidance on how to manage the health and safety of staff at UCL during the pandemic. Topics include using display screen equipment, working with COVID-19 samples and risk assessments for returning to campus.


Your health and wellbeing

If you feel unwell and think that you may have coronavirus, please follow the latest Government advice. You can also find more information on the NHS website.

                                                                                                                    

If you are worried about coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager or check our list of resources to support staff during the outbreak.


You can access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.


Face coverings

You can find information on face coverings, including how to make your own, on the UCL Institute of Making website and the Public Health England website.   

                                                            

Questions?

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager in the first instance.


If you have a more general question, please check that it has not already been answered on the websites given above. If it hasn’t, members of UCL staff can submit a question about keeping safe on campus via our online form. Alternatively you can email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699. Lines are open 9am–5pm, Monday–Friday.
