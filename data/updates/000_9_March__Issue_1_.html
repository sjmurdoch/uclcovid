Monday 9 March 2020

Daily coronavirus (Covid-19) update


Dear colleagues,

We are writing to you with an update about coronavirus and its potential impact on UCL’s staff and students.

We know that many of you are feeling anxious about the virus and the speed at which the situation is changing. Please be reassured that UCL is continuing to work with Public Health England (PHE) and it is our policy to follow their advice, together with guidance from the NHS and the Foreign & Commonwealth Office. On the advice of PHE, the university remains open and we recommend that students, staff and visitors attend their scheduled activities as normal.

At this stage, we do not believe there is reason for our community to worry unduly about the virus. However, noting that the situation is evolving and to ensure that you are well-informed about UCL’s position and the mitigating steps we are taking, we have decided to issue a daily update for the time being. These emails will include background information and official advice; details about UCL’s plans to identify and address any risks; and any new guidance that you will need to follow.


UCL updates for staff and students

  • On 7 March UCL issued new travel advice for students who are thinking about returning home during their studies.
  • A series of frequently asked questions about events have been answered on the main UCL website.
  • Each Head of Department has been asked to identify two coronavirus mitigation coordinators (one academic, one administrator). Please submit any questions you may have about this activity via our online form.
  • Please make sure you are able to work remotely, should the need arise. If you have a work laptop and charger take them home with you each night. Alternatively, you may also choose to use your own personal computer from home.


Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we will add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.

If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs. If you have a specific queries, we will get you a bespoke response, but please note that we are receiving a high volume of questions and it may not be immediate. If we receive the same or similar questions from a number of sources, we will update the FAQs accordingly.


Government advice and information

  • After the Government’s emergency Cobra meeting today, the UK remains in the coronavirus ‘containment’ phase.
  • As of 9am on 9 March 2020, 24,960 people have been tested in the UK, of which 24,641 were confirmed negative and 319 were confirmed as positive. Three patients who tested positive for COVID-19 have died. See the full government response on gov.uk.
  • Public Health England updated the list of affected areas and countries on 8 March to reflect the changing situation in Italy.


Potential impact on UCL

The working group of Deans, Heads of Department, members of the senior management team and Students’ Union UCL has been established to mitigate the short and longer term risks to the university across 15 strands of activity. These are:


  1. Student numbers
  2. Learning and teaching
  3. Pre-sessional teaching
  4. Assessments
  5. Enrolment
  6. Keeping UCL running
  7. Student Experience
  8. Graduations
  9. (a) Staff overseas and (b) Students overseas
  10. Communicating with departments
  11. Procurement
  12. Finances
  13. Visitors
  14. Events
  15. Research


If you have any questions about the working group or would like to propose potential risk areas for the group to consider, please submit your comments via our online form.

Support available for staff and students

If you have any concerns about your health you should contact the free NHS 111 telephone helpline (or find specific advice on the NHS website if you are unable to get through).


If you are worried about the coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager in the first instance. You can also access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact UCL's Student Support and Wellbeing team.

       

Best wishes,
