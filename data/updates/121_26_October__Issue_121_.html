1. Message to students about teaching arrangements for Terms 2 and 3

Today, all students have been emailed with an update on teaching arrangements, co-signed by Anthony Smith, Vice-Provost (Education & Student Affairs) and Officers of the Students’ Union, indicating it is likely that teaching in Terms Two and Three will be similar to Term One; all core teaching and assessment online, and a limited amount of educational activity on campus, with alternative activity for students not on campus.


You can read the message in full on the UCL Student News website.

             

2. Connect to Protect - update for line managers


We have updated our guidance about what to do if a student or someone you manage tells you they have tested positive for coronavirus. If this happens, you must notify your Head of Department, but please do not share this information with other staff or students as it remains confidential.

If an individual reports that they have tested positive through UCL Connect to Protect, our case managers will follow up the report and speak with them to identify anyone who has potentially been in close contact with them. This will be determined on a case-by-case basis.

If you have any questions about UCL Connect to Protect or you think you’ve been in close contact with someone who has tested positive, students can contact us though the askUCL student enquiries system. Members of staff can contact us via our online form or alternatively you can email covidhelpline@ucl.ac.uk. If you would like to speak to someone on the phone, please call our helpline on 0203 108 5699. Lines are open 9am-5pm, Monday-Friday.

                       

3. Your Wellbeing: Complete the survey for a chance to win £50

Please complete Week 19 of the ‘Your Wellbeing’ survey which is now live on the HR website – it takes less than five minutes and will help the Provost and the leadership team understand how staff are coping during the pandemic.

HR are currently offering three £50 Amazon vouchers to winners who will be picked from members of staff who complete four consecutive Your Wellbeing surveys – find out more on the HR website.

                       

4. Coronavirus: The Whole Story podcast – Could COVID-19 trump Trump?

With US election night looming, this week’s episode is all about the role that coronavirus is playing in voter decisions in America. In previous episodes, we’ve discussed the colossal impact that COVID-19 has had on the medical world, society and the economy. This week, we speak to experts from the UCL Institute of the Americas and the Centre on US Politics to explore the impact of the pandemic on the political landscape across the pond.
