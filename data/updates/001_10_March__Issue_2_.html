Tuesday 10 March 2020

Daily coronavirus (Covid-19) update


Dear colleagues,


We are writing to you with today’s update about coronavirus, with further guidance for UCL staff and students.


Thank you to all of you who took the time to read and respond to yesterday’s email by submitting your questions and comments via the online form. We are working to find the answers to your queries and we will use these to update the list of frequently asked questions as quickly as we can.


It’s important that we pull together as a community, and that we stay calm during this difficult time. The university remains open and we recommend that students, staff and visitors attend their scheduled activities as normal. However, we would like to remind you that UCL will not penalise any students who choose not to attend teaching events. Information for students who wish to return home during their studies is available on the UCL website.


UCL’s policy is to follow the advice of Public Health England (PHE), the NHS and the Foreign & Commonwealth Office (FCO). In addition to this guidance, UCL is advising against all international UCL trips for staff, undergraduate students, contractors and visitors, in-bound and out-bound. Further advice for planned postgraduate research fieldwork will be confirmed as soon as this is available.


We know that many of you may also have plans to travel for personal reasons, especially over the Easter break. The situation is changing rapidly so please check the FCO website for travel advice for the specific country or area you are planning to visit. Remember that circumstances may change during your trip, and you may be subject to travel restrictions or required to self-isolate on your return to the UK. Please consider if your trip is essential and read the frequently asked questions for information about what to do if your plans are affected.


Some of you have reported that you are concerned about the evidence that people with pre-existing health conditions and older people are at greater risk of developing severe symptoms should they contract the virus. If you fall into one of these groups, you should speak to your line manager or department in the first instance. If you can work remotely, please do so. This also applies to people who live with and / or care for older people and people with health conditions.

  

UCL updates for staff and students

       

  • Answers to frequently asked questions about international travel, self-isolating and temporary staff have been added to the UCL website.
  • Please register your mobile number now via MyHR so we can contact you via SMS in case of an emergency. On the menu, go to Employee Self Service then select Personal Information and Personal Information again. Students should register their mobile numbers via Portico.
  • If you suspect you may have coronavirus, please tell your line manager. If you are notified that a member of your team may be affected, you must contact UCL Workplace Health.
  • Some of you have asked what information is being shared with students. We are directing them to the main coronavirus webpage via the UCL homepage, Moodle pages and the current students website. We are also including regular updates in the weekly MyUCL e-newsletters. Over 200 students have already made contact with askUCL.
  • We are developing a version of the frequently asked questions in Mandarin and expect to publish these tomorrow. We will be able to share more information in the next daily email.


Please keep checking ucl.ac.uk/coronavirus regularly for the most up-to-date information as it becomes available. This is UCL’s main webpage where we will add the latest advice and answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about coronavirus.


If you have a specific question about your individual circumstances please speak to your line manager. If you have a more general question that hasn’t been answered online, please submit a question via our online form. We will review them and answer queries via updates to the FAQs.


Potential impact on UCL


The working group of Deans, Heads of Department, members of the senior management team and Students’ Union UCL is meeting daily and the Education Committee is meeting weekly to mitigate the short and longer term impact of the virus on UCL. They are continuing to develop their work across 15 strands of activity:


  1. Student numbers: understanding challenges/opportunities related to our student number planning and student diversification.
  2. Learning and teaching: how do we continue to teach our students as the outbreak develops (including online opportunities)?
  3. Pre-sessional teaching: how do we support students who do not conform to an October/June year?
  4. Assessment: how do we assess our students if we can’t follow our normal approach?
  5. Enrolment: how do we enrol our students if they can’t enter the UK in line with our dates/at all
  6. Keeping UCL running: what are the implications of having to shut UCL, major services such as public transport being suspended or people self-isolating?
  7. Student Experience: how do we continue to offer a rich experience to our students?
  8. Graduations: understanding the impact on our Graduation ceremonies.
  9. (a) Staff overseas: how do we support them? (b) Students overseas: how do we support them?
  10. Communicating with Departments: how does advice and guidance reach our colleagues in departments and how do we receive feedback?
  11. Procurement: how do we continue to procure things, and what is the risk of not being able to do so?
  12. Finances: what will be the impact on our financial performance?
  13. Visitors: what risks do external visitors pose to UCL?
  14. Events: what is the risk of hosting large-scale events (1000+ people)?
  15. Research: how will our research activity be affected in the short and long term?

    

If you have any questions about the working group or would like to propose potential risk areas for the group to consider, please submit your comments via our online form.

       

Support available for staff and students


If you have any concerns about your health you should contact the free NHS 111 telephone helpline (or find specific advice on the NHS website if you are unable to get through).


If you are worried about the coronavirus or your wellbeing more generally, please speak to your line manager in the first instance. You can also access free counselling services via the Employee Assistance Programme, Care First, which is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Please telephone 0800 197 4510 to speak to an independent professional counsellor or information specialist in confidence.


Please advise any students who are worried to contact askUCL.
