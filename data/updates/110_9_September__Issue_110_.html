Wednesday 9 September 2020

Coronavirus update: issue 110


Dear colleagues,


With announcements over the weekend about our government’s approach to Brexit and the ongoing impact of the pandemic on our lives, with the daily national number of new cases clearly on the rise again, it remains a time of great uncertainty. Thank you for your hard work and stalwart efforts both to ensure that UCL has continued to run over the summer and for the extensive preparations that you are making for the start of term. Your contribution is vital to what we do.


Whilst the signs are encouraging it is still not known what our eventual financial position will be. Although we are anticipating that a high number of students are likely to enrol with us this autumn, the situation remains uncertain – particularly international PGT – and it is clear that the costs of running the university safely in the COVID world are high. I want to reassure you that since the start of the pandemic, we have done everything we can to look after our staff and protect jobs. It is our community that makes UCL so special, and the health and wellbeing of our staff and students remain our highest priority.


There are two issues that I want to bring to your attention: the result of the Universities and Colleges Employers Association (UCEA) national pay negotiations, and the Universities Superannuation Scheme (USS) consultation on pensions. Both are agreed at a national level, but UCL is working hard to reduce any impact on you, our staff.


UCL, like most universities in the UK, takes part in national pay negotiations where it was agreed there would be no pay uplift for 2020–21, due to the financial impact of COVID-19 on the sector nationally. This is disappointing and so we are using the measures we have control of locally to mitigate the effects of this decision here at UCL. UCL has decided to increase London weighting by 2% and continue to award the automatic incremental pay progression for staff on grades 1–9. As the year progresses and we have a clear view of our overall financial position, we will also revisit whether or not we can do more, particularly for those at the lower end of the pay scales.


In addition, I want to make sure that members of USS are aware of the new consultation for the 2020 pensions valuation which opened on Monday. An easier-to-digest version of this highly technical document will be available next week and we will share this with you. Proposals include a significant increase in the contributions by both employees and employers, which at first look are unaffordable. It is really important that you are aware of the choices and can give us your thoughts. I am concerned about the intergenerational fairness of any decision that is made about the future direction of the scheme. In short, it is going to be very difficult to find the right balance between the best-possible retirement for staff with a scheme that is affordable for you whilst you are working, and also affordable for UCL.


I am keen to know your views so they can be included as part of UCL’s official response to the consultation. Over the coming weeks, UCL will be running virtual Town Hall events and sessions with Council and Academic Board, as well as inviting feedback online, to make sure we have heard the views across our community.


This is a lot to take in and comes at a time when many of you are busy preparing for the new term, but please be assured that UCL will always do its level best to support you.


With best wishes,
Professor Michael Arthur
President & Provost

1. Staff pay

As the Provost outlines above, due to the financial impact of COVID-19 on universities this year, there will be no national offer of a pay uplift for 2020–21. This pay award is determined nationally. Other aspects of pay can be determined locally and UCL is committed to recognising and rewarding our staff where our finances allow.


Staff on grades 1–9 will still receive the automatic incremental pay progression as normal. The amount varies by grade, but for over half of these colleagues this means they will receive an incremental rise equivalent to a pay award of around 3% of base salary. UCL is also increasing our London weighting by 2%. This will be processed in the September payroll but backdated to 1 August 2020. Increases related to the 2019/20 promotion round and other contribution-related pay increases below grade 10 (such as additional increments and honorarium payments) are also available.


For more information about the national pay negotiations and the increases UCL is making to staff pay this year, please visit the HR website.

                                                

2. Reporting and managing coronavirus cases

As you may have seen in the news, the Government has reported a rise in coronavirus cases and limited social gatherings to six people from Monday. UCL is monitoring the situation and considering our response.


We have updated the Keeping Safe on Campus webpages with step-by-step guidance on what to do if you feel unwell, or if you are a Head of Department and a student or member of staff tells you they may have coronavirus. This includes links to Government advice on self-isolating and getting a test, and information about notifying UCL and where you can find support. Remember, you must stay at home if:


  • you (or someone you live with) has a new continuous cough, high temperature or a loss of or change in your sense of taste or smell;
  • you have tested positive for the virus;
  • or you have been contacted by NHS Test and Trace.


This guidance will remain in place until mid-September, when we will launch a new reporting tool to monitor and respond to coronavirus cases at UCL. This system is being designed with UCL public health experts who have been advising the Government. If you would like to help test the tool before it’s launched, please contact digitalcommunications@ucl.ac.uk.


UCL is also currently planning for a number of scenarios including any potential coronavirus cases on campus or further lockdown restrictions. We will share more information with you next week.


3. Getting to work by walking, cycling and public transport

UCL has produced a new bank of resources to help you plan your journey to work on campus.


As well as reducing the spread of coronavirus, walking and cycling can be cheaper, healthier and better for the environment than other forms of transport. It can even be faster, too. If you'd like to walk all or part of the way to work, you can find walking maps of Bloomsbury and estimated journey times between London Overground and Underground stations on the Sustainable UCL website. We have also launched a bike buddy scheme for staff and students, and we're installing 600 new cycle racks on campus.


If you need to use public transport, please avoid peak travel times and consider using Tottenham Court Road or King's Cross Underground stations, which have larger concourse areas where it's easier to facilitate social distancing. For more information, please visit the Transport for London website or check your train operator's guidance.


4. Today's Teams Tip

One of the benefits of having meetings pop-out in a separate window is a different approach to meeting notes. Taking notes in context of the meeting may help you to bring more structure to the session, and record and assign actions.


Top Tip: Meeting notes are now taken directly on the Meeting notes tab in the main Teams window. To access or take notes from within the meeting window, click the ellipsis (…) then ‘Show meeting notes’ and you’ll see the usual side pane come up. In the pane, there is a ‘Take notes’ button that brings you to the Meeting notes tab.


If you have any tips for using Teams you want to share, please email isd-webcomms@ucl.ac.uk or post your tip on Twitter or Instagram with the hashtag #TeamsTips. For more information, please have a look at the Teams SharePoint site. If you are having problems using Teams, contact the IT Services Desk.


5. Remote, not Distant at UCL: Face coverings

As face coverings are required in more and more spaces, including public transport, shops and UCL’s campus buildings, we want to know what you’ve been wearing. Have you been especially creative? Or has your team adopted themed face coverings? We’d love to see your pictures of you or your team wearing your face coverings on our virtual photo wall. You can find a list of places where face coverings are required and who is exempt on the UK Government website. UCL’s Institute of Making has also created a guide for how to make your own.
